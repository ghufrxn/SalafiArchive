However, Abu Hamid al-Isfaraa'eenee (d. 406H), an Imaam of the Sunnah, would openly warn against al-Baqillani so much so that al-Baqillani would have to hide if he wanted to go out to the lavatory out of intense fear because of the severity with which Abu Hamid warned against him and his bid'ah. Abu Haamid roused the people against al-Baqillani because his saying was that the Qur'an that we have, recited, heard, memorized is created. For that reason, the warnings of Abu Haamid have been recorded in history and the fateful ealier remark of al-Darqutni (he died in 385H) has also been recorded in history, but we treat that to be from the qadar of Allaah by which Allaah puts His servants to trial to enable their testing and tribulation, to see who adheres to His wahy (revelation) and who chooses to follow his desires and the opinions of men. The point here is that if these innovated usool of al-Ma'ribee and Ar'oor and al-Banna (and now Ali Hasan al-Halabi) aimed at defending the innovators (and those from Ahl al-Sunnah who become innovators) were correct, then by Allaah, if these usool had been implemented by the Imaams of the Salaf (and free and innocent they are of having such baatil presumed about them) then falsehood would have been victorious over truth and the likes of us and you would not have any Salafi aqidah to hold on to! Yet Allaah never allowed such baatil principles to appear and gain prominence in that time and nor will Allaah allow them victory in this time, since, as the Messenger (sallallaahu alayhi wasallam) said, "There will never cease to be a faction in my Ummah victorious upon the truth, they will not be harmed by those who abandon them or those who oppose them until the command of Allaah arrives," for there will always be Scholars and Imaams of the Sunnah holding these innovators and their false principles and foundations at bay! So the intent here is that just as anyone who clung to the saying of al-Darqutni whilst knowing what had been said by Abu Hamid al-Isfaraa'eenee regarding al-Baqillani would be considered a follower of desires, then likewise when someone clings to the saying of Shaykh Abdul-Muhsin al-'Abbaad whilst knowing what has been established of concrete evidences against this Ikhwani Dajjaal Mubtadi', Abu al-Hasan al-Ma'ribee, by Shaykh Rabee', he is a follower of desires. That the reality has not become clear to certain other scholars is not a proof for him at all.

These are the very foundations and principles (relating to disparagement) which these Ikhwani innovators have been waging a war against as part of their aim to defend their figureheads, to dilute and dissolve the Salafi Manhaj and cause wastage to its adherents so as to give a free reign and open field to themselves and their da'wah. So when there appears differing between Scholars then there is a methodology that is to be followed and principles that are to be implemented to allow the evidence and truth to be manifest - and there is no excuse for anyone to use the mere differing in and of itself to justify his non-acceptance of the actual evidence that lies with one particular scholar exclusive to another.
