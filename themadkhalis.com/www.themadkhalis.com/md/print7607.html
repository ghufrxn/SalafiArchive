And images of these hand-written notes are included at the end of the book. We will be quoting from this book inshaa'Allaah in this series. Shaykh Muhammad Amaan al-Jaamee: There is No Dispute About Sayyid Qutb Being an Ash'ari

Shaykh Muhammad Ammaan al-Jaamee stated about Sayyid Qutb:

The book 'az-Zilaal' of Sayyid Qutb is a tafseer that is not a tafseer based upon narrations, nor a tafseer from the language. Rather it is a composition containing a confused mixture of the ideas of the Ash'arees, the ideas of 'Wahdatul - Wujood' and the ideas of the Soofees - and he was an Ash'aree - about which there is no dispute.

So I advise small students not to read the book 'az-Zilaal', just as I advise the greater students - from the students of knowledge - to read it in order to make clear to lesser students the futility contained in it - as advice to them.

Source: A lecture: Answers to questions about the manhaj of Ahl us-Sunnah wal-Jamaa'ah in calling to Allah. (Cassette No. 1. Jeddah 4/1413H]

From the affairs Sayyid Qutb spoke of:

  • The Qur'an is created, the saying of the Jahmiyyah, Mu'tazilah and Ash'ariyyah
  • Rejection of aahaad hadeeth in matters of aqidah, the saying of the Mu'tazilah, Ash'ariyyah
  • Ta'weel of al-Istiwaa with the ta'weels of the Jahmiyyah, Mu'tazilah
  • Expressions of Wahdat ul-Wujood (unity of existence) of the Soofees (represented in the Ash'aris)
  • Negating what he deems to be "hawaadith" (events, occurrences) which is the standard doctrine of the Ash'ariyyah in negating the Sifaat Fi'liyyah, such as al-Istiwaa.
