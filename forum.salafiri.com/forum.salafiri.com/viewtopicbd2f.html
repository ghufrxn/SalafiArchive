A Woman Owns Gold Which Reaches The Minimum Required Amount For Zakaah - ‘Allaamah Muqbil ibn Haadee al-Waadi’ee

Posted: Mon Jul 10, 2017 11:01 am
by Shehzad Sattar
‘Allaamah Muqbil ibn Haadee al-Waadi’ee was asked:

A woman owns gold which reaches the minimum required amount for zakaah; is it permissible for her to pay her zakaah to her (Muslim) sisters or to her husband, bearing in mind that she does not own anything except this gold?

Response:

Yes, it is permissible for her to pay her zakaah to her sisters if they are poor. Poorness is neediness; meaning that one does not have that which suffices him. It is also permissible for her to pay her zakaah to her husband if he is poor; as the Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him) said to Zaynab, the wife of ‘Abd-Allaah ibn Mas’ood, when she said:

“Is it permissible for me to give zakaah of my gold to him (my husband).”

He said:

“Yes.”
