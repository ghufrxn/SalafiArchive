Reciting The Qur`an In A Loud Tone Inside The Masjid

Posted: Wed Jan 04, 2017 11:09 pm
by Shehzad Sattar
Question: He was asked regarding reciting the Qur`an in a loud tone inside the Masjid?

Response: He replied: that such an act is considered Makruh [reprehensible] if there is somebody inside [present in] the Masjid, praying Salaah, because that person will get distracted by the loud recitation.

Reference: Fataawaa Ibn Abee Zayd al-Qayruwaanee, Fataawaa regarding the Salaah – Fatwa No.66 Page 120

Footnotes

[1] Makruh: Literally meaning abominable or reprehensible. According to the Scholars of Islamic Jurisprudence, it can be defined in the following words: Refraining from doing an act out of obedience to Allaah [based upon proof and evidence], which would subsequently necessitate a reward. However, the perpetrator is not eligible for punishment if the act is committed. For example, drinking whilst standing. [TN]

[2] He is Abu Muhammad Abdullaah ibn Abee Zayd Abdur–Rahmaan Annafzee al-Qayruwaanee, nicknamed Maalik-Assageer [Little Maalik] for his in-depth knowledge of Imaam Maalik Ibn Anas`s opinions on the subsidiary issues of the Sharee`ah [Fiqh]. He was born in the year 310 AH corresponding to 922 CE in the north of Tunisia; he studied with the major scholars of his time and the number of his teachers exceeded to over 50. He authored over 38 books. One of his famous books is Muqaddamatul-Qayruwaanee, which is an explanation of the Salafee Aqeedah. This has been explained in detail by Allaamah Abdul-Muhsin al-Abbaad, the Muhaddith of Madeenah in the Prophet`s Masjid, may Allaah`s praise and peace be with him. Ibn Abee Zayd al-Qayruwaanee died in the year 386 A.H. on Monday evening in the month of Sha`baan at the age of 76. [TN]
