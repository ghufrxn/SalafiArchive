Usamah Hasan, Darwinism, Evolutionary Forces and the Creation of Man - Part 4: Scholars Verdicts on Belief in Darwinism
Filed under: Darwinism and Atheism
Thursday, January 20 2011 - by Abu.Iyaad
Key topics: Darwinism • Evolution

Mail to a Friend • Printer friendly

In what has preceded in Part 1, Part 2 and Part 3, it has been established that Usamah Hasan is trying to mix between two things that simply cannot mix, Darwinism with the Qur'an, and the evidence for that has been presented in those articles. We would like to finish here with something from the Sunnah, a hadeeth through which an important point needs to be made, and then a couple of verdicts of the orthodox Muslim scholars on this matter.

The Hadith in Sahih al-Bukhari

This is occurs in the Book of the Prophets, in the chapter Allaah's Creation of Aadam and his Offspring:

خلق الله آدم وطوله ستون ذراعا ثم قال اذهب فسلم على أولئك من الملائكة فاستمع ما يحيونك تحيتك وتحية ذريتك فقال السلام عليكم فقالوا السلام عليك ورحمة الله فزادوه ورحمة الله فكل من يدخل الجنة على صورة آدم فلم يزل الخلق ينقص حتى الآن

Allaah created Aadam with sixty-cubits [around 30 metres] in length. Then He said go and give salutations to those angels then listen to how they greet you [that being] the greeting of you and your offspring. So he said, "As salaamu alaykum" and they said, "As salaamu alaykum wa rahmatullaah" and so they added "wa rahmatullaah." And everyone who enters Paradise will be upon the form of Aadam (in size), and the creation has never ceased to decrease [in size] till now.

From this hadeeth:

Allaah created Aadam from a collection of the dusts of the earth, and made him sixty-cubits in length. From his offspring (through the kawnee asbaab which are the male and female fluids and a term in the womb) are people of different complexions, and they have never ceased decreasing in size till this ummah and the affair has remained established upon that. And this is simply an indication that Allaah placed inherent capacities and propensities within mankind through which the variation, richness and changes [in traits, features and qualities] take place, and which are determined by what is passed on from parent to child. And the same is said of the plants and animals and all living things. They were created in complete functional forms, giving their form of creation and then guided as to their mode, progeny and so on, and Allaah has written all things in the Preserved Tablet. So we affirm Allaah's qadar, qadaa, taqdeer, khalq and the asbaab that Allaah has placed in His creation, and we believe in all of that together. And it is a well known established principle with Ahl al-Sunnah that sound, uncorrupted reason (aql sareeh) does not conflict with an authentic text (naql saheeh), and where it does, then the reason (aql) needs to be re-evaluated. So the revealed texts are true and they explain the truth and what they contain concords with and is in perfect agreement with reality, as it is all from the knowledge of Allaah, the Exalted. As for mankind, then they have been given little knowledge, and so they attempt to find answers, with whatever tools they have at their disposal, and even that does not allow them to know except very little. And in history, the greatest of tyrants, arrogants and their likes who opposed the Prophets and Messengers, had whatever sciences and knowledge through which they tried to reject what is firmly rooted in their fitrah. And today things are no different, except that we have a much higher level of sophistication and subtlety which sits on top of the underlying kibr (arrogance), takhdeeb (denial), juhood (rejection). This sophistication [in giving the illusion of true scientific enquiry] is being driven by these types of kufr, and these in turn sit on top of that fitrah, concealing it, and repressing it. All of these atheists today are very explicit in saying Darwinism is the biggest driver to atheism, it is what Dawkins said drove him to atheism and made him take up his lifelong mission of fighting against belief in God, and these are the types of people Usamah Hasan is sharing sentiments with, against the Muslims, as it relates to Darwinism.

From what has preceded in this series, it is impossible to reconcile Darwinism with the Qur'an and the Sunnah, and any attempt to reconcile comprises takdheeb of Allaah (the Qur'an) and of the Messenger (the Sunnah), by absolute necessity, whether one realizes it or not, and this is what the orthodox Scholars of Islaam have stated:

Rulings of the Scholars on The One Who Believes Man Descended From Apes

There occurs in the Fataawaa Nur Alaa al-Darb (1/83-85) a fatwa of Ibn Baz (rahimahullaah) in response to a question posed about the belief that man used to be an ape in the beginning and then gradually evolved and changed into a man. The fatwa can be found here.

From the answer:

This statement which the questioner has mentioned is rejected (munkar) false (baatil) saying that is in opposition to the Book of Allaah, the Mighty and Majestic, and the Sunnah of His Messenger (alayhis salaatu was salaam) and the consensus of the Salaf of the Ummah... it is obligatory that a servant believe that the creation of Aadam is other than the creation of apes, and that the origin of [the son of] Aadam is the origin that he is upon now, and his origin is not of apes or other than them. Rather, he is a a human fashioned upon his form that is witnessed. So the statement that his origin is from apes is a rejected saying, a false saying. Rather, if disbelief was ascribed to the one saying it, it would be appropriate, and what is most apparent, and Allaah knows best, is that whoever says it alongside his knowledge of what the legislation came with, then he is a kaafir (disbeliever), because he belies (mukadhdhib) Allaah and His Messenger and belies the Book of Allaah in the creation of Aadam.

And also the fatwa of Shaykh Ibn Uthaymeen (rahimahullaah) in response to a questioner who said he studied in a book of history that man's origin is from apes, and with the passing of time he changed into a human. The answer can be found here.

From the answer:

This statement is not correct, I mean the saying that the origin of man is from apes, however the one speaking with this, he is in reality an ape, deformed in intellect and deformed in vision (insight), so it is befitting that we call him an ape, and he is not a human, even if he is in the form of a human... this saying that the origin of man is from apes is not correct and believing in it is disbelief, because it is rejection (takdheeb) of the Qur'an...
