Disapproval Of Stealing

Chapter of Thaa – One
Thawr Ibn Zaid ad-Dili: One Hadith

[141] From Malik from Al-‘Ala Ibn Abdurahman from his father and Ishaq Ibn Abdullah that they both heard Abu Hurairah (Radi Allahu Anhu) say that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, We went out in the company of Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) on the day of (the battle of) Khaibar (for Jihad), and we did not get any gold or silver as war booty, but we got property in the form of things and clothes. Then a man called Rifa’a bin Zaid, from the tribe of Bani Ad-Dubab, presented a slave named Mid’am to Allah’s Apostle (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam). Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) headed towards the valley of Al-Qura, and when he was in the valley of Al- Qura an arrow was thrown by an unidentified person, struck and killed Mid’am who was making a she-camel of Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) kneel down. The people said, “Congratulations to him (the slave) for gaining Paradise.” Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, “No! By Him in Whose Hand my soul is, for the sheet which he stole from the war booty before its distribution on the day of Khaibar, is now burning over him.” When the people heard that, a man brought one or two Shiraks (leather straps of shoes) to the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam). The Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, “A shoe lace of fire, or two shoe laces of fire.”

The first part has now been completed with the aid and support of Allah. 

[GRADING] – [IT’S CHAIN IS SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (2/459 hadith no.1012, book 21, chapter 13, hadith no.25, and with it Sahm A’ir), at-Tamhid (2/3) al-Istidhkar (no.949)

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.6707) and Muslim (no.115) from the hadith of Malik

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) It is known from this hadith that Abu Hurairah (Radi Allahu Anhu) became a Muslim before the battle of Khaibar. 

2) The battle of Khaibar occurred in the seventh Hijri year. 

3) Some narrators have mentioned the wording of battle of Hunain instead of the battle of Khaibar in this hadith. And Allah knows best.

4) Stealing is Haram. In particular stealing from the war booty wealth is haram and a big sin. 

5) Declaring and testifying a specific person as being destined for Jannah without any proof (from the Quran and Hadith) is incorrect. 

6) It is allowed to take an oath when it is necessary, rather it is also allowed to take an oath when it is not necessary in which the aim is to put an emphasis on one’s point of view and Allah’s greatness.

7) It is Sunnah to accept a gift on the condition that there are no doubts that it is bribe or that it can lead to other Haram acts.

The Prohibition of Eating the Meat of Domesticated Donkeys

[372] Abdullah bin Abu Aufa (Radi Allahu Anhu) said: We were smitten with hunger during the nights of Khaibar. On the Day of Khaibar, we fell upon domestic asses and we slaughtered them, and when our earthen pots boiled with them, the announcer of Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) made an announcement that the earthen pots should be turned over, and nothing should be eaten of the flesh of the domestic asses. Some of the people said that Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) had forbidden the meat because the Khumus had not been paid, while others said: He prohibited it for ever.

[GRADING] – [BUKHARI AND MUSLIM] 

[REFERENCES]

Transmitted by

Bukhari, Book of One-fifth of Booty to the Cause of Allah (Khumus), Chapter: The food gained as war booty in the battlefield [no.3155]

Muslim, Book of The Book of Hunting, Slaughter, and what may be Eaten, Chapter: The prohibition of eating the meat of domesticated donkeys [no.1937]

See also Nasai [no.4350] (Graded Sahih by Darusalam) 

[RULINGS FROM THE HADITH]

1) In the battle of Khaibar the meat of domestic donkeys was made haram. See the previous two ahadith no. 370, 371

2) There is also a hadith from Ali ibn Abi Talib (Radi Allahu Anhu) on the prohibition of eating domestic donkeys. Bukhari [no.4216], in this regard there a numerous hadith in Bukhari under the book of Kitab al-Maghazi under the chapter of Ghazwa Khaibar.

3) The hadith of Bukhari [no.5527] and Muslim [no.1936] mentions that when the prohibition for eating the meat of domestic donkeys was announced the companions were cooking the meat and at that very instance, whilst demonstrating great love and understanding of this Dīn and reverential love for the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wasallam), they drained and spilled their dishes.
