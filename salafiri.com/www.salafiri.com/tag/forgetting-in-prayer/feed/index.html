What Should The Person Do If He Forgets In The Prayer

Ayub Ibn Abi Tamimah as-Sakhtiyani: Three Ahadith

[128] From Malik from Ayub Ibn Abi Tamimah from Muhamad Ibn Sirin from Abu Hurairah (Radi Allahu Anhu) that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) offered two rak`at and finished his prayer. So Dhul-Yadain (Radi Allahu Anhu) asked him, “Has the prayer been reduced or have you forgotten?” Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam)  said, “Has Dhul-Yadain spoken the truth?” The people replied in the affirmative. Then Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) stood up and offered the remaining two rak`at and performed Taslim, and then said Takbir and performed two prostrations like his usual prostrations, or a bit longer, and then got up.

[GRADING] – [IT’S CHAIN IS SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] – [BUKHARI]
Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (1/93 hadith no.206, book 3, chapter 15, hadith no.58), at-Tamhid (1/341) al-Istidhkar (no.178) 

And transmitted by al-Bukhari (no.1228) from the hadith of Malik and narrated by Muslim (97/573) from the hadith of Ayub as-Sakhtiyani. 

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) If an individual does the final salam before completing the full Salah then he should count the raka’s he has already performed and make up the remaining missing raka’s of that Salah and do the prostration of forgetfulness at the end of it. The prostration of forgetfulness should be done like the prostration performed in the normal Salah or longer than it. 

2) The Salah does not become fasid if an individual forgets and does the Salam (early) or talks during the Salah. 

3) It is Sunnah to verify a doubt in a hadith. After establishing that it is authentic it is necessary to act upon it. 

4) The Prophets and Messengers (Alayhimusalam) can forget in worldly matters and Salah etc. however please note that they can never forget in matters relating to conveying the religion, information of the previous nations etc. 

5) It is not allowed to leave a matter on a foundation of doubtfulness when that matter is certain. 

6) In the event that there is an apparent contradiction in a narration and it is not possible to reconcile it then  precedence will be given to the group (of other narrators who have narrated the same narration) in comparison to this one narrator. 

7) It is Sunnah to perform the Takbir during the prostration of forgetfulness. 

8) Some people have said that this hadith is Mudtarib. See at-Tamhid (1/364) which has details showing that this saying is false. 

9) It is incorrect to say that Dhul Yadain (Radi Allahu Anhu) was martyred in the battle of Badr because Dhu ash-Shamalin (Radi Allahu Anhu) was martyred and Dhul Yadain (Radi Allahu Anhu) lived for quite a long time after the death of the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam). 

10) The Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) did not have the knowledge of the unseen, otherwise why would he ask other people to verify for him? Also see hadith no.156

What Should the Person Do if He Forgets In the Prayer

Abdurahman al-A’raj: Three Ahadith

[81] from Malik from Ibn Shihab from al-A’raj that Abdullah Ibn Buhayna (Radi Allahu Anhu) said, “The Messenger of Allah, (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam), prayed two rakas with us and then stood without sitting (in the tashahud position) and the people stood with him. When he had finished the prayer and we waited for him to say the taslim, he said ‘Allah is greater’ and did two sajdas from the sitting position before the taslim and then (came up from the sajdas and) said the taslim.”

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri clarified hearing (this hadith from Abdurahman al-A’raj) in Bukhari (no.829)

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 1/96 hadith no.214, book 3, chapter 17, hadith no.65), at-Tamhid (10/183), al-Istidhkar: (no.184, 185)          

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.1224) and Muslim (no.570) from the hadith of Malik

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) No one from the creation is free (or protected) from making mistakes and forgetting except for those who Allah protects.

2) If the person praying salah forgets (to sit down for) the first tashahud and stands up instead, then he shouldn’t sit down (straight away), rather he should complete the prayer (rak’a) and perform the prostration of forgetfulness before or after the salam. If someone stands up then sits back down (straight away after realising the mistake) then according to the majority of the scholars his prayer doesn’t become invalid. See at-Tamhid (10/185)

3) Sa’d Ibn Abi Waqas (Radi Allahu Anhu) was leading the prayer and stood up in the third rak’a (instead of sitting down for tashahud). The people said Subhan Allah however he didn’t sit back down (straight away) and rather he made an indication to the people to stand up and completed the prayer making the two (extra) prostrations (at the end). (Musanaf Ibn Abi Shaybah 2/34 hadith no.4493 and its chain is [Sahih] authentic)
This understanding is also established from Uqba Ibn Amir (Radi Allahu Anhu) in a detailed narration. See al-Mustadrak 1/325 hadith no.1214, and al-Awsat of Ibn al-Mundhir (3/288) and its chain is [Sahih] authentic)

4) Doing salam to one side whilst performing the prostration of forgetfulness is not established from the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) or the noble companions (Radi Allahu Anhum).

5) If the Imam forgets in the prayer and then he forgets to perform the prostration of forgetfulness then according to Hakam Ibn Utba the people praying behind the Imam should perform the prostration of forgetfulness and according to Hamad Ibn Abi Sulayman they don’t need to perform it. (Musanaf Ibn Abi Shayba 2/39 hadith no.4525 and its chain is [Sahih] authentic)
It is established from the hadith of Abdullah Ibn Mas’ud (Radi Allahu Anhu) (Sahih Bukhari no.1226 and Sahih Muslim no.572) that if the Imam forgets to perform the prostration of forgetfulness then he should still perform it after doing the taslim (with the people who were praying behind him)

6) The scholars have an ijma that it is fardh to perform ruku, sujud, qiyam and the last sitting down (tashahud) in salah. (at-Tamhid 10/189)
Therefore if any one of these have been missed out then the raka would have been missed, and it is necessary to repeat this raka.

What Should the Person do if he Forgets in the Prayer

[24] And with the same chain of narration that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) said, “When you stand in prayer, Shaytan comes to you and confuses you until you do not know how much you have prayed. If you find that happening perform two sajdas (at the end of the last tashahud) from the sitting position.”

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri clarified hearing (this hadith from Abu Salamah Ibn Abd ar-Rahman) in Humaydi (with my Tahqiq (verification): (no.953), A’zami edition: (no.947)

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]

Al-Muwatta (narration of Yahya 1/100 hadith no.220, book 4, chapter 1, hadith no.1) at-Tamhid (7/89), al-Istidhkar: (no.193)    

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.1232) and Muslim (82/389) after the hadith (no.569) from the hadith of Malik.

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]

1) Khanzab is the name of the shaytan who puts waswasa (whisperings) in the hearts of those who are performing prayer. (Sahih Muslim: (68/ 2203). In a mawdhu (fabricated) narration in Guniyah ut-Talibin the word “hadith” has been cited regarding Shaytan which is an error of the scribe.

2) Sajdah Sahu (prostration of forgetfulness) is wajib and Sunnah when a mistake is done in prayer. The Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) said, “For each forgetfulness there are two prostrations after giving the salutation.” (Sunan Abu Dawud: (no.1038), and its chain is [Hasan] good)

Note: When putting into consideration other proofs, doing the two sajdas before the salam is also allowed and doing it after the salam.

3) The Imam of the Shias, Ibn Babawayah al-Qumi wrote, “May Allah’s curse be upon the extremists and mufawidah (ie the rafidah), they deny that the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) forgot (doing the sajdah sahu)….the reason why Allah made the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) forget, is so that people know he is a human being and do not end up making him a Rab and divine (i.e. object of worship), and secondly so that the people know about the ruling (issue) of sahu. (Man La Yahdhar al-Fiqhiyah (vol.1 pg.234)

4) It is not established from any hadith that the sajda sahu is performed only after the first salutation i.e. salam. It is written in Fatawa Alamghiri without any proof: “The correct view is that the salutation is done to one side and this is the madhab of the majority” (1/125) . Attributing this to the majority is rejected because it goes against the event (mentioned in the hadith) and it does not contain any reference (or any further information to prove this).
