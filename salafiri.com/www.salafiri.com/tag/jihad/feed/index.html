Hadith No. 104

Imam At-Tirmidhi said: Muhammad bin Bashar narrated to us: Wahb bin Jarir narrated to us: My father informed me on the authority of Yunus from Qatadah, who narrated from Anas (may Allah be pleased with him) who said,

“The pommel of the sword of the Messenger of Allāh ﷺ was [made] from silver.”

Grading and References 

Ṣaḥīh, Sunan At-Tirmīdhi (#1691, and he [Abū ʿĪsā At-Tirmidhi] said ‘This is a ḥasan gharīb ḥadīth’), Sunan Abī Dāwūd (#2583), Sunan An-Nasāʿī (#5376)

Explanation and Benefits

1. In some cases (for example, a ring made of silver or the handle of a sword, and other than these) it is permissible for men to use silver and in other cases (for example, drinking and eating from silver utensils) it is not permissible for anyone to use.

2. One should be prepared for Jihād at all times.

3. Keeping weapons and to adorn them with silver is a Sunnah.

4. The fundamental matters of the religion of Islām are protected and compiled, and in the matters of ijtihād, the doors are open for the scholars of Ahlul-Ḥaqq until the Day of Judgement.

(Shama’il At-Tirmidhi with the Tahqiq of Shaykh Zubayr ‘Aliza’i p. 143)

Whoever commits suicide with anything in this world, he will be punished with that very thing on the Day of Resurrection.[1]

And the one who kills himself by diving (or plunging), will also be diving (or plunging) the same way in the Fire.[4]

This contradicts the views and claims of other writers, that Saudi Arabia promotes violence, this in of itself shows the fallacy of their arguments. They say Saudi Arabia is known to spread Salafism and according to the definition of Salafism, which I must add, is self concocted and a fabrication, in order to justify their narrative and an understanding that conforms to their falsehood. In reality, all such terms related to Salafism are false as there is only one type of Salafism. We can deliberate on this later, but when Jessica Stern; a research professor out of Boston University and who also served on the Clinton administrations National Security Council Staff and J.M Berger; a fellow with George Washington University’s programme on extremism, explained these terms,[3] whilst borrowing them from Quintan Wiktorowitz.[4] According to their definition of ‘Quietist Salafism’ they say:

The quietist faction is, in a sense, the strain of Salafism that has responded the least to the world events of the twentieth century. Individuals in this group understand their central project to be the purification of Islam and do not participate in politics.[5] Though there are quietists Salafis across the Muslim world, the center of gravity for this movement is the existing religious establishment in Saudi Arabia. Saudi Arabia is somewhat atypical for a country in the Muslim world, but the very things that make it unique have made is hospitable to the quietists.[6]

On one hand, many like J. Stern and J.M. It suggests, in a sense, that Saudi Arabia is responsible for movements such as ISIS because of the role that the Saudi Arabian government played in facilitating the spread of Salafism across the region.[7]

As injudicious and untruthful as it sounds, which is undoubtedly the case, this diatribe would have been just ridiculed and brazenly dismissed as a very poor judgement of error, but to add insult to injury, they quote an article of Ed Hussain as evidence![8] This article is a culmination of half truths and some rudimentary information, spun to give a repulsive intent; the authors quote it to suggest a colossal claim, this has but except caused them stern embarrassment. In fact, the term “Salafism” is a direct reference to these early years, and refers to the first few generations of Muslims, known as the salaf. Salafis prefer the Islam of these early Muslims and believe that centuries of human interpretation – influenced by pre-existing religious traditions, cultural biases, political agendas and individual self-interests – have corrupted Islam and led to decline across the Muslim world.[9]

The Virtue Of The Area Which Is Between The House Of The Messenger Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) And The Minbar

One Chapter Of al-Kha
Khubaib Ibn Abdirahman: He has two ahadith  

[154] From Malik from Khubaib Ibn Abdirahman from Hafs Ibn Asim from Abu Hurairah (Radi Allahu Anhu) or Abu Sa’id al-Khudri (Radi Allahu Anhu) that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, “(The land) Between my house and my minbar is a garden from one of the gardens of Jannah, and my minbar is over my Lake-Tank. (Kauthar);

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] 
Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (1/197, hadith no.464, book 14, chapter 5, hadith no.10), at-Tamhid (2/285) and al-Istidhkar (no.433) 

Transmitted by Ahmad (2/465, 533) from the hadith of Malik with the same meaning and narrated by al-Bukhari (no.7335) from the hadith of Malik which is from Abu Hurairah (Radi Allahu Anhu) without any doubt. 

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) Some people say that the meaning of Jannah in this hadith is that this area will be put into Jannah on the Day of Judgement and some other people say that this is where the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayi Wa Sallam) used to live. The people used to learn about the Quran, Iman and things about the religion from the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) and those who act upon this will end up reaching Jannah. Therefore this has been metaphorically called a garden from one of the gardens of Jannah just like it has been mentioned in a hadith that Jannah is under the shades of swords (Bukhari no.2818). Meaning that the sincere believer who fights in the path of Allah will go to Jannah. See at-Tamhid (2/287) and Allah knows best.   

2) Some people use this hadith to try and establish that Madinah Munawarah is more virtuous than Makkah Mukaramah however this deduction is incorrect and neither have the pious predecessors understood this. In a hadith the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) declared that Makkah Mukaramah is the best part of Allah’s Earth. See Sunan Ibn Majah (no.3108, and it’s chain is Sahih and at-Tirmidhi authenticated it no.3925, and al-Hakim on the conditions of the two Shaikhs 3/7 and adh-Dhahabi agreed with him) and at-Tamhid (2/288). No one has the right to talk against this issue after the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) has made a decision. 

3) If a narrator of a narration has a doubt on which thiqah narrator he specified then this is not harmful rather this narration is Sahih on the condition that the rest of the chain is also Sahih. This is because all the companions are classified as being Udul (i.e. all of them are thiqah) therefore the exact companion not being specified in this chain does not cause it to be harmful. wal-Hamdullilah 

4) The Hawd Kawthar is a true reality which Allah specifically gave to His beloved (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam). Ibn Abdul Bar said that the Hawd Kawthar has been mentioned in the mutawatir hadith of the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam). Ahlus Sunnah and the people of truth believe in this and affirm it. Similarly they believe in the hadith which mention the intercession (on the day of judgement) and the punishment of the grave and they affirm this. (at-Tamhid 2/309)

It Is Necessary To Establish The Proof Against A People By Inviting Them To Embrace Islam Before You Declare War On Them

[149] From Malik that Humaid at-Tawil narrated to me from Anas Ibn Malik (Radi Allahu Anhu) that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) set out for Khaibar and reached it at night. He used to not attack (immediately) if he reached the people at night, till it became the morning. So, when the day dawned (and it was the morning), the Jews came out with their bags and spades. When they saw the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam); they said, “(It is) Muhammad (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam)! By Allah! (It is) Muhammad (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) and his army!” The Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, “Allahu-Akbar! (Allah is the Greatest) and Khaibar is ruined, for whenever we approach a nation (i.e. enemy to fight) then it will be a miserable morning for those who have been warned (about Hell and the punishment).”

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] – [BUKHARI]
Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (2/468,469 hadith no.1035, book 21, chapter 19, hadith no.48 and in it ‘lam yugiz’), at-Tamhid (2/215) and al-Istidhkar (no.972) 

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.2945) from the hadith of Malik and Humaid at-Tawil clarified hearing in Bukhari (no.2943) 

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) It is not necessary to invite the Kuffar to Islam again during the time of war when they have already been invited to accept the religion of Islam prior to the war.
