Passing Urine Or Defecating Whilst Facing The Qiblah Is Prohibited

Rafi’ Ibn Ishaq: Two ahadith

[124] From Malik from Ishaq Ibn Abdillah Ibn Abi Talha from Rafi’ Ibn Ishaq Mawla of the people of ash-Shifa they said about him that he was the Mawla of Abi Talha that he heard Abu Ayub (al-Ansari) (Radi Allahu Anhu) the companion of the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) say when he was in Egypt, “If anyone of you goes to an open space for answering the call of nature he should neither face nor turn his back towards the Qiblah; he should either face the east or the west.”

[GRADING] – [IT’S CHAIN IS SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] 
Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (1/193, hadith no.455, book 14, chapter 1, hadith no.1), at-Tamhid (1/303) and he said, “This hadith is authentically connected” and al-Istidhkar (no.424) 

Transmitted by an-Nasa’i (1/21 hadith no.20) from the hadith of Ibn al-Qasim from al-Imam Malik and the route of the hadith is in Bukhari (no.144) and Muslim (no.264) and other than them. 

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) Facing or putting one’s back towards the Qiblah (Makkah) whilst urinating or defecating are not allowed. There are some authentic ahadith which show the permissibility of putting ones back towards the Qiblah and urinating or defecating therefore only during extreme circumstances is one allowed to face the Qiblah and urinate or defecate due to having no other choice or a Sharia compliant excuse. 

2) Being displeased with and rejecting those things which go against the Quran and Sunnah  is the methodology and exclusive sign of the people of Iman. 

3) The noble companions (Radi Allahu Anhum) never thought about going against the the command or action of their beloved Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) rather they were always ready to strive upon acting on the Quran and Hadith. 

4) The text remains upon its general meaning unless and until a specific evidence is available. 

5) It is necessary to act upon the rulings of the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) rather it is a sign of Iman even if these rulings are not clearly mentioned in the Quran.

The Urine of an Infant Baby Boy

[56] And from the same chain of narration from Ubaydillah Ibn Abdullaah  Ibn Utba Ibn Mas’ud from Um Qays Ibn Mihsan that she brought a baby boy of her’s who was not yet eating food to the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) and he sat him in his arms and he urinated on his garment, so the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) called for some water and sprinkled over it but did not wash it.

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

Ibn Shihab clarified hearing (this hadith from Ubaydullah Ibn Abdullah Ibn Utba Ibn Mas’ud) in Muslim (104/287)

[REFERENCES] – [BUKHARI]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 1/64 hadith no.137, book 2, chapter 30, hadith no.109) at-Tamhid (9/108) al-Istidhkar: (no.116)

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.223) from the hadith of Malik and narrated by Muslim (no.287) from the hadith of Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri.

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]

1) The hadith (where it says that) water should only be sprinkled over the area where the baby boy, who has not started to eat, has urinated and the area where the baby girl, who has not started to eat, has urinated should be washed is mutawatir. See Nadhmul Mutanathar (no.37)

2) The noble Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) also used to have affection, love and show compassionate behavior towards very small children (babies). Allah the Most High sent him as a mercy to all the worlds. (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam).

3) If an infant baby boy (who is currently suckling) urinates on someone’s clothes or body then it will not be necessary to wash the affected area, rather just sprinkling water over that area will be sufficient. See hadith (no.461) (of this book)

4) A’isha (Radi Allahu Anha) said about the one who is affected by the urine of a small baby boy: it should be sprinkled over. (Musanaf Ibn Abi Shaybah (1/119) hadith (no.1274), and its chain is authentic)

5) Um Qays’s name is Jazamah Bint Wahb Ibn Muhsin (Radi Allahu Anha)

6) There is a consensus that the urine of every person who eats is impure. (at-Tamhid (9/109))

7) Every Qiyas is rejected in comparison to the Quran and Sunnah

8) There is a marfu’ narration from Abus Samh (Radi Allahu Anhu) that the urine of a female should be washed and the urine of a male should be sprinkled over. (Sunan Abu Dawud: (no.376) and its chain is [Sahih] authentic, and Ibn Khuzaimah authenticated it: (no.283) and al-Hakim (1/166), and adh-Dhahabi) Ibn Abdul Bar declaring this hadith to be [Daif] weak is a mistake.
