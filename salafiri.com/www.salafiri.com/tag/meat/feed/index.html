 All Of The Meat Of Animals (Carnivores) With Fangs (Canine Teeth, i.e. Carnivores) Are Haram

Isma’il Ibn Abi Hakim: One hadith

[113] From Malik from Isma’il Ibn Abi Hakim from Ubaida Ibn Sufyan[1] al-Hadrami from Abu Huraira (Radi Allahu Anhu) that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, “The eating of all fanged beasts (predators) of prey is unlawful.”

[GRADING] – [IT’S CHAIN IS SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] – [MUSLIM]
Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (2/496 hadith no.1097 and Malik said,”And it is an affair (which is accepted) with us”, book 25, chapter 4, hadith no.14), at-Tamhid (1/139) and he said,”and this hadith is established as being authentic and its authenticity is agreed upon” and al-Istidhkar (no.1029) 

Transmitted by Muslim (15/1933) from the hadith of Malik 

[1] from the narration of Yahya Ibn Yahya it has come in the original “Sitin (Sixty)”

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) From the general wording of the hadith it is understood that all the types of predators such as dogs, cats, foxes, wolves, lions, scorpions and hyenas etc. are haram.

2) From the viewpoint of this hadith, it abrogates the narration which mentions that Hyena’s are a type of prey. 

3) This hadith is classified as being a principle therefore if any animal, which we know of or don’t know of yet, meets the mentioned characteristics then eating it becomes haram.

4) Ibn Abdul Bar said, “It is necessary to understand the prohibition which is mentioned in the marfu hadith to be of tahrimi (i.e. haram) except if there is a specific proof (Qarina) that it should be understood as being highly recommended.” See at-Tamhid (1/140)

5) The evidence will either be mentioned from the Quran or Sunnah or Ijma will support it or the proof will be understood from the righteous predecessors.  

6) There is an Ijma of the Muslims that those things which are impure (for example feces etc.) is impure for every individual and is severely haram which is not halal in any condition. 

7) There is no difference of opinion between the Muslim scholars that the eating and selling of monkeys is not allowed. Ibn Abdul Bar said, “According to me the same ruling applies to Elephants.” (at-Tamhid 1/157) 

8) The Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) prohibited taking the skins of predators. (as-Sunan al-Kubra of al-Bayhaqi 1/21 and its chain is [Hasan] good)

Therefore these skins do not become pure from tanning. Some people say that the skin of dogs becomes pure from tanning and therefore it is allowed to make a prayer mat or bucket from it. However since this goes against the above hadith this is rejected. 

9) The fish known to be called Sharks are classified as being predators too, therefore it is not halal to eat them. and Allah knows best. For more detailed fiqhi benefits see the previous hadith no.52 and no.76

For The Qurbani 10 People Can Have A Share In The Sacrifice Of A Camel And 7 People Can Have A Share In The Sacrifice Of A Cow

[106] From Malik from Abu az-Zubair from Jabir Ibn Abdullah as-Sulami (Radi Allahu Anhu) that he said,”In the year of Hudaibiya (6 AH), we, along with Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam), sacrificed a camel for seven persons and a cow for seven persons (as a Qurbani).

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] – [MUSLIM]
Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (2/486 hadith no.1068, book 23, chapter 5, hadith no.9), at-Tamhid (12/147), and he said,”This hadith is [Sahih] authentic according to the people of knowledge.” and al-Istidhkar (no.1002) 

Transmitted by Muslim (350/1318) from the hadith of Malik and Abu az-Zubair clarified hearing this hadith from Jabir Ibn Abdullah as-Sulami (Radi Allahu Anhu) in Ahmad (3/378 hadith no.15043) 

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) Seven people can share the sacrifice of a cow or camel. According to another hadith ten people can share in the sacrifice of a camel. (See Sunan at-Tirmidhi no.1501, and its chain is [Hasan] good, Sunan an-Nasa’i no.4397, Sunan Ibn Maja no.3131)

2) The year of Hudaibiya is the 6th year of Hijri. See at-Tamhid (12/147)

3) The scholars have a difference of opinion in regards to when a dhimmi (non-muslim who lives in a Muslim country and pays the jizyah) has one part of the sacrifice. Some say it is allowed and some say it is not allowed. The most correct view is to not include the dhimmi to have a share in the sacrifice.  

4) If there is a person of bid’ah who is included in the sacrifice and their bid’ah does not reach the level of kufr then that sacrifice is allowed. However it is better to only include those who have the correct aqidah in the sacrifice and not to include any person of bidah. 

5) All the sacrifice can also be done from one person.

6) If there are only six people who share a part each in the sacrifice of a cow or camel then one of them individuals should take two parts (T.N. as opposed to one so that it becomes a seven part sacrifice). The seventh part should not be distributed among all the six participants because there is no evidence to do this.  

7) If a person can not go to the scared sanctuary (T.N. Makkah) due to a Sharia compliant excuse then it is allowed to sacrifice on behalf of him which is external to the sacred sanctuary. However it is better for him to carry out the sacrifice in the boundary of the sacred sanctuary. 

8) Sacrificing one goat on behalf of one household is enough. See Sunan at-Tirmidhi (no.1505, and its chain is [Hasan] good and he said, “[Hasan Sahih]“

Abu Ayub al-Ansari (Radi Allahu Anhu) said that we used to sacrifice one goat on behalf of the whole household. (al-Muwatta 2/486 hadith no.1069, and its chain is [Sahih] authentic, and Sunan al-Kubra of al-Bayhaqi 9/268)

By performing qiyas on the above, having one share in the sacrifice of a cow from the whole household is enough. Allah knows best. 

9) Performing sacrifice anytime other than during Hajj is a Sunnah and it is not obligatory. 

See al-Muwatta (2/487, Malik said,”The sacrifice is Sunnah, and it is not obligatory. I prefer that anyone who has the price of the animal should not abandon it.” 

10) There is an Ijma that the ruling of a Buffalo is the same as a cow because the Buffalo is a type of cow. See the book of Ijma of Ibn al-Mundhir (no.91). However from other evidences and as a precautionary measure it is better to not have it as a sacrificial animal. And Allah knows best.

The Qurbani Meat Can Be Eaten For Longer Than 3 Days

[105] From Malik from Abu az-Zubair from Jabir Ibn Abdullah as-Sulami (Radi Allahu Anhu) that that Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) forbade eating the flesh of sacrificed animals beyond three (days). then he said afterwards, “Eat, give in charity, make a provision and keep (store) it.”

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] – [MUSLIM]
Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (2/484 hadith no.1065, book 23, chapter 4, hadith no.6), at-Tamhid (12/163) and al-Istidhkar (no.999) 

Transmitted by Muslim (29/1972) from the hadith of Malik and narrated by Ata Ibn Abi Rabah from Jabir with the same meaning and Abu az-Zubair  clarified hearing this hadith from Jabir Ibn Abdullah as-Sulami (Radi Allahu Anhu) in Ahmad (3/378 hadith no.15042) 

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) The ruling of prohibition in eating Qurbani meat after 3 days is abrogated. Also see hadith no.309 

2) It is correct to use and eat the Qurbani meat yourself in addition to storing it. Additionally, giving it in charity or to relatives, friends and other than them as a gift is a good thing.  

3) From the understanding of this hadith it is known that there are 3 days for Qurbani. In connection to this a summarised research of this issue is below:

All those narrations which mention that the days of tashriq are all days for sacrifice, are all weak and not established.

Ibn Umar (Radi Allahu Anhu) said, “The sacrifice can be done up to two days after the Day of Sacrifice.” (Muwatta Imam Malik 2/487, hadith no.1071 and its chain is [Sahih] authentic)

Ibn Abbas (Radi Allahu Anhu) said, “There are two additional days of sacrifice after the first day of sacrifice and the most virtuous sacrifice is the one performed on the first day of slaughtering.” (Ahkam ul-Quran of at-Tahawi 2/205 hadith no.1576 and it is [Sahih] authentic)

Anas (Radi Allahu Anhu) said, “There are two days of sacrifice after the first day.” (Ahkam ul-Quran of at-Tahawi 2/206 hadith no.1571 and it is [Sahih] authentic)

Ali (Radi Allahu Anhu) said, “There are three days for sacrifice.” (Ahkam ul-Quran of at-Tahawi 2/205 hadith no.1569 and it is [Hasan] good)

This is the position of the majority of the companions and majority of the scholars and this is the most correct.  

For more details see the al-Hadith magazine Hazro: edition no.44, pg.6-11

4) It is allowed to allocate parts of the Qurbani meat. A part for oneself, another part for the poor and the third part for relatives and friends. It is also allowed to not allocate parts.     

5) In the Islamic Sharia the aim of abrogated (previous rule) and abrogating (new rule) was part of educating and reforming the society. Therefore one should now act upon the new rule as opposed to the abrogated (previous ruling).

The Permissibility of Eating Chicken Meat

[375] Narrated Zahdam Once, while we were sitting with Abu Musa Al-Ash`ari (Radi Allahu Anhu), there was brought to him a meal which contained chicken meat, and there was sitting beside him, a man from the tribe of Bani Taimul-lah who looked like one of the Mawali. Abu Musa said to the man “come and then eat”. Abu Musa said to him (again), “Come along because indeed I saw the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu alayhi wa sallam) eat it.” Narrated in Bukhari in a lengthy hadith

[GRADING] – [BUKHARI]

[REFERENCES]

Transmitted by

Bukhari, with the same meaning in a lengthy hadith, Book of Tawhid, Chapter: “While Allah has created you and what you make” [no.7555, 6649, 5518, 4385, 3133]

Muslim, The book of Oaths; Chapter: It is recommended for the one who swears an oath then sees that something else is better than it; To do that which is better and offer expiation for his oath [no.1649]

Nasai [no.4346], Tirmidhi [no.1817, 1826]

[RULINGS FROM THE HADITH]

1) This hadith proves Chicken meat is halal and can be consumed.

The Permissibility of Eating Locusts

[374] Narrated Abdullah Ibn Abu `Aufa (Radi Allahu Anhu):
We participated with the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) in seven Ghazawat (battles), and we used to eat locusts with him.

[GRADING] – [NARRATED BY MUSLIM]

[REFERENCES]

Transmitted by

Bukhari, Book of Hunting, Slaughtering, Chapter: The eating of locusts [no.5495]

Muslim, Book of The Book of Hunting, Slaughter, and what may be Eaten, Chapter: The permissibility of eating Locusts [no.1952]

Abu Dawud [no.3812], Tirmidhi [no.1821-1822], Nasai [7:210], Ahmad [4:380], Darimi [no.2010], Musnad Humaidi [no.713]

[RULINGS FROM THE HADITH]

1) This hadith establishes that locusts are halal. You eat them by frying (boiling, grilling, cooking etc.) them.

2) Shaikh Abdullah Bassam mentions there is ijma of the Muslims on the lawfulness of eating locusts. [Tawḍih al-Aḥkam [7:10] 

3) It was narrated from ‘Abdullah bin ‘Umar that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said:

“Two kinds of dead meat and two kinds of blood have been permitted to us. The two kinds of dead meat are fish and locusts, and the two kinds of blood are the liver and spleen” (Sunan Ibn Majah, Book of Food, [no.3218, Ahmad [2:97 no.5690]

4)   This is one of the best diets (rations) to have whilst being in battle because:

  1. Readily available and you don’t need to look after them like other animals such as cows, chicken etc.
  2. Plenty of them available when they (typically) come in swarms
  3. They mature within weeks and reproduce in large numbers
  4. Easy to cook
  5. High in protein and good fats
  6. Lightweight to carry
