The Sixth Major Sin
Disrespect of the Parents

Allah, Mighty and Majestic, says,

“And your Lord has decreed that you worship none but Him. And that you be dutiful to your parents. If one of them or both of them attain old age in your life, say not to them a word of disrespect, nor shout at them but address them in terms of honour. And lower unto them the wing of submission and humility through mercy, and say: ‘My Lord! Bestow on them Your Mercy as they did bring me up when I was young.'”
(Surah Al-Isra’, 17:23-24)

And The Most High says,

“And We have enjoined on man to be good and dutiful to his parents…”
(Surah Al-Ankabut, 29:8)

until the end of the Ayah.

46. The Prophet ﷺ said, “Shall I not inform you about the worst of the Major Sins?…” , then he mentioned disrespect of the parents from them. Agreed upon.¹

47. And he ﷺ said, “The pleasure of the Rabb lies in the pleasure of the father, and the anger of Allah lies in the anger of the father.” Sahih.²

48. And it is narrated from him ﷺ, “The father is the middle gate of Paradise, so if you wish then guard it, and if you wish then neglect it.” At-Tirmidhi authenticated it.³

49. And it is narrated that he ﷺ said, “Paradise is under the feet of mothers.”⁴

50. And he ﷺ said to a man that asked him for permission to take part in Jihad with him, “Are your parents alive?” So he replied in the affirmative, so he said, “So strive (Jahid) in their service.”⁵

51. And he said, “Your mother, then your father, then your sister, then your brother, then your closest relatives.”⁶

52. And it is narrated on the authority of the Prophet ﷺ that he said, “The one that disrespects his parents will not enter Paradise, nor the one that reminds people of his favours, nor the one addicted to alcohol, nor the one that believes in magic.”⁷

53. ‘Abdullah bin ‘Amr (may Allah be pleased with them both) said, “A bedouin came and said: ‘O Messenger of Allah! What are the Major Sins?’ So he (the Prophet ﷺ) replied, ‘Associating partners with Allah.’, he said, ‘Then what?’, he replied, ‘Then disrespecting the parents.’, he said, ‘Then what?’, he replied, ‘To take an oath Al-Ghamus.’“⁸

54. And it is narrated that he ﷺ said, “The one that disrespects his parents will not enter Paradise, nor the one that belies Al-Qadr.”⁹

55. And ‘Isa bin Talha bin ‘Ubayd Allah narrated from ‘Amr bin Murrah Al-Juhani (May Allah be pleased with him) that a man said, “O Messenger of Allah! If I pray the Five Prayers, and fast Ramadan, and give the Zakah, and make pilgrimage (Hajj) to Al-Bayt, then what do you think will happen with me?” He said, “Whoever does that will be with the Prophets, the Truthful and the Martyrs, except the one that disrespects his parents.”¹⁰

56. On the authority of Bakkar bin ‘Abdul Aziz bin Abi Bakrah, who said: My father narrated to me, on the authority of of Abi Bakrah in Marfu’ form (i.e., that the Prophet ﷺ said), “Allah will defer [the punishment] for whatever sins He Wills until the Day of Resurrection, except for disrespect of the parents, for verily it is hastened for whoever does it.”¹¹

57. And the Prophet ﷺ said, “A son cannot repay his father [for what he has done for him], except if he finds him as a slave, then buys him and sets him free.” Narrated by Muslim.¹²

58. And on the authority of the Prophet ﷺ, with a Hasan (acceptable) chain of narration, that he said, “Allah cursed the one that disrespects his parents.” 

59. And he ﷺ said, “The maternal auntie has the same status as the mother.” At-Tirmidhi authenticated it.¹³

60. And it is narrated on the authority of Wahb bin Munabbih that he said, “Verily Allah said: O Musa! Honour your parents, for verily whosoever honours his parents, then I will extend his life, and bestow him with a child that will be dutiful to him, and whosoever disrespects his parents, then I will shorten his life and give him a child which disrespects him.”¹⁴

61. And Ka’b said, “By the One in Whose Hand is my soul! Verily Allah will bring about the destruction of a slave when he disrespects his parents, so as to create a punishment for him, and verily Allah will increase the lifespan of a slave when he is dutiful to his parents, so as to increase him in good.”¹⁵

62. And Abu Bakr bin Abi Maryam said, “I read in the Torah: Whoever hits their father is to be killed.”¹⁶

63. And Wahb said, “In the Torah: Whoever strikes their father is to be stoned.”

(Al-Kaba’ir of Imam Adh-Dhahabi, p.130-143,
Maktaba Al-Furqan, 2003CE/1424H, 2nd Edn.)

An Introduction to the Treatise

Origin

This treatise is found in Kitab Al-Kawakib Ad-Darari of Imam Ibn ‘Urwah, Volume 39 across six pages in Darul Kutub Adh-Dhahariyyah, and in Markaz Makhtutat w’at-Turath Kuwait #5/12811, and its chain of narration to the author is authentic, every narrator is a trustworthy Imam.

Narrators

  1. ‘Ali bin Husayn bin ‘Urwah Abu’l Hasan Ad-Dimashqi, he was born in the year 760H, Ibn Muflih said regarding him, “The Shaykh, the ‘Aalim, the Pious, the Role-Model – he arranged the Musnad (of Imam Ahmad) in accordance to the chapters of Al-Bukhari, and he named it Al-Kawakib Ad-Darari Fi Tartib Musnad Al-Imam Ahmad ‘Ala Abwab Al-Bukhari, and he explained it in 120 volumes.”
  2. Yahya bin Yusuf bin Ya’qub Ar-Rahbi Ad-Dimashqi, he was born in the year 714H, and Ibn Hajar said regarding him, “He took from many of the companions of Ibn Taymiyyah, and he passed away in the year 894H.”
  3. Al-Imam Al-Hafidh Abu ‘Abdillah Muhammad bin Ahmad bin ‘Uthman Adh-Dhahabi, the author of this treatise, he was born in the year 673H, and his father was a goldsmith, hence he was known as Adh-Dhahabi. He was the great Hafidh and scholar of Hadith, author of the famous ‘Siyar A’lam An-Nubala’ and ‘Tarikh Al-Islam’ – well versed in the various sciences such as Aqidah, Fiqh, Hadith, History, and his Shaykhs were many, the most famous of which being Al-Hafidh Ibn Kathir, author of the famous Tafsir, may Allah have mercy on them all.

The Topic of this Treatise

This treatise pertains to affirming and establishing the attribute of Al-Yadayn (The Two Hands) of Allah, Exalted is He, and the author establishes these Lofty Attributes by using evidences from the Ayat of the Qur’an, Ahadith, and Athar of the Salaf As-Salih. The methodology of the Ahlul-Hadith, Ahlus-Sunnah, is to affirm these attributes without Tahrif (distortion), or Takyif (ascribing a modality to these attributes), or Tamthil or Tashbih (likening these attributes to the creation) –  The early Imams of this Ummah discussed these Lofty Attributes in their works, such as:

Imam At-Tirmidhi, “They (the Jahmiyyah) say: ‘Allah did not create Adam with His Hand’, and they say: ‘Verily Hand here only means power.'” (Sunan At-Tirmidhi)

‘Abdullah bin Zubayr Abi Bakr Imam Al-Humaydi (Student of Sufyan bin ‘Uyaynah), “The Sunnah with us is […] belief in the Ru’yah (seeing Allah) after death, and what has been mentioned in the Qur’an and Hadith, such as: {…and the heavens will be rolled up in His Right Hand…} [Az-Zumar, 39:67].” (Usul As-Sunnah, Musnad of Al-Humaydi)

Imam Al-Muzani (Student of Imam Ash-Shafi’i), “…Then He created Adam with His Hand.” (Sharh As-Sunnah of Imam Al-Muzani)

Imam Harb Al-Kirmani (Student of Imam Ahmad and Ishaq bin Rahuyah), “and He (Allah) created Adam with His Hand, in His Image.” (Ijma’ Al-Salaf fil I’tiqad)

Imam Ibn Abi Dawud, “and the Jahmiyyah certainly reject His Right Hand.” (Al-Ha’iyyah)

Imam Ibn Abi Zayd Al-Qayrawani, “And verily He is [able to be] heard and seen, and He Grips and Opens [His Hand], and both of His Hands are outstretched.” (Al-Jami’ fi’l Sunan w’al-Adab w’al-Maghazi w’at-Tarikh)

Imam As-Sabuni said, “They (Ashab Al-Hadith) do not distort the Words from their proper places (meanings) by claiming the Two Hands are only His Blessings, or His Power, [like] the distortions of the Mu’tazila Jahmiyyah, May Allāh destroy them.” (Aqidah As-Salaf wa Ashab Al-Hadith)

The Treatise (Narrations 1-25)

Ash-Shaykh Shams Al-Din Muhammad bin Ahmad bin ‘Uthman Adh-Dhahabi said:

Affirming Al-Yad for Allah, Exalted is the Attribute from His Attributes

Allah, Exalted is He, said,

“What prevented you from prostrating to whom I created with both My Hands.”
(Surah Sad, 38:75)

And He said,

“Rather! Both His Hands are outstretched.”
(Surah Al-Ma’idah, 5:64)

And He said,

“Do they not see that We have created for them from what Our Hands have made…”
(Surah Ya-Sin, 37:61)

And He said,

“…and the Heavens will be rolled up in His Right Hand…”
(Surah Az-Zumar, 39:67)

And He said,

“Verily, those who pledge allegiance to you are pledging allegiance to Allah, the Hand of Allah is above their hands.”
(Surah Al-Fath, 48:10)

  1. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, “Verily, Musa met Adam, so he said: ‘Allah created you by His Hand, and the Angels prostrated to you, so by Allah! If you had not done what you had done, not one single person from your offspring would enter the Fire!’ So he replied, ‘O Musa! You are the one who Allah chose for His Message and to Speak with…'”¹ and he mentioned the Hadith.
  2. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, “Allah will fold up the Heavens on the Day of Resurrection, then He will take them by His Hand and He will say, ‘I am the King! Where are the Tyrants? Where are the arrogant?'”²
  3. ‘Umar bin Hamzah mentioned the previous Hadith, and he added, “I heard ‘Ikrimah saying: Both of the Hands of Allah are Right, so He will fold up the Heavens and take them by His Hand, then say: ‘I am the King!’, then he will take the Earths by His other Hand.'”³
  4. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, “Verily when a slave gives charity from good, Allah accepts it, and takes it with His Right Hand, and grows it as one of you would raise his colt or young camel, and even if a man gives charity with a crumb, then it grows in the Hand of Allah until it becomes like Mount Uhud – so give in charity.” These Ahadith are authentic.⁴
  5. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, “Verily the first thing that Allah created was the Pen, so He took it with His Right Hand, and both of His Hands are Right, and He wrote what is to be from the actions, righteous or immoral, [in every] moist or dry [place], so He counted them in Adh-Dhikr, then He will say: Read, If you wish: “This record of Ours speaks about you with truth, Verily, We were recording what you used to do.” [Al-Jathiya, 45:29]
    This is a Hasan Hadith.⁵
  6. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, “The Right Hand of Allah is full, and nothing in the night or the day (i.e., His Bounty) diminishes it, and in His other Hand is the Two Scales, He raises it and lowers it.” Agreed upon (by Bukhari and Muslim).⁶
  7. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, “The Just will be on pulpits of light on the Right of Ar-Rahman, and Both of His Hands are right – those that are just with their families and their judgements and those that they are in charge of.” Muslim narrated it.⁷
  8. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, “Allah will take His Heavens and His Earths by His Hands and He will say, ‘I am Allah.’ – And he (The Messenger of Allah) clenched his fingers and opened them – ‘I am The King, I am Ar-Rahman.'” And this is a Sahih Hadith.⁸
  9. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, “Verily, on the Day of Resurrection, Allah (Mighty and Majestic) will gather the Heavens and the Earths in His Grasp, then He will say: ‘I am Allah.'”⁹
  10. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, “Allah created the Garden of Eden, and He planted its trees by His Hand, then He said to it, ‘Speak.’ So it said, ‘Certainly, the believers are successful.’ [Surah Al-Mu’minun, 23:1].” Authentic.¹⁰
  11. Abu Bakr As-Siddiq said, “Allah created the creation, and they were in His Grasp, so He said to those in His Hand: Enter into Jannah with peace, and He said to those in His other Hand: Enter into the Fire.” Al-Lalaka’i and Ibn Battah both narrated it through the path of ‘Abdur Rahman.¹¹
  12. The Prophet ﷺ said, “Verily, Charity falls into the Hand of Allah, Exalted is He, before it falls into the hand of the recipient.” And this is authentic.¹²
  13. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, “Verily, Allah wrote a book with His Hand, and He placed it with Him above the Throne: ‘Verily My Mercy has overcome My Anger.'” And this is an authentic Hadith.¹³
  14. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, “Allah created the creation, and he decreed the Divine Will, then He took the People of the Right by His Right Hand, and He took the People of the Left by His Other Hand, and Both of His Hands are Right, then He Said: Am I not your Rabb?…” And this is a Hasan Hadith, Ibn Battah narrated it in Al-Ibanah.¹⁴
  15. Ibn ‘Umar said, “Verily Allah created four things with His Hand: Adam, and the Pen, and the Garden of Eden [and the Throne], and He said for the rest of the creation: ‘Kun (Be)’ and so it was.” More than one narrated this from ‘Ubayd, such as Sufyan Ath-Thawri and ‘Abdul Wahid Ibn Ziyad.¹⁵
  16. And it is authentic from from Mughith bin Sumi that he said, “It has reached me that verily Allah did not create anything with His Hand except three: He planted Al-Jannah with His Hand, and He created Adam with His Hand, and He wrote the Tawrah with His Hand.”¹⁶
  17. And it is authentic from from Hakim bin Jabir that he said, “I have informed you that your Rabb did not touch anything with His Hand except three: He planted Al-Jannah with His Hand, and He created Adam with His Hand, and He wrote the Tawrah with His Hand.”¹⁷
  18. And it is authentic from Sa’id bin Abi Hilal who said, “It has reached me that the first thing revealed from Allah upon Musa was: ‘In the Name of Allah, Ar-Rahman, Ar-Rahim, This is a book from Allah by His Hand in the Tablets for His Slave, Musa.'”¹⁸
  19. And it is authentic from ‘Ata’ bin Yasar that he said, “The Angels said: ‘O Rabb! You made Bani Adam, then you made them to eat, and to drink, and to enjoy women, and you did not make any of those for us, so when you made the Dunya for them, then make the Akhirah for us.’ So Allah, Mighty and Majestic, said: ‘I will not make the offspring of the one I made by My Hands like the one I said ‘Kun’ (Be) for.'” ¹⁹
  20. And ‘Abdullah bin Salih narrated it from Al-Layth, from Hisham bin Sa’d, from Zayd bin Aslam, from ‘Ata’ bin Yasar, from ‘Abdullah bin ‘Amr and bin Al-‘As, and this chain of narration is authentic and established, ‘Uthman bin Sa’id Ad-Darimi likewise narrated it from ‘Abdullah bin Salih.²⁰
  21. And it is authentic from Ibn ‘Abbas that he said, “The Seven Heavens and the Seven Earths are not in the Hand of Ar-Rahman, except like a mustard seed in the hand of one of you.”²¹
  22. And it is authentic from Wahb bin Munnabih that he said, “The creation is not in the Grasp of Allah, except like a mustard seed here in the hand of one of you.”
  23. And it is authentic from Ibn ‘Abbas that he said, “Allah wiped the back of Adam, then removed every purity with His Right Hand, and removed every impurity with His Other Hand.”²³
  24. And it has been narrated with a Hasan chain of narration that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, “The first thing Allah created was the Pen, then He took it with His Right Hand, and Both of His Hands are Right, and He wrote the Dunya and what is to occur in it.” Narrated by Baqiyyah bin Al-Walid, from Arta’ bin Al-Mundhir, from Mujahid, from Ibn ‘Umar.²⁴
  25. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, “Whoever gives the equivalent of a date from good income in charity, and Allah does not accept anything except good, then verily Allah raises it for one of you like one of you raises his colt or young camel, then it grows in the Hand of Ar-Rahman until it becomes greater than a mountain.”²⁵

Hadith Number 2

The Virtue of the Land of Sham

 

Ash-Shaykh al-Musnad Kamaludeen Aboo Nasr Abdulazeez Ibn Abdilmun’im Ibn al-Khadir Ibn Shibl Ibn Abdil Harithee reported to us – he recited it and I heard him on the day of Jumah (Friday) sixth of Sha’baan in the year 669 Hijri at Jami’ Dimashq that Al Hafidh Aboo Muhammad al-Qaasim Ibn al-Hafidh Abil Qasim Alee Ibn al-Hasan Ibn Hibatillah Ibn Asaakir reported to us – he read it in Rabial Aakhir in the year 596 Hijri: that Abul Fadha’il Naasir Ibn Mahmood Ibn Alee al-Qudsee as-Saa’igh and Aboo al-Qaasim Nasr Ibn Ahmad Ibn Muqaatil as-Soosee reported to us – they both read it and said that: Abul Hasan Alee Ibn Ahmad Ibn Zuhayr al-Malikee reported to us that: Abul Hasan Alee Ibn Muhammad Ibn Shujaa’ ar-Rib’ee  al-Malikee narrated to us that: Abu Bakr Muhammad Ibn Abdurahmaan Ibn Ubaydillah al-Qattaan reported us that: Khaythamah narrated to us that: al-Abbaas Ibn al-Waleed narrated to us that: Uqbah Ibn Alqamah narrated to us that: Sa’eed Ibn Abdilazeez narrated to us that: from Atiyyah Ibn Qays from Abdillah Ibn Amr (Ibn al-Aas) (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) that he said, 

the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhee Was-Sallam) said, “I saw the spine of a book under my pillow, then I saw an eminent nur (light) moving towards Sham (Syria]. For verily when the fitan (trials and tribulations) arise. iman (belief) will be in Shaam.”  

He was born in the year 589 Hijri and died in Sha’baan, year 672 Hijri. 

[GRADING AND REFERENCES] – [Hasan- good] 

Ibn Asaakir narrated this from Muhammad Ibn Abdurahmaan Ibn Ubaydullah al-Qattaan (Tareekh Dimishq (1/102)) and Abul Abbaas Muhammad Ibn Ya’qoob al-Asam narrated this from Abbaas Ibn Waleed Ibn Mazeed (Juz al-Asim: (no.51), also Haakim (4/509) hadeeth (no.8554) Bayhaqee (Dalail an-Nubuwah (6/448)) and Aboo Na’eem al-Isbahaanee (Hilyatul Awliyaa (5/252) and other than them have transmitted it from the chain of Sa’eed Ibn Abdul Azeez from Yunus Ibn Maysarah Ibn Halbis from Abdullah Ibn Amr Ibn al-Aas (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) and both Haakim and Dhahabee have declared it authentic on the conditions of Bukharee and Muslim! 

Both of these chains (of Bayhaqee and Asim) are [Hasan] good.  

[BIOGRAPHY OF THE NARRATOR OF THE HADEETH]  

Abdullah Ibn Amr Ibn al-Aas (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) 

A Summarised comprehensive discussion of Abdullah Ibn Amr Ibn al-Aas (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) is as follows: 

Name and lineage:    

Abu Muhammad Abdullah Ibn Amr Ibn al-Aas Ibn Waa’il Ibn Haashim Ibn Sa’eed Ibn Sa’d Ibn Sahm Ibn Amr Ibn Hasees Ibn Ka’b Ibn Lu’ay Ibn Ghaalib al-Qurashee as-Sahmee al-Makkee (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) 

Students:  

Aboo Umaamah As’ad Ibn Sahl Ibn Haneef (RadhiAllaahu Anhu), Anas Ibn Maalik (RadhiAllaahu Anhu), Jubayr Ibn Nafeer al-Hadhramee, al-Hasan al-Basree, Humayd Ibn Abdurahmaan Ibn Awf, Zar Ibn Jaysh al-Asadee, Saalim Ibn Abee al-Ja’d, as-Saa’ib Ibn Furookh ash-Shaa’ir, Sa’eed Ibn Musayyab, Shuayb Ibn Muhammad Ibn Abdullah Ibn Amr Ibn al-Aas Waalid Amr Ibn Shuayb, Ta’oos Ibn Kaysaan, Aamir ash-Shu’bee, Abdullah Ibn Buraydah al-Aslamee, Abdullah Ibn Ubaydullah Ibn Abee Maleekah, Ataa Ibn Abee Rabaah, Ikrimah Mawlah Ibn Abbaas, Amr Ibn Deenar al-Makkee, Qaasim Ibn Muhammad Ibn Abee Bakr as-Siddeeq, Mujahid Ibn Jabr, Masrooq and Abu Burdah Ibn Abee Musa al-Ash’aree and other than them. Rahimahullah. 

Virtues: 

1. The Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhee Was-Sallam) gave Abdullah Ibn Amr (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) permission to write his hadeeth. (Sunan Abee Dawood: (no.3646) and its chain is authentic, Musnad Ahmad (2/162) 

2. Aboo Hurayrah (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) said, “There is none among the companions of the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhee Was-Sallam) who has narrated more Hadiths than I, except `Abdullah bin `Amr (bin Al-`As) who used to write them and I did not.” (Saheeh Bukharee (no.113) His written collection is famously known as as-Saheefah as-Saadiqah from the chain of Amr Ibn Shuayb from his father from his grandfather. (Also see al-Muwatta, Ibn al-Qaasim’s narration with my verification (pg.49-50) 

3. Handhalah Ibn Khuwaylid al-Anmbaree (al-Ghazzee/Thiqah) narrated: “I was with Mu’awiyah (Radiullahu anhu) and two men came arguing about the (cutting off the) head of Ammaar (Ibn Yassaar (Radiullahu anhu), both of these men were claiming to have killed him. Then Abdullah Ibn Amr (Radiullahu anhu) said, “Whichever one of you wants to be happy, then be happy (!) because I heard the Messenger of Allah (Sallalaahu Alayhee wa sallam) say, “a rebel (rebellious, insurgent) group will kill him (Ammaar (Radiullahu anhu).” Mu’awiyah (Radiullahu anhu) said, “Why are you with us (in the battle of Sifin)?”  He (Abdullah Ibn Amr (Radiullahu anhu) replied, “My father complained to the Messenger of Allah (Sallalaahu Alayhee wa sallam) about me, so he said (to me), “whilst your father is alive then obey (listen and follow him) and don’t disobey him”. So (that’s why) I am with you and I don’t kill and fight.” (Musnad al-Imaam Ahmad (2/164-165) hadeeth (no.6538) and its chain is authentic). Abdullah Ibn Amr Ibn al-Aas (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) said, “What is my connection with Sifin and what is my connection with killing and fighting the muslims? I wish that I died 10 years ago. By Allah! I didn’t strike a sword in this (battle), nor throw a spear and neither did I shoot an arrow. (Tabaqaat Ibn Sa’d (4/266) and its chain is authentic)    

Knowledge of narrations: 

The number of his narrations is 700 from which 7 are in the in the Saheehayn (TN. Saheehayn means the two Saheehs i.e. Saheeh Bukharee and Saheeh Muslim). 

Death:  

At the incident (event) of Hirrah, Dhul Hijjah 63 Hijri (RadhiAllaahu Anhu)   

There is a major difference of opinion as to when he died and the writer of this book has given preference the saying of Imaam Ahmad Ibn Hanbal. And Allah knows best.

Abu Khuzaimah Ansari

 

The dar ut tahqiq website have published a few articles on the proficiency of Imām Abū Ḥanīfah in Ḥadīth, sure they have the prerogative as they are his ardent, muʿtassub and bigoted blind followers. This is a brief response to it.

As the readers knows, we are currently publishing Shaikh Irshad ul-Ḥaqq al-Athari’s book ‘Asbāb Ikhtilāf al-Fuqahā’ which is an answer to Shaikh Muḥammad Awwamahs ‘Athar al-Ḥadīth Sharīf.’ Whilst translating this many years ago, at the time I added some detailed notes to the proceeding narration which is what you will be reading insha’Allāh.

Therefore, this narration will be uploaded again without the notes in the series of the book.

Shaikh Irshād ul-Ḥaqq al-Atharī said,

Imām Abū Ḥanīfah’s own statement has been transmitted from him by his student, Imām Abū ʿAbdur Rahmān ʿAbdullāh bin Yazīd Muqrī who said,

“I heard Abū Ḥanīfah say the Ahadīth he generally narrates are incorrect.” [Tārīkh Baghdād [13:402][1], [al-Kāmil [7:2473][2]

The chain of this statement from the Imām is authentic.

Footnotes

[Khaṭīb Baghdādī transmitted this statement with the following chain, “I was informed by Ibn al-Faḍal who was informed by Daʿlaj bin Aḥmad who was informed by Aḥmad bin ʿAlī al-Ābār and it was narrated to them by Maḥmūd bin Ghīlān and Ibn Muqrī narrated to them saying he heard Abū Ḥanīfah say….”

All the narrators are trustworthy and upright.

Ibn al-Muqrī is Muḥammad bin ʿAbdullāh bin Yazīd. Hafiẓ Khalīlī said, “Trustworthy with agreement.” [Tahdhīb [9:284], Taqrīb [pg.456][3]

Maḥmūd bin Ghīlān is also trustworthy and a preserver, he is also a narrator of Ṣaḥīḥ Bukhārī and Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim [Tahdhīb [10:64], [Sīyar ʿAlām an-Nabulā [12:223][4], [Tadhkirah ul-Ḥuffāz [2:475][5]

Aḥmad bin ʿAlī al-Ābār is also a trustworthy Imām and a preserver ie Ḥāfiẓ. [Tārīkh Baghdād [4:306], Sīyar [13:443] [Tadhkirah ul-Ḥuffāz [2:639][6]

Dʿalaj bin Aḥmad is a well known trustworthy scholar of Ḥadīth and a jurist. Imām Ḥākim said he was an Imām of the Ahlul Ḥadīth of his time. [Sīyar [16:30][7], Tārīkh Baghdād [8:387][8], Tadhkirah ul-Ḥuffāẓ [3:881][9]

Ibn al-Faḍal is Muḥammad bin al-Ḥussain bin Muḥammad bin al-Faḍal al-Baghdādī al-Qaṭṭān. He is a well known teacher of Imām Baihaqī and Imām Khaṭīb Baghdādī. Imām Dhahabī said that he was trustworthy with agreement. [Siyār [17:331],[10] Tārīkh Baghdād [2:246][11] others. 

Thus, the narrators of this chain are all trustworthy; however, Ibn al-Muqrī hearing from Imām Abū Ḥanīfah is debatable. Imām Ibn ʿAdiyy has transmitted the same statement from ʿAbdullāh bin ʿAbdul ʿAzīz via the chain of Maḥmūd bin Ghīlān from al-Muqrī. In fact Imām Tirmidhī has also transmitted it from Maḥmūd bin Ghīlān and said he heard it from al-Muqrī [al-ʿEllal al-Kabīr [2:966].

What this means is that Maḥmūd bin Ghīlān did not narrate from Ibn al-Muqrī but rather from his father whose name is ʿAbdullāh bin Yazīd al-Muqrī. As for ʿAbdullāh bin Yazīd he is the famous Muhaddith, Imām Bukhārī’s teacher as well as a trustworthy Imām. [Tahdhīb [6:83][12], Sīyar [10:166][13]

Imām Ibn ʿAdiyy also transmitted the same statement via Aḥmad bin Muḥammad bin Saʿīd from Muḥammad bin ʿAbdullāh bin Suleimān from Salamah bin Shabīb from al-Muqrī-and this chain is also Ḥasan. Salamah bin Shabīb al-Nīsābūrī is trustworthy [Tahdhīb [4:146][14] and others. Also note Al-Kāmil mentions Maslamatah which is incorrect and needs to be corrected.

Muḥammad bin ʿAbdullāh bin Suleimān is the famous trustworthy scholar of Ḥadīth from Kūfa. [Sīyar [14:41] and others. Aḥmad bin Muḥammad bin Saʿīd is Ibn ʿUqadah who is a famous Hāfiẓ of Ḥadīth however, some have spoken against him whereas others declared him to be trustworthy. [Sīyar [15:340], Tārīkh Baghdād [5:14], Līsān [1:263][15] and others.

However he is not alone in this narration, nonetheless this narration is authentically established from Imām al-Muqrī and Allah knows best.)
