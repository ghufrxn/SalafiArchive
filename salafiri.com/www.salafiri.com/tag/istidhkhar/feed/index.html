The Duha Prayer is Recommended

[37] And with the same chain of narration that “I never once saw the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) doing the voluntary prayer of duha (all the time), and I understand this prayer to be recommended. Sometimes the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) would refrain from a practice that he loved to do, fearing that people would do the same and it would become fard (obligatory) for them.”

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri clarified hearing (this hadith from Urwa Ibn az-Zubayr) in Ahmad (6/86 hadith (no.24559))

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 1/152,153 hadith no.357, book 9, chapter 8, hadith no.29 and with it (the words): and I pray this prayer) at-Tamhid (8/134), al-Istidhkar: (no.127)   

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.1128) and Muslim (no.718) from the hadith of Malik with the same meaning.

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]

1) In one narration A’isha (Radi Allahu Anha) was asked, “Did the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) pray the duha prayer? And she replied, “No, except when he came back from a journey (then he prayed it) Sahih Muslim (no.717) In another narration A’isha (Radi Allahu Anha) said, “The Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) prayed four rak’ahs or more for the duha prayer. (Sahih Muslim: (no.719)

It is known (from this) that according to A’isha (Radi Allahu Anha) The Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) prayed the duha prayer, but he didn’t always pray it. See Sunan al-Kubra of al-Bayhaqi (3/49)

2) The duha prayer is not wajib (obligatory) or sunnah mu’akidah rather it is recommended [mustahab] and more virtuous (if it is prayed then left out). [Trans Note: Salatul Duha should not be prayed everyday as their is no clear evidence for this]

3) The duha prayer is 2, 4 or 8 rakas. Sahih Muslim (no.720 and no.721, no.719) Sahih Bukhari: (no.1176), and Sahih Muslim: (no.336) after hadith (no.719) it is sunnah to perform the salaam after every 2 rakas.

4) The Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) commanded Abu Hurayrah (Radi Allahu Anhu) and Abu ad-Darda (Radi Allahu Anhu) to pray the duha prayer. (Sahih Muslim: (no.721, no.722))

5) The time for the duha prayer starts straight after sunrise and its most virtuous time (to pray it in) is when the feet of the female camel’s offspring become hot (feel the heat of the sun) (because of the sunshine). (Sahih Muslim: no.748)) This prayed is also called Salatul Awabin (the prayer of those who repent a lot). This time is approximately until the fourth part of the beginning of the day.

6) The summary of the blessed Prophet’s (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) saying is that whosoever does the ablution and leaves to the house to perform the compulsory prayer (in the masjid) then he receives the reward of Hajj and whosoever goes to pray the duha prayer then he receives the reward of Umra. Sunan Abu Dawud (no.558) and its chain is good [Hasan], and Musnad Ahmad (5/268) and All praise is due to Allah.

The Virtue of Saying Amin In the Prayer and Amin Loudly

Sa’id and Abu Salamah: Two Ahadith

[18] And with the same chain of narration that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) said, “When the Imam says ‘Amin’, say ‘Amin’, for the one whose ‘Amin’ coincides with the ‘Amin’ of the angels – his previous wrong actions are forgiven.” 

Ibn Shihab said, “The Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) used to say ‘Amin’ (extending it).”

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]
Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri clarified hearing this hadith (from Sa’id Ibn Musayab and Abu Salamah Ibn Abdirahman) here (in this hadith by saying, “He reported to me…” [Akhbaraahu])

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]
Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (1/87 hadith no.191, book 3, chapter 11, hadith (2/44), at-Tamhid (7/8) and al-Istidhkar (no.167)

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.780) and Muslim (no.410) from the hadith of Malik.

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]

1) Imam Abul Abbas Siraj reported from Imam Zuhri with an authentic chain that, “When the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) prayed ‘Wa Ladhalin’ then he would say Amin loudly” (hadith as-Siraj, written by me (pg.35))

2) The noble muhadithin have used this hadith to establish the issue of reciting Amin loudly. See Sahih Bukhari (no.780) and Sahih Ibn Khuzaimah ((1/286) hadith (no.570)) and Sunan Ibn Majah (no.852) and Sunan an-Nasa’i ((2/144) hadith (no.929))

3) Wail Ibn Hujr (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) narrated that he prayed behind the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) and he prayed Amin loudly. (Sunan Abu Dawud: (no.933), al-Khilafiyat of Bayhaqi, written by me (pg.1/51) and its chain is [Hasan] good)
Putting this type of narration into consideration Imam Muslim says: “All the narrations that the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) said Amin loudly are mutawatir” (book of tamyiz written by me (pg.9), published (pg.40))

4) The narration where Imam Shu’ba mentioned Amin (being said) quietly is weak according to the noble muhadithin because of it being [Shadh] strange. Even if this narration was authentic then this only meant that in the quiet prayer Amin should be said quietly. Remember that there is an ijma to say Amin quietly in the quiet prayers.

5) The companions and tabi’in (in the loud prayers) used to say Amin loudly. See Sahih Bukhari (before hadith (no.780)) and Mussanaf Ibn Abi Shaybah ((2/425) hadith (no.7963) and its chain is [Hasan] good)
It is the action of the Jews being jealous of the salam and Amin. See Sunan Ibn Majah ((no.856) and its chain is authentic and Ibn Khuzaimah authenticated it: (no.1585), and Busiri in Zawa’id Ibn Majah, and Mundhiri in Targhib Wa Tarhib (1/328)) also see my book “al-Qawlul Matin Fi Jarh Bita’min”

6) Some people take from this hadith this issue that, “The Angels say Amin quietly because their voice is not heard therefore Amin should be said quietly”. So we stress and request the question that do you see or hear other actions of the Angels? and by not hearing Amin you result in giving a flawed fatwa that Amin should be said quietly? This is just equivalent to ‘resorting to sinking straws’ (meaning trying to achieve something that can’t be done, straws float and can’t sink)

7) The virtue of praying Amin in the prayer is a means of forgiveness.

8) From this hadith it is established that if the latecomer has recited a part of Surah Fatiha or is going to start reciting Surah Fatiha, and the Imam says Amin then he (the latecomer) should also say Amin and finish reciting Surah Fatiha after. Now if he has reached his Wa Ladhalin and the Imam is reciting (another surah) after saying Amin then he will not say Amin rather he will stay quiet just how it is established from other general proofs.

The Importance of Controlling and Overcoming Anger

[17] And with the same chain of narration that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) said, “A strong person is not the person who throws his adversaries to the ground. A strong person is the person who contains himself when he is angry.”          

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

Transmitted by Muslim (108/2609) from the hadith of Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri who said, “Humayd Ibn Abdurahman reported to me that Abu Hurayrah (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) said:” with the same and its chain is authentic.

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (2/906 hadith no.1746, book 47, chapter 3, hadith no.12) at-Tamhid (6/321) and al-Istidhkar (no.1678)
Transmitted by Bukhari (no.6114) and Muslim (no.2609) from the hadith of Malik.

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]

1) As-Sura’atu: in the language it means a person who is a very capable wrestler (subduer), a powerful (tremendous, great, mighty) wrestler (athlete) and being dominant (able to prevail). See al-Qamus ul-Wahid (pg.921)

As-Sur’atu: is said to a person who is subdued (wrestled), weak wrestler and who is dominated.

2) It is more virtuous to have patience, tolerance and struggle against your own self in comparison to fighting against your personal enemy. At-Tamhid (6/323).

3) Getting angry without a valid excuse legislated in the Shari’ah is not a likeable action to do

The Virtue of Praying in Congregation

Sa’id Ibn Musayab: Seven Ahadith

[11] From Malik from Ibn Shihab from Sa’id Ibn al-Musayyab from Abu Hurayrah (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) said, “Prayer in congregation is better than the prayer of one of you on his own by twenty-five parts.”

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]
Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri said, “Sa’id Ibn Musayab and Abu Salamah Ibn Abdurahman reported to me that Abu Hurayrah (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) said: “etc Transmitted by Bukhari (no.648)

[REFERENCES] – [MUSLIM]
Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (1/129 hadith no.287, book 8, chapter 1, hadith no.2) at-Tamhid (6/316) and al-Istidhkar (no.256)

Transmitted by Muslim (no.649) from the hadith of Malik. And narrated in Bukhari (no.648) hadith of az-Zuhri from Sa’id Ibn Musayab and Abu Salamah from Abu Hurayrah (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) with the same lengthy meaning.

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) The larger the amount of people in the congregational prayer of the Muslims, with the correct beliefs, the more virtuous it is. The Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) said, “And his prayer with two men is better than his prayer with one, but if there are more it is more pleasing to Allah, the Almighty, the Majestic.” (Musnad Ahmad (5/140), and its chain is [Hasan] good, Sunan Abu Dawood: (no.554) and Ibn Khuzaimah authenticated it: (no.1477), and Ibn Hibban, al-Mawarid: (no.429) and there is another hadith from Ibn Majah: (no.790) and others and its chain is [Hasan] good)

Note: the jarh i.e. criticism of Hafidh Ibn Abdul Barr on this hadeeth is rejected

2) A person’s prayer on his own and not in congregation is accepted, however praying in congregation is more virtuous.

3) In some narrations the reward (for praying in congregation is) 27 times greater. There is no contradiction, rather every person will be rewarded in accordance to their intention, sincerity, following the Sunnah and best actions – if Allah wills

It is not allowed to go to an area where there is a plague and to run away from it (if you are there when the plague breaks out)

Abdullah Ibn Amir Ibn Rabia al-Adawi: One hadith

 

[9] From Malik from Ibn Shihab from Abdullah Ibn Amir Ibn Rabia that Umar Ibn al-Khattab (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) went out to ash-Sham. When he came to Sargh, near Tabuk, he heard that the plague had broken out in ash-Sham. Abd ar-Rahman Ibn Awf (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) told him that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) said, “If you hear that a land has a plague in it, do not go forward to it. If it comes upon a land which you are in, do not depart in flight from it.” So Umar Ibn al-Khattab (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) came back from Sargh.

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]
Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri clarified hearing this hadith with al-Qadhi Ahmad Ibn Mohammed Ibn Esa al-Burti in the Musnad of Abdurahman Ibn Awf (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) (no.6) and its chain is [Hasan] good

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]
Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (2/896,897 hadith no.1722, book 45, chapter 7, hadith no.24) at-Tamhid (6/210) and al-Istidhkar (no.1654)

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.5730, no.6973) and Muslim (no.2219) from the hadith of Malik

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADEETH]
1) Salim Ibn Abdullah Ibn Umar (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) narrated that because of the hadith of Abdurahman Ibn Awf (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) Umar (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) returned back. (Sahih Bukhari: (no.6973) and Sahih Muslim: (100/2219) this is the one of the most highest examples of following of the Sunnah.

2) Khabr Wahid is a proof with the condition that the person narrating is reliable and trustworthy.

3) If a person becomes victim to a plague the area they are in, it is possible the person’s belief can become corrupt. Perhaps this is the reason why it is forbidden to go to the area where there is a plague or to leave that area (if you are there when the plague breaks out).

4) This is an example of the love the companions (RadhiAllaahu Anhum) had for the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam)
