It Is Not Allowed To Blow On A Drink

Ayub Ibn Habib: One Hadith

[131] From Malik from Ayub Ibn Habib Mawla of Sa’d Ibn Abi Waqas from Abil Muthana al-Juhani that he said, I was present with Marwan Ibn Hakam whilst Abu Sa’id al-Khudri (Radi Allahu Anhu) entered so Marwan Ibn Hakam asked him, Have you heard from the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) that it is prohibited to blow on drinks (water etc.)? So Abu Sa’id al-Khudri (Radi Allahu Anhu) said, Yes. A man said: O Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) I can not drink in one breath (as I still stay thirsty). He (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said: “Then remove the cup away from your mouth.” He said, (What about if) I see something floating in it (i.e. in the drink?) He (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said: “Then spill it out.”

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

[REFERENCES]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (2/925 hadith no.1783, book 49, chapter 7, hadith no.12), at-Tamhid (1/391) al-Istidhkar (no.1715)

And transmitted by at-Tirmidhi (no.1887, and he said: [Hasan Sahih]), and Ibn Hibban (al-Mawarid no.1367), and al-Hakim (4/139) all of them from the hadith of Malik. 

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) From this hadith it is known that it is prohibited to blow on a hot drink such as milk, tea etc. in order to cool it down. Likewise it is prohibited to blow on the drink to remove straws etc. 

2) If there is a Sharia compliant excuse then a scholar can go to the leader on the condition that the scholar says the word of truth and does not go for any personal selfish reason.

3) It is allowed to drink water in one gulp (breathe) however it is better to drink it in three gulps (breathes). 

4) This hadith is an excellent refutation of those people who blow on water bottles or dishes etc. for cures or blessings.

5) If an individual does not know about an issue then he should ask the scholar, and the scholar should give the answer with proofs rather it is better to reference the proofs when giving the answer. 

6) One should inspect the water etc. before drinking it.

Eating etc. Should be Done With the Right Hand

Abu Bakr Ibn Ubaydullah: One Hadith. And it contains Itisal and some further examination (looking into, investigation, scrutinizing)

[62] From Malik from Ibn Shihab from Abu Bakr Ibn Ubaydullah Ibn Abdullah Ibn Umar from Abdullah Ibn Umar (RadiAllahu Anhu) that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) said, “When you eat, eat with your right hand and (when you drink then) drink with your right hand. Shaytan eats with his left hand and drinks with his left hand.”

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

Humaydi said in his Musnad (no.635), “Sufyan narrated to us: az-Zuhri narrated to us: Abu Bakr Ibn Ubaydullah Ibn Abdullah Ibn Umar reported to me that he heard his grandfather Abdullah Ibn Umar say: “….” To the end of the hadith and its chain is [Sahih] authentic.

It is known from this that that this chain of narration is [Mutasil] connected and (because of) its itisal (being [Mutasil] connected) further verification is not needed.

[REFERENCES] – [MUSLIM]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 2/922, 923 hadith no.1777, book 49, chapter 4, hadith no.6) at-Tamhid (11/109) al-Istidhkar: (no.1709)          

Transmitted by Muslim (no.2020) from the hadith of Malik.

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]

1) It is known from this that it is prohibited to eat (and drink) with the left hand without a valid excuse legislated in the Shari’ah. The Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) has prohibited from eating (and drinking) with the left hand and walking in one shoe. See hadith (no.104), and Sahih Muslim (70/2099)

2) It is necessary to focus on prioritising the etiquettes and mannerisms of the Shar’iah in eating, drinking, and all the worldly affairs.

3) The Shayatin meaning the Jinn, eat and drink.

4) Eating and drinking with the left hand without any valid excuse is the way of Shaytan.
