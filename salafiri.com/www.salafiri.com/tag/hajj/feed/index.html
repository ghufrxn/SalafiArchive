Performing Sa’e between Safa and Marwa

[146] From Malik from Jafar ibn Muhammad from his father from Jabir ibn Abdullah that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam), walked when he came down from Safa and Marwa and then, when he reached the middle of the valley, he broke into a light run until he had left it.

[GRADING] – [IT’S CHAIN IS SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] 

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 1/374, 375 hadith no.851, book 20, chapter 42, hadith no.131), at-Tamhid (2/93), al-Istidhkar: (no.799)

Transmitted by Nasa’i (5/243 hadith no.2984) from the hadith of Abdurahman Ibn al-Qasim from Malik

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) An individual should walk between Safa and Marwa when doing Sa’e and if an individual has a sharia compliant excuse such as an illness then it is allowed for that individual to perform Sa’e on a wheelchair, being carried on their companions shoulders or lying down on a bed. 

2) If an individual performing Sa’e assists and carries another person to perform Sa’e for a wage or for free, then the Sa’e of the individual who provides the help and support is valid and this is the same ruling for Tawaf also. 

3) The Sa’e should be performed after performing the Tawaf of the House of Allah therefore doing Tawaf first and then doing the Sa’e is incorrect.

An Individual Should Sacrifice The Sacrificial Animal Himself 

[145] From Malik from Jafar ibn Muhammed ibn AIi from his father from Jabir ibn Abdullah (Radi Allahu Anhu) that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) sacrificed some of his sacrificial animals himself, and someone other than him sacrificed the rest.

[GRADING] – [IT’S CHAIN IS SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] 

Al-Muwatta (Abi Mus’ab az-Zuhri’s narration (1/534 hadith no.1381)

Transmitted by an-Nasa’i (7/231 hadith no.4424) from the hadith of Malik. And in Yahya Ibn Yahya’s narration (1/394 hadith no.909) “Malik from Ja’far from his father from Ali Ibn Abi Talib” and it is incorrect. See at-Tamhid (2/106) and al-Istidhkar (no.849)  

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) It is better and more virtuous for an individual to carry out the sacrifice himself. It is also allowed to appoint someone else to carry out the sacrifice on his behalf. 

2) It is allowed to appoint a Wakil.

3) The meaning of “other than him” in the hadith is Ali Ibn Abi Talib (Radi Allahu Anhu) just as it is established from other narrations.

Dua At Safa And Marwa

[144] From Malik from Jafar ibn Muhammed ibn AIi from his father from Jabir ibn Abdullah (Radi Allahu Anhu) that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) used to say (the takbir), “Allah is the Greatest” three times when he stopped on Safa, and “There is no diety worthy of worship in truth except Allah, alone, without any partner. To Him belong the Kingdom and praise, and He has power over everything” three times, and make dua. He would then do the same on Marwa.

[GRADING] – [IT’S CHAIN IS SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] 

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (1/372 hadith no.847, book no.20, chapter no.41, hadith no.127), at-Tamhid (2/91), al-Istidhkar no.795

Transmitted by an-Nasa’i (5/240 hadith no.2975) from the hadith of Ibn ul-Qasim from Malik and narrated by Muslim (no.1218) from the hadith of Jafar Ibn Muhamad. 

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) One should go on Safa and Marwa whilst being able to see the house of Allah (Ka’bah) as this is the sunnah. 

2) There are seven circuits (laps) whilst doing the Safa and Marwa Sa’i. 

(1) From Safa to Marwa (2) From Marwa to Safa (3) From Safa to Marwa (4) From Marwa to Safa (5) From Safa to Marwa (6) From Marwa to Safa (7) From Safa to Marwa 

The seventh circuit is the last one. After this the person who is performing Umrah can cut or shave their hair and take off their Ihram. The person performing Hajj Ifrad or Hajj Qiran becomes free from the restrictions of Ihram after throwing the pebbles at the Jamarat and then performing the Qurbani or shaving the hair. 

3) Abdullah ibn Umar (Radi Allahu Anhu) used to make dua on Safa saying, “O Allah, You have said, ‘call on Me – I will answer you’ and You do not break Your promise. So I am asking You, in the same way that You have guided me to Islam, not to take it away from me, and that You make me die while I am muslim.”(Muwatta Imam Malik 1/373 hadith no.848 and it’s chain is [Sahih] authentic). From this it is known that making other duas at Safa and Marwa is allowed.

It Is Necessary To Keep A Sequence (Order) In Hajj

[143] From Malik from Jafar ibn Muhammad Ibn Ali from his father that Jabir ibn Abdullah (Radi Allahu Anhu) said, “I heard the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) say as he left the Masjid, intending to go to Safa, ‘We begin with that with which Allah began,’

[GRADING] – [IT’S CHAIN IS SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] 

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 1/372 hadith no.846, book 20, chapter 41, hadith no.126), at-Tamhid (2/79, al-Istidhkar: (no.794)

Transmitted by Nasa’i (5/239 hadith no.2972) from the hadith of Ibn al-Qasim from Malik and narrated by Muslim (no.1218) from the hadith of Ja’far Ibn Muhammed with another wording

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) It is possible that ‘waw’ is not used to denote a sequence however where it is established that there is a sequence or structure in the actions or sayings of the noble Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) then at that point (following that) sequence is necessary. For example if someone starts the Sa’i at Marwa then his Sa’i will be invalid and he will have to repeat his Sa’i correctly.

2) If someone performs ablution backwards (i.e. washes the feet first then head etc.) then his ablution is invalid because this way of performing an ablution has not been established from the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) or the companions (Radi Allahu Anhum) therefore he must perform the ablution again properly (According to the sunnah). If he performs salah with a backwards ablution then he has to make up that salah (after performing the correct ablution). Also see hadith no.144 and no.146

3) The sequences or structures (of Islamic acts of worship) mentioned in the Quran and Sunnah are not devoid of wisdom. One should take them into consideration.

The Tawaf Will Begin From The Hajri Aswad (Black Stone)

[142] From Malik from Jafar ibn Muhammad from his father that Jabir ibn Abdullah (Radi Allahu Anhu) said, “I saw the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) hastening from the Black Stone until he reached it again, three times.”

[GRADING] – [IT’S CHAIN IS SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] – [MUSLIM]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 1/364 hadith no.827, book 20, chapter 34, hadith no.107), at-Tamhid (2/68), al-Istidhkar: (no.775)

Transmitted by Muslim (235/1263) from the hadeeth of Maalik

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) There is a consensus that the Tawaf starts from the black stone. Then you go anti-clockwise with the maqam of Ibrahim on your right hand side and the Ka’bah on your left hand side. It is sunnah to run on the first three circuits and then to walk in the remaining four circuits.

2) It is from the sunnah to kiss, touch or say Bismillah Allahu Akbar and points towards the black stone.

3) It is not allowed to perform the tawaf backwards or clockwise.

4) During the farewell Hajj the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) performed Ramal in the first three circuits, meaning that he walked really fast almost like running (brisk walking) and at that time none of the polytheists from Makkah were present therefore Ramal is sunnah until the day of Judgement.

5) There is a consensus that the women will not perform Ramal during the Tawaf rather they will walk normally in all seven circuits of Tawaf.

6) If an individual is not able to perform Ramal then no dam (compensation) is obligatory for them to do and if they are unable to do Ramal because of not having enough free space then there is no sin upon them.

7) The Ramal starts from the black stone and finishes at the black stone (i.e. one full lap). The Ramal (from the black stone) till the Yemeni corner is for the weak only.
