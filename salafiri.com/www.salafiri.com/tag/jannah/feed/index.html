The Virtue Of The Area Which Is Between The House Of The Messenger Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) And The Minbar

One Chapter Of al-Kha
Khubaib Ibn Abdirahman: He has two ahadith  

[154] From Malik from Khubaib Ibn Abdirahman from Hafs Ibn Asim from Abu Hurairah (Radi Allahu Anhu) or Abu Sa’id al-Khudri (Radi Allahu Anhu) that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, “(The land) Between my house and my minbar is a garden from one of the gardens of Jannah, and my minbar is over my Lake-Tank. (Kauthar);

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] 
Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (1/197, hadith no.464, book 14, chapter 5, hadith no.10), at-Tamhid (2/285) and al-Istidhkar (no.433) 

Transmitted by Ahmad (2/465, 533) from the hadith of Malik with the same meaning and narrated by al-Bukhari (no.7335) from the hadith of Malik which is from Abu Hurairah (Radi Allahu Anhu) without any doubt. 

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) Some people say that the meaning of Jannah in this hadith is that this area will be put into Jannah on the Day of Judgement and some other people say that this is where the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayi Wa Sallam) used to live. The people used to learn about the Quran, Iman and things about the religion from the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) and those who act upon this will end up reaching Jannah. Therefore this has been metaphorically called a garden from one of the gardens of Jannah just like it has been mentioned in a hadith that Jannah is under the shades of swords (Bukhari no.2818). Meaning that the sincere believer who fights in the path of Allah will go to Jannah. See at-Tamhid (2/287) and Allah knows best.   

2) Some people use this hadith to try and establish that Madinah Munawarah is more virtuous than Makkah Mukaramah however this deduction is incorrect and neither have the pious predecessors understood this. In a hadith the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) declared that Makkah Mukaramah is the best part of Allah’s Earth. See Sunan Ibn Majah (no.3108, and it’s chain is Sahih and at-Tirmidhi authenticated it no.3925, and al-Hakim on the conditions of the two Shaikhs 3/7 and adh-Dhahabi agreed with him) and at-Tamhid (2/288). No one has the right to talk against this issue after the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) has made a decision. 

3) If a narrator of a narration has a doubt on which thiqah narrator he specified then this is not harmful rather this narration is Sahih on the condition that the rest of the chain is also Sahih. This is because all the companions are classified as being Udul (i.e. all of them are thiqah) therefore the exact companion not being specified in this chain does not cause it to be harmful. wal-Hamdullilah 

4) The Hawd Kawthar is a true reality which Allah specifically gave to His beloved (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam). Ibn Abdul Bar said that the Hawd Kawthar has been mentioned in the mutawatir hadith of the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam). Ahlus Sunnah and the people of truth believe in this and affirm it. Similarly they believe in the hadith which mention the intercession (on the day of judgement) and the punishment of the grave and they affirm this. (at-Tamhid 2/309)

The Soul of a Believer After Dying Will be in Paradise in the Belly of a Bird

Abdurahman Ibn Ka’b Ibn Malik: One Hadith

[72] From Malik from Ibn Shihab that Abd ar-Rahman Ibn Ka’b Ibn Malik al-Ansari told him that his father, Ka’b Ibn Malik, used to relate that the Messenger of Allah (Sallahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, “The Soul of the Mu’min is (in the form of) a bird that sits in the trees of the Garden (Paradise) until Allah returns it to his body on the day He raises him.”

[GRADING] – [SANADAHU SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] – 

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 1/240 hadith no.569, book 16, chapter 16, hadith no.49), at-Tamhid (11/56), al-Istidhkar: (no.523)    

Transmitted by Nasa’i (4/108, hadith no.2075) from the hadith of Malik and its chain is [Sahih] authentic and it has an accusation in it without a qadiha. And the hadith is authentic with Tirmidhi (no.1641) and Ibn Hibban (no.734) and it has a witness with Ahmad (6/424,425) etc.  

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]

1) There is a difference of opinion in the chains of this hadith however this chain is authentic.

2) As the person who is alive flies in the air by using the Aeroplane (perhaps) just like this the soul flies in the air in the form of a bird in Jannah.

It is transmitted in a hadith that, “The souls, of the martyrs live in the bellies of green birds who have their nests in chandeliers hung from the Throne of the Almighty. They eat the fruits of Paradise from wherever they like and then nestle in these chandeliers.” (Sahih Muslim no.1887 [no.4885])

3) Saying that “the dead person receives another body” is completely without proof and is not established from the Salaf al-Salihin.

4) After dying the Barzakh life is true, however we are not conscious of it in this life.

5) The soul does not return to the dead persons body except during the time of questioning in the grave and on the day of judgement the soul will return to the body.

6) Jannah has beautiful trees and lots of fruits.

7) Those who say that “the martyrs and righteous come back into this world after death in bodily form or souls” this saying is without any proof and is false.

8) The commentator of the Quran and Tabi’i Qatadah Ibn Da’amah said, “We have been informed that the souls of the martyrs come in the form of white birds (in Jannah) and eat the fruits of Jannah” (Tafsir Abdur Razaq no.481 and its chain is [Sahih] authentic)

The Virtue of the Person Who Fasts

[31] And with the same chain of narration from Abu Hurayrah the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) said, “Whoever hands over two of any type of property in the way of Allah is called to the Garden, with the words ‘O slave of Allah! This is good!’ Whoever is among the people of prayer, is called from the gate of prayer. Whoever is among the people of jihad is called from the gate of jihad. Whoever is among the people of sadaqa, is called from the gate of sadaqa. Whoever is among the people of fasting, is called from the gate of the well-watered. (Bab ar-Rayan).” Abu Bakr as-Sidiq (Radi Allahu Anhu) asked, “Whoever gets called from any of these gates then he does not need anything else after this (meaning he is guaranteed to be paradise and being successful). Can someone be called from all of these gates?” He (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, “Yes, and I hope you are among them.”

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri clarified hearing (this hadith from Humayd Ibn Abd ar-Rahman Ibn Awf) in Bukhari (no.3666)

[REFERENCES] – 

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 2/469 hadith no.1036, book 21, chapter 19, hadith no.49) at-Tamhid (7/183), al-Istidhkar: (no.973)

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.1897) from the hadith of Malik and narrated in Muslim (no.1027) from the hadith of Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri.

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]

1) The first khalifah without exception is Abu Bakr As-Sidiq (Radi Allahu Anhu) who was the most virtuous, that he will be called to enter Janah from all of its doors (gates).

2) There is a specific gate established for the fasting person due to its virtue.

3) Janah has many (8) gates.

4) It is necessary to have correct beliefs, righteous actions and Allah’s Grace and Mercy in order to enter Janah.

5) One should offer something extremely valuable as an offering to Allah in order to seek his pleasure.

6) One should continuously endeavor to do righteous actions.

7) This Hadith proves the virtue of Struggling (Jihad) in the path of Allah and giving charity.

The Virtue of the Person Whose 2 or 3 Children Die

[15] And with the same chain of narration that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) said, “No Muslim who has three children who die, will be touched by the Fire except to fulfill Allah’s oath.”

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri clarified hearing (this hadith from Sa’id Ibn Musayab) in Humaydi (with my verification: (no.1026) and the manuscript of A’dhami: (no.1020))

[REFERENCES] – [BUKHARI]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (1/235 hadith no.557, book 16, chapter 13, hadith no.38) at-Tamhid (6/346) and al-Istidhkar (no.511)

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.6656) and Muslim (no.2632) from the hadith of Malik.

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) A huge reward is given from the mercy and nobility of Allah the most High because of the patience a Muslim endures.

2) Muslim children (who have not reached the age of puberty) are in Jannah.

3) A companion’s (RadhiAllahu Anhu) child died so the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) said to him: “Will it not make you happy to know that you will not come to any of the gates of Paradise but you will find him there, trying to open it for you?” (Musnad Ali Ibn al-Jad: (no.1075), and its chain is [Sahih] authentic, Musnad Ahmad (3/436), (5/34,35), Sunan an-Nasa’i (4/22) hadith (no.1871), (4/118) hadith (no.2090)

It is known (from this), the parents who have patience on their child’s death will have them open the door of Jannah for them on the day of judgement.

4) “Except to fulfil Allah’s oath” is an indication to the Qur’anic ayah, “And there is none of you except he will come to it.” [Surah Maryam ayah:71]. (Fath ul-Bari (11/661) hadith (no.6656), published by Dar us-Salam). For more details please refer back to the Tafsir of the mentioned ayah and the books of Tafsir.
