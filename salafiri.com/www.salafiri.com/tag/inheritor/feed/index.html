A Muslim Cannot be the Heir of a Disbeliever

Ali Ibn al-Hasan: One Hadith

[65] From Malik from Ibn Shihab from Ali Ibn Husayn Ibn Ali Ibn Abi Talib from Amr Ibn Uthman Ibn Afan from Usamah Ibn Zayd that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, “A Muslim cannot be the heir of a disbeliever”

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri clarified hearing (this hadith from Ali Ibn Husayn) in al-Humaydi (with my verification no.542) and Ibn Majah (no.2730)

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 2/519 hadith no.1127, book 27, chapter 13, hadith no.10) and with it “Umar Ibn Uthman” and it is a mistake, the most correct is “Amr Ibn Uthman Ibn Afan” at-Tamhid (9/160) al-Istidhkar: (no.1051)              

Transmitted by Nasa’i in al-Kubra (4/81 hadith no.6372 – 6375) from the hadith of Malik and transmitted by Bukhari (no.6764) and Muslim (no.1614) from the hadith of Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri.

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]

1) According to the majority of the companions, Tabi’in and those who came after them, a Muslim cannot be the heir (inherit from) of a disbeliever. See Sharh Sahih Muslim of An-Nawawi (2/33)

Jabir Ibn Abdullah al-Ansari (Radi Allahu Anhu) said, “A Muslim doesn’t inherit from a Jew or Christian and neither does a Christian or Jew inherit from a Muslim except that the person is his slave man or slave woman. (Musanaf Abdur Razzaq (6/18 hadith no.9865 and its chain is authentic, as-Sunan al-Kubra of al-Bayhaqi 6/218)

2) For more details concerning this issue see my article “The inheritance of the non-Muslims and the Mas’udiyah sect” (al-Hadith Journal: 30 pg no.43 to 46.

3) A summary of one of the sayings of Imam Malik is, that it is the ijma of our scholars that a Muslim does not inherit from a non-Muslim, whether they are related or if it is a direct family (heir) relation. See al-Muwatta (2/520 under hadith no.1131)

4) A high position trustworthy narrator can sometimes make a mistake just as the mistake which is in the published manuscript of Muwatta Imam Malik of the name of Amr Ibn Uthman. Leaving the mistaken part, this trustworthy narrator’s narrations will be all authentic and used as a proof.

5) There is an addition at the end of this narration in Sahih Bukhari (no.1764) and Sahih Muslim (no.1614 [no.4140] that says, “and a non-Muslim doesn’t inherit (can’t be the heir of) a Muslim”. It is clearer than the Sun that the trustworthy (narrator’s) addition is accepted and a proof.

The Description (Explanation) of a Life Pension

Jabir Ibn Abdullah: One hadith

[21] Malik related to me from Ibn Shihaab from Abu Salamah Ibn Abd ar-Rahman Ibn Awf from Jabir Ibn Abdullah al-Ansari that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) said, “If someone is given a life pension, for him and his posterity, it belongs to the person to whom it has been given. It never reverts to the one who gave it because he gave a gift and the rules of inheritance apply to it.”

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri clarified hearing (this hadith from Abu Salamah Ibn Abd ar-Rahman) in Nasa’i ((6/276) hadith no.3777)

[REFERENCES] – [MUSLIM]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (6/756 hadith no.1517, book 36, chapter 37, hadith no.43) at-Tamhid (7/112) and al-Istidhkar (no.1446)

Transmitted by Muslim (no.1625) from the hadith of Malik.

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]

1) Jabir (RadhiAllahu Anhu) narrated that the life pension which the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) permitted is where he says, “This is for you and for your inheritors” and if he says this, “This is for you for however long you live” so the life pension will return back to the person who gave it (after that person receiving the life pension dies). (Imam) Zuhri used to give fatwa in accordance to this. (Sahih Muslim: (23/1625), Dar us-Salam (numbering): (no.4191)

2) The mother of the believers Hafsa daughter of Umar Ibnul Khattab (RadhiAllahu Anha) gave a house to the son of Zayd Ibnul Khattab (RadhiAllahu Anhu) for his lifetime. When Zayd’s (RadhiAllahu Anhu) son died, Abdullah Ibn Umar (RadhiAllahu Anhu) took that house back. He understood that (because he was the inheritor of his sister) this house is his. (Muwatta Imam Malik (2/756) hadith (no.1519), and its chain is authentic)

3) Qasim Ibn Muhammad Ibn Abu Bakrah used to say that I have found the people on this saying that they used to restrict themselves on these terms and conditions for their wealth (property). (Muwatta Imam Malik (2/756) hadith (no.1518), and its chain is authentic)

4) Imam Malik used to say: we act on this in our place (Madinah) that the life pension returns back to the person who gave it on the condition that he didn’t say that ‘this is for you and for your inheritors’. (Muwatta Imam Malik (2/756) under hadith (no.1518))
