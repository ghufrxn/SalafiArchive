The Close Relatives Should Be Given Priority When Giving Charity

[116] From Malik from Ishaq Ibn Abdillah Ibn Abi Talha from Anas Ibn Malik (Radi Allahu Anhu) he said, “Abu Talha (Radi Allahu Anhu) had more property of date-palm trees gardens than any other amongst the Ansar in Medina and the most beloved of them to him was Bairuha garden, and it was in front of the Masjid of the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam). Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) used to go there and used to drink its nice sweet water.” Anas (Radi Allahu Anhu) added, “When these ayat were revealed ‘By no means shall you Attain righteousness unless You spend (in charity) of that Which you love. ‘ (Sura no.3 al-Imran aya no.92)

Abu Talha (Radi Allahu Anuh) said to Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) ‘O Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam)! Allah, the Blessed, the Superior says in the Quran: “By no means shall you attain righteousness, unless you spend (in charity) of that which you love.” (Sura no.3 al-Imran aya no.92) And no doubt, Bairuha garden is the most beloved of all my property to me. So I want to give it in charity in Allah’s Cause and I hope its reward from Allah. O Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam)! Spend it where Allah makes you think it feasible.’ On that Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, ‘Bravo! It is a useful property. I have heard what you have said (O Abu Talha), and I think it would be proper if you gave it to your family and relatives.’ So Abu Talha (Radi Allahu Anhu) said, I will do so, O Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam).’ Then Abu Talha (Radi Allahu Anhu) distributed that garden amongst his relatives and his cousins (paternal uncle’s sons).”

[GRADING] – [IT’S CHAIN IS SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]
Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (2/995, 996 hadith no.1940, book 58, chapter 1, hadith no.2), at-Tamhid (1/198) and al-Istidhkar (no.1877) 

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.1461, no.2752) and Muslim (42/998) from the hadith of Malik

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) This hadith shows the tremendous virtue of Abu Talha (Radi Allahu Anhu). He spent his most beloved wealth in the path of Allah. 

2) The noble companions were always ready to sacrifice their wealth and lives for the sake of the Quran and Sunnah. 

3) You should give preference to your close relatives when it comes to good deeds, keeping the ties of kinship, honoring, good behavior and charity. This is the best thing to do.

4) Having love for wealth is a natural thing on the condition that it does not go against the Sharia. Making plantations etc. and letting the noble scholars benefit from it is all allowed. 

5) It is allowed to act upon the general meaning unless there is a specific proof which gives an exception. 

6) Whosoever gives something in charity will not have the right to ask for it back in return. 

7) You should spend the best things in the path of Allah, whether that is wealth or your life and therefore you should choose the best and most intelligent people to teach religious knowledge to your children.  

8) The action of Abu Talha (Radi Allahu Anhu) is one of the best commentaries on the blessed ayah , “By no means shall you Attain righteousness…” Subhan Allah

9) The Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) commanded Abu Talha (Radi Allahu Anhu) to distribute his wealth to his close relatives. The Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam’s) amazing amount of generosity is established from this.

The Description Of Zakah

Muhammad Ibn Abdillah Ibn Abi Sa’sa’a: Two Hadith

[92] From Maalik from Muhammad ibn Abdullah ibn Abd ur-Rahman ibn Abi Sa’sa al-Ansari from al-Mazini from his father from Abu Sa’id al-Khudri (Radi Allahu Anhu) that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, “There is no Ushar on less than five awsuq of dates, there is no zakah on less than five awaq of silver and there is no zakah on less than five camels.”

[GRADING] – [IT’S CHAIN IS SAHIH]
Ibn Shihaab az-Zuhree clarified hearing (this hadeeth from Mahmood Ibn Rabee’ al-Ansari (RadhiAllaahu Anhu)) in Bukharee (no.425)

[REFERENCES] – [BUKHARI]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 1/244, 245 hadith no.579, book 17, chapter 1, hadith no.2), at-Tamhid (13/113), al-Istidhkar: (no.533)

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.1459) from the hadith of Malik

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) The Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, “There is no Ushar on a crops (measure, food, grain etc.) or dates except when they reach a quantity of five wasaq and there is no zakah on silver except when it reaches a quantity of two hundred dirhams.” (Sharh Ma’ani al-Athar of at-Tahawi 2/35 and it’s chain is [Hasan] good) 

Ibn Abdul Bar has said that this is a prestigious sunnah and is regarded as being accepted by everyone. (at-Tamhid 20/136) 

2) 1 wasaq is equivalent to 7 sa’a and 1 hijazi sa’a is equivalent to 5.3 pounds which equals 2kg 99g 520mg. See Mawlana Faruq Asgar Sarim’s book “Islami Awzan (The Islamic weight measurements)” (pg. 59) there is a difference of opinion on the correct weight of a sa’a from the scholars of the truth therefore it is better to round it up to 2.5kg when giving the charity so you are free from doubts and Allah knows best. 

3) There are fourty dirhams in 1 uqiya which is equivalent to 472g to 1220g. See Islami Awzan pg. 21

4) Zakah becomes obligatory after 1 full year

5) From the 2nd point above it is established that it is not necessary to give the ushur if the amount of crops (measure) is below 750kg or according to another opinion 630 kg. Ushur becomes obligatory after reaching the mentioned limits. Also see hadith no.402

Essential Q & A’s regarding Zakaat Al-Fitr

Rulings & Wisdoms

 

In the name of Allaah, the Most Merciful, the Bestower of Mercy.

It is incumbent upon every Muslim to seek knowledge and learn, more so before a particular act of worship is obligated upon him. Below is a concise summary of the basic rulings of Zakaat Al-Fitr.

 

What is Zakaat Al-Fitr?

Zakaat al-Fitr is an obligatory charity during Fitr i.e. when the fasting of Ramadhan is broken with Eid.

 

What is the wisdom behind Zakaat Al-Fitr?

The Prophet (sal Allaahu alayhi wa sallam) beautifully explained the wisdom for the obligation of Zakaat Al-Fitr, he said:

“It is purification for a fasting person, from idle talk and false speech, and also to feed the poor.” [1]

i.e. despite a fasting person trying his best to stay away from false speech during fasting, however sometimes he may fall short. Therefore, Zakaat Al-Fitr is a purification from this.

It is also a way of us showing our gratitude to our Lord, who bestowed upon us Ramadhan and allowed us to live till Eid.

 

Who needs to pay Zakaat Al-Fitr?

The Prophet (sal Allaahu alayhi wa sallam) obligated Zakaat Al-Fitr

…on the Muslims, free or a slave, male or female, young or old.” [2]

Therefore, it is an obligation upon every Muslim, however the responsibility falls on the head of the household such as the father or husband, to donate it on behalf of his dependants. This is assuming they have enough food for themselves.

It is not obligated to pay Zakaah Al-Fitr on behalf of a foetus, however it is recommended and desired.

What should be donated?

The Prophet (sal Allaahu alayhi wa salaam obligated Zakaat al-Fitr to be given

“a Saa’ of dates, a Saa’ of barley…” [3]

Abu Sa’eed Al-Khudree narrates that the companions

“would give Zakaat al-Fitr on behalf of every young and adult, the free and enslaved in the era of the Messenger of Allah one Saa’ of staple foodstuff…” [4]

The above – and other narrations – show us that Zakaat Al-Fitr should be donated in the form of the staple food of one’s locality.

 

 

How much should be donated?

The measure used at the time of the Prophet (sal Allaahu alayhi wa sallam) was a Saa’. [5]

In modern measurements this relates to:

Rice: 2.3kgs Raisins: 1.64kgs

Dates: 1.8kgs Wheat: 2.04kgs

Lentils 2.1kgs Flour: 2.06kgs

Couscous 1.8kgs [6]

From the above measurements we can see that Zakaat Al-Fitr ranges from 1.5kgs to 2.5kgs approximately depending on the type of food. For this reason, the scholars have recommended for 3kgs of food to be given as a matter of caution. [7]

 

How is it given and to whom?

Zakaat al-Fitr is given to any poor person who cannot afford his daily food for himself and his family. There are three possibilities for Zakaat Al-Fitr:

  1. The correct measure of food should be given to poor people in your locality. This is the Sunnah and it fulfils the objective of community cohesion.
  1. If poor people are not found in one’s community, then the correct measure of food can be transferred to a different community or country, especially if the poor are relatives.
  1. Finally, if none of the above are possible, then a person should give the monetary value of food, this is not from the Sunnah however it is done for a particular need or benefit. [8]

 

How should it be given?

From the above it is clear that the best practice is to give it directly to the poor in one’s locality. A person should include his wife and children in every step of the donation – buying the food, each child measuring their own quantity, packaging it and finally donating it to the poor.

However, we should also be sensitive to the poor who are receiving the Zakaat, that they are not to be made to feel belittled, therefore where appropriate anonymity can be maintained.

 

Why is it given to the poor specifically?

Eid is a day of happiness and festivities, and nobody should feel left out on such a day. For this reason, essential food items are distributed to the poor without them asking. It is also encouraged for Muslims to give gifts and honour the poor in other appropriate ways.

 

When should it be given?

The best time to donate the Zakaat Al-Fitr is before the Eid prayer, however it can be given earlier by a day or two especially if it is felt that it may not reach the poor in time. If it is delayed until after the Eid Prayer, it is no longer considered as Zakaah, but mere charity.

The Prophet (sal Allaahu alayhi wa sallam) said,

“Whoever donates the Zakaah before the Eid Prayer then it is an accepted form of Zakaah, and whoever gives it after the Prayer it is a Sadaqah.” [9]

Allaah knows best; May peace and blessings be upon our beloved Prophet, his family and companions.

 The Description of the Prophet’s (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) Generosity

[78] From Malik from Ibn Shihab from Ata ibn Yazid al-Laythi from Abu Sa’id al-Khudri that some people of the Ansar asked the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) and he gave to them. Then they asked him again, and he gave to them until he used up what he had. Then he said, “What wealth I have, I will not hoard from you. Whoever has forbearance, Allah will help him. Whoever tries to be independent, Allah will enrich him. Whoever tries to be patient, Allah will give him patience, and no one is given a better or vaster gift than patience.”

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri clarified hearing (this hadith from Ata Ibn Yazid al-Laythi) in Bukhari (no.6470)

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 2/997 hadith no.1945, book 58, chapter 2, hadith no.7), at-Tamhid (10/131), al-Istidhkar: (no.1882)    

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.1469) and Muslim (no.1053) from the hadith of Malik

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]

1) The Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) was very generous and kind towards the Umma.

2) It is allowed to give charity more than once to the one who asks and is in need.

3) Whoever doesn’t have money (which is more than he needs) then he can excuse himself (give his reason to not being able to give charity) to the one who asks (for it)

4) The best manner is that the one who is in need shouldn’t ask and should rather have patience and trust (in Allah) in all matters. It is not good to look at other people’s charity and ask for it without any sharia compliant excuse because of greediness just as it is established from other Ahadith. Also see hadith no.174

5) Ala Ibn Abdurahman Ibn Ya’qub said, “Charity does not in any way decrease the wealth and the servant who forgives (other people), Allah adds to his respect, and the one who shows humility, Allah elevates his status in the view (of the people).” (al-Muwatta Yahya’s narration 2/1000 hadith no.1949, and its chain is [Sahih] authentic)

This narration (athar) is also present in Sahih Muslim from the chain of, “al-Ala Ibn Abdurahman from his father from Abu Huraira (Radi Allahu Anhu)” from the hadith of the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) (Sahih Muslim hadith no.2588, Darusalam numbering no.6592)

It is Allowed to Give One Third of Your Wealth in your Will and It is Sunnah to Look After an ill Person

Amir Ibn Sa’d: One Hadith

[68] From Malik from Ibn Shihab from Amir Ibn Sa’d Ibn Abu Waqas from his father Sa`d Ibn Abi Waqas: That he said, “In the year of the last Hajj of the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) I became seriously ill and the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) used to visit me inquiring about my health. I told him, ‘I am reduced to this state because of illness and I am wealthy and have no inheritors except a daughter. Should I give two-thirds of my property in charity?’ He said, ‘No.’ I asked, ‘Half?’ He said, ‘No.’ then he added, ‘One third, and even one-third is much. You’d better leave your inheritors wealthy rather than leaving them poor, begging others. You will get a reward for whatever you spend for Allah’s sake, even for what you put in your wife’s mouth.’ I said, ‘O Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam)! Will I be left alone after my companions have gone?’ He said, ‘If you are left behind, whatever good deeds you will do will upgrade you and raise you high. And perhaps you will have a long life so that some people (Muslims) will be benefited by you while others (non-Muslims) will be harmed by you. O Allah! Complete the emigration of my companions and do not turn them renegades.’ But Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) felt sorry for poor Sa`d Ibn Khaula as he died in Mecca.” (but Sa`d Ibn Abi Waqas lived long after the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) 

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri clarified hearing (this hadith from Amir Ibn Sa’d Ibn Abi Waqas) in Bukhari (no.56) and Humaydi (no.66)

[REFERENCES] – [BUKHARI]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 2/763 hadith no.1533, book 17, chapter 3, hadith no.4), at-Tamhid (8/374, 375), al-Istidhkar: (no.1462)          

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.1295) from the hadith of Malik and Muslim (no.1628) from the hadith of Zuhri.

From the narration of Yahya Ibn Yahya

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]

1) In the will for the heirs only one third of the wealth is allowed to be given (to charity or gifts). It is not allowed to exceed this except if there is no heir then some scholars say it is allowed to give all the wealth to charity.

2) In this narration some narrators have said, “year of the conquest” instead of the farewell Hajj however what is most correct is that this event happened at the farewell Hajj.

3) Whatever charity, gifts or freeing of slaves is done during the death bed (illness before death) then this will be from the one third that is allowed to be left behind (given).

4) Allocating from the Qu’ran is allowed from the authentic hadiths because this hadith has made the general ruling for wills to become specific.

5) Some scholars have said that more than one third can be given in the will on the condition that the heirs consent to it.

6) If the person who is dying is poor then it is better for him not to give one third of his wealth away in his will and rather leave it for his relatives so that they don’t end up begging other people.

7) If the one who is virtuous is ill then the Sunnah is that a virtuous person should visit him during his illness. Jabir Ibn Abdullah al-Ansari (Radi Allahu Anhu) narrates that the Prophet (Sallalaahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, “Whoever visits a sick person then he enters into mercy and when he sits down then he becomes enveloped  in (mercy).” (al-Adab al-Mufrad of Bukhari no.522, and its chain is [Hasan] good and there is another route in Ahmad 3/304, Ibn Hibban in his Sahih no.711, al-Hakim 1/350, al-Bazar no.775 and its chain is [Hasan] good)

8) The foundations and pillars of actions are intentions. Whatever is spent for the sake of Allah then you will be rewarded with it. Insha Allah

9) Spending money on your family and near relatives and helping them as much as you can (with what you can afford) is from charity also where reward will be obtained Insha Allah.

10) Apart from the Majestic Qu’ran, the noble Prophet (Sallalaahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) received another type of revelation because of his saying, “because of you a group of people (a nation) will receive benefit from you and another people (nation) will receive loss” is news about the unseen (future events) whereby who and from is completed. Sa’d Ibn Abi Waqas (Radi Allahu Anhu) lived after the Prophet (Sallalaahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) and he conquered Iran whereby the Muslims gained benefit and the disbelievers gained a loss. Only Allah knows the unseen (future events) therefore it has become established that Allah had told the Prophet (Sallalaahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) about this. Wal Hamdullilah

From this hadith there are numerous other benefits that are known. For example the muhajir (one who migrates to another land) can’t come back to live in his homeland again. It is allowed to write about or say about lamenting over the death of a Muslim brother, on the condition that the lamenting has no extravagance, magnification and lies. So it is Sunnah to regret (have sorrow) and have a sympathetic express over distress and the original distress (affliction) is the loss in religion and (good) deeds. Etc.
