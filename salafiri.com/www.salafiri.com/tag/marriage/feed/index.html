Question: If the lower garment is raised above the knees is wudhu invalidated? What part of the body if uncovered invalidates wudhu? Does raising the lower garment to the thighs break and invalidate wudhu?

Answer: Wudhu is not invalidated, nor does it break If garments or clothes are raised above the knees or to the thighs, or if private parts are uncovered. This means a person’s wudhu does not break if clothes are removed from these body parts. However, if a person touches his private parts without clothes (or a barrier) then it is necessary for him to renew or repeat his wudhu. The Noble Prophet ﷺ said,

Whoever touched his private parts should perform wudhu. (Sharh Ma’ani al-Athar, 1/58, Chapter, does touching the private parts obligate Wudhu or not).      

20/7/1402H

(Al-Fatawa al-Rehmaniyyah 1/101)

Question: Zayd’s wife returned to her parent’s house in a state of anger! Zayd issued two Talaqs (divorces) and three months have passed, but now Zayd wants to back to his wife. So, is the first nikah still valid and sufficient or do they need to perform a second nikah?

Answer: If three menstrual period cycles have elapsed the nikah needs to be renewed in order for them to in wedlock. If three menstrual periods have not passed, the first nikah is sufficient without the need to renew the nikah. Allah says,

 “And divorced women shall wait for three menstrual periods…” (Surah al-Baqarah, 2:228)

(Fatawa Salafiyyah, page 29)

It Is Necessary To Perform A Walima (Wedding Banquet) Even If It Is Small

[150] From Malik that Humaid at-Tawil narrated to me from Anas Ibn Malik (Radi Allahu Anhu) that Abdur-Rahman bin `Auf came to Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) and he had marks of Sufra (yellow perfume). Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) asked him (about those marks). `AbdurRahman bin `Auf told him that he had married a woman from the Ansar. The Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) asked, “How much Mahr (Dowry) did you pay her?” He said, “I paid gold equal to the weight of a date stone.” Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said to him, “Give a wedding banquet, even if with one sheep.” 

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] – [BUKHARI]
Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (2/548, hadith no.1184, book 28, chapter 21, hadith no.47), at-Tamhid (2/178) and al-Istidhkar (no.1104) 

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.5153) from the hadith of Malik and narrated by Muslim (81/1427) from the hadith of Humaid at-Tawil  and he clarified hearing in Bukhari (no.5072) 

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) It is Sunah to perform a Walima to the best of your ability.  

2) It is better to give the dowry during the time of nikah or wedding day. 

3) It is allowed to marry someone who is not from your tribe. See point no.5 

4) The dowry can be a lot and it can be a little, a specific amount has not been established. However one should not be very extravagant just as Umar (Radi Allahu Anhu) commanded. See Sunan Abi Dawud (no.2106) and Musnad Ahmad (1/48 hadith no.340 and it is [Hasan])

The Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, “The best Nikah is the one which is easy.” (Sahih Ibn Hiban, al-Ihsan no.4060 (other manuscript no.4072) and it’s chain is [Sahih], Sunan Abi Dawud no.2117, and authenticated by al-Hakim 2/181, 182 on the conditions of the two Shaykhs and adh-Dhahabi agreed with him)

5) It is completely correct and allowed to marry someone who is from your tribe or someone who is not from your tribe. 

6) Only Allah the Most High knows the unseen.

7) From this hadith it is also known that the concept of a traditional Barat (T.N. i.e. a groom’s wedding procession where it is customary for the bridegroom to travel to the wedding venue (often the bride’s house) in a vehicle (traditionally being a mare), accompanied by his family members (typically carried out by those who are from the Indian Subcontinent)) does not exist in Islam, because how could it be possible that the companion did not take the most beloved person of his, Muhamad the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam), with his Barat.

It Is Prohibited To Perform An Engagement (Khitba – Asking For A Woman In Marriage) With A Woman Who Is Already Engaged

[97] From from Malik from Muhammad ibn Yahya ibn Haban from al-A’raj from Abu Hurairah (Radi Allahu Anhu) that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, “Do not ask for a woman in marriage when another Muslim has already done so.”

[GRADING] – [ITS CHAIN IS SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] – 

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 2/523 hadith no.1134, book 28, chapter 1, hadith no.1), at-Tamhid (13/19) and he said, “This hadith is established as being [Sahih] authentic.” al-Istidhkar: (no.1058)

Transmitted by Nasa’i (6/73 hadith no.3242) from the hadith of Abdurahman Ibn al-Qasim from Malik and narrated by Bukhari (no.5143) from a lengthy hadith of al-‘Araj, and it is narrated by Malik from Abi az-Zinad from al-‘Araj from Abu Huraira (Radi Allahu Anhu) as you will see in (hadith) no.351

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) Performing an engagement is allowed on the condition that it does not involve hindu (or other non-islamic) customs and things which go against the commands of the Sharia.

2) Every Muslim is the Islamic brother of other Muslims on the condition that he is not someone who does things which go against the Quran and Sunnah. 

3) If someone becomes engaged to a woman and sets the mahr amount with her then other people should not think about trying to be engaged or married to this woman. In this regard, the intercommunication (T.N. i.e. the setting of the mahr etc.) between the two parties implies acceptance. After this acceptance has occurred, it is not allowed for another person to attempt in having an engagement or marriage with this woman.     

4) There is goodness and prosperity for all the humankind in Islam.

5) You should stay far away from and prevent yourself from getting into differences, mischief and arguments.

6) Also see hadith no.229 and no.351.

It Is Allowed To Have Marital Relations With One’s Wife Whilst She Is Pregnant Or Breastfeeding After The Post Natal Bleeding

[90] from Malik that Muhammad ibn Abd ur-Rahman ibn Nawfal who said, ”Urwah ibn az-Zubayr informed me from A’isha Um ul-Mu’minin (Radi Allahu Anha) that Judama bint Wahb al-Asadiya (Radi Allahu Anha) informed her that she heard the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam), ‘I intended to prohibit ghila (having sexual intercourse with your pregnant or suckling wife) but I remembered that the Greeks (Romans?) and Persians do that without it causing any injury to their children.’ ”

[GRADING] – [IT’S CHAIN IS SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] – [MUSLIM]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 2/607, 608 hadith no.1329, book 30, chapter 3, hadith no.16), at-Tamhid (13/90), and he said, “And this hadith is established to be [Sahih] authentic” al-Istidhkar: (no.1249)

Transmitted by Muslim (no.1442) from the hadith of Malik

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) The husband can have sexual relations with his wife whilst she is breastfeeding her child after the post natal bleeding period. 

2) It is allowed for you to narrate the stories of the previous nations on the condition that the events have authentic chains. 

3) The one who is more virtuous can narrate from the one who is less virtuous because A’isha (Radi Allahu Anha) narrated this hadith from Judamah (Radi Allahu Anha) and by consensus of Ahlus Sunnah A’isha (Radi Allahu Anha) is more virtuous than Judamah (Radi Allahu Anha). 

4) It is allowed for a husband to have sexual intercourse with his wife who is pregnant on the condition that there is no risk of harm being done to the child who is in the woman’s womb. 

5) Every saying and action of the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) is a mercy, kindness and benefit for the Muslim Ummah. 

6) One can take credible and beneficial things from other nations. 

7) Doing Ijtihad is allowed on the condition that it doesn’t go against a clear evidence.
