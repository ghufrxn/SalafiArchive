The Prohibition of Eating the Meat of Domesticated Donkeys

[372] Abdullah bin Abu Aufa (Radi Allahu Anhu) said: We were smitten with hunger during the nights of Khaibar. On the Day of Khaibar, we fell upon domestic asses and we slaughtered them, and when our earthen pots boiled with them, the announcer of Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) made an announcement that the earthen pots should be turned over, and nothing should be eaten of the flesh of the domestic asses. Some of the people said that Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) had forbidden the meat because the Khumus had not been paid, while others said: He prohibited it for ever.

[GRADING] – [BUKHARI AND MUSLIM] 

[REFERENCES]

Transmitted by

Bukhari, Book of One-fifth of Booty to the Cause of Allah (Khumus), Chapter: The food gained as war booty in the battlefield [no.3155]

Muslim, Book of The Book of Hunting, Slaughter, and what may be Eaten, Chapter: The prohibition of eating the meat of domesticated donkeys [no.1937]

See also Nasai [no.4350] (Graded Sahih by Darusalam) 

[RULINGS FROM THE HADITH]

1) In the battle of Khaibar the meat of domestic donkeys was made haram. See the previous two ahadith no. 370, 371

2) There is also a hadith from Ali ibn Abi Talib (Radi Allahu Anhu) on the prohibition of eating domestic donkeys. Bukhari [no.4216], in this regard there a numerous hadith in Bukhari under the book of Kitab al-Maghazi under the chapter of Ghazwa Khaibar.

3) The hadith of Bukhari [no.5527] and Muslim [no.1936] mentions that when the prohibition for eating the meat of domestic donkeys was announced the companions were cooking the meat and at that very instance, whilst demonstrating great love and understanding of this Dīn and reverential love for the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wasallam), they drained and spilled their dishes.

Translated Abu Ubaydah
Translated, Checked & Additional Notes
Abu Hibban & Abu Khuzaimah Ansaari

The Meat of Domestic Donkeys

[371] Narrated Jabir bin `Abdullah (Radi Allahu Anhu):

The Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) prohibited eating domestic donkey meat and allowed the eating of horse flesh. (Narrated by Bukhari)

And (narrated) by Muslim (by Jabir bin ‘Abdullah) We ate during the time of Khaibar the (flesh) of horses and of wild donkeys, but Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) prohibited us (to eat) the flesh of domestic donkeys.

[GRADING] – [NARRATED BY BUKHARI]

[REFERENCES]

Transmitted by

Bukhari, Book of Hunting, Slaughtering, Chapter: The meat of domestic donkeys, [no. 5520],

Muslim, Book of The Book of Hunting, Slaughter, and what may be Eaten, Chapter: Chapter: The permissibility of eating horse meat, [no.1941]

Abu Dawud [no.3788], Nasai [7:210], Ahmad [3:361], Baihaqi [9:326], Darimi [no.1993], Ibn al-Jarud [no.885]

Also see the previous hadith.

[RULINGS FROM THE HADITH]

1) It is unlawful i.e. haram to eat the meat of domestic donkeys. see also the hadith of Amr ibn Shuʿayb with a similar meaning. Ahmad [2:219], Abu Dawud [no.3811], Nasai [7:239], Baihaqi [9:333], Hakim [2:103]. There are also numerous narrations in this regard also in Ibn Hibban and Ahmad.

2) Some hadith mention the reason it is impermissible to eat their meat is because they are unclean and unhygienic animals [Bukhari no.5528, 4198] Muslim [no.1940]. The hadith of Amr ibn Shuʿayb above mentions also animals that eat filth. 

3) The hadith of Bukhari [no.5527], Muslim [no.1936] mentions when eating the meat of domestic donkeys was announced the companions were cooking the meat at the very instance and whilst demonstrating great love and understanding of this Dīn and reverential love for the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wasallam); they drained and spilt their dishes.

4) Both cooked and uncooked meat of donkeys is unlawful as per the hadith of Bara (Radi Allahu Anhu) Bukhari [no.4226] 

5) It is lawful and thus permissible to eat horse (meat). See previous hadith no.370

6) It is permissible however to eat the meat of wild donkeys. See Muslim [no.1194, 1196]

The Description of Mutah Being Prohibited

Abdullah and Hasan Ibn Muhammad: One Hadith

[64] From Malik from Ibn Shihab from Abdullah and Hasan, the sons of Muhammad Ibn Ali Ibn Abu Talib from their father (RadiAllahu Anhu) that the Messenger of Allah, (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) forbade temporary marriage with women and the flesh of domestic donkeys on the Day of Khaybar.

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri clarified hearing (this hadith from Abdullah and Hasan) in Bukhari (no.5115)

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 2/542 hadith no.1178, book 28, chapter 18, hadith no.41) at-Tamhid (10/94) al-Istidhkar: (no.1098)  

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.4216, no.5523) and Muslim (29/1407) from the hadith of Malik.

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]

1) Taking pleasure from a woman in temporary marital relations is called Mut’at al-Nikah. In the beginning of Islam this was allowed and afterwards it was prohibited forever. Sabrah Ibn Ma’bad al-Juhni (RadiAllahu Anhu) narrates that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) said, “O people, I had permitted you to contract temporary marriage with women, but Allah has forbidden it (now) until the Day of Resurrection.” (Sahih Muslim (21/1406 [no.3422])

2) Imam Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri said, “ I haven’t seen any people more similar to the Christians than the Sabiens” The narrator of this Athar Ahmad Ibn Yunus said, “They (the meaning of Sabians here) are the Rafidah” (ash-Shar’iah of al-Ajuri (pg.955) hadith (no.2028) and its chain is [Sahih] authentic)

3) Some scholars did not think that it is haram (to eat the meat of) donkeys because of not having knowledge of this hadith and other authentic Ahadith, therefore this understanding of these scholars is rejected since it goes against the authentic Ahadith.

4) Although the Qur’an doesn’t mention that (eating the meat of) donkeys is haram, it is clearly established from the authentic ahadith that (eating the meat of) donkeys is haram. Apart from Ali (RadiAllahu Anhu), these Ahadith are also established by the following companions in marfu’ form:

Jabir Ibn Abdullah (Sahih Bukhari no.4219 and Sahih Muslim 36/1941) Bara Ibn Azib (Sahih Bukhari no.4266, no.5525, no.5526 and Sahih Muslim 28/1938 [no.5012], Abdullah Ibn Abi Awfah (Sahih Bukhari no.3155, no.5525, no.5526 and Sahih Muslim 26/1937 [no.5010], Anas Ibn Malik (Sahih Bukhari no.2991, and Sahih Muslim no.1940), Abu Tha’labah al-Khushni (Sahih Bukhari no.5527, and Sahih Muslim 23/1936 [no.5007], Abdullah Ibn Umar (Sahih Bukhari no.5521, and Sahih Muslim 24/561 after hadith no.1936 [no.5008], Salamah Ibn Akwah (Sahih Bukhari no.2277, and Sahih Muslim 33/1802 after hadith no.1939, [no.5018], Abdullah Ibn Abas (Sahih Bukhari no.4227, and Sahih Muslim no.1939), al-Hakim Ibn Umar wal-Ghifari (Musnad al-Humaydi with my verification no.861 and its chain is authentic, manuscript of al-‘Adhami no.859), Miqdam Ibn Ma’di Karb al-Kindi (Sunan Abu Dawud no.4604 and its chain is authentic, and Musnad Ahmad 4/131), Abdullah Ibn Amr Ibn al-As (Sunan Abu Dawud no.3811 and its chain is [Hasan] good) RadiAllahu Anhum

These hadith are mutawatir. See Nadham al-Mutanathir of al-Kitani (page 162 hadith no.163)

5) A specific proof is given priority over general proof.

6) The authentic Ahadith are the explanation and commentary of the Majestic Qur’an.

7) If the tafsir of the Majestic Qur’an is done by leaving the authentic Ahadith and the understanding of the righteous salaf and is explained with language only, poems and the distortions of the rejectors of hadith, then nothing will be obtained except misguidance.

8) The command of the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhsi Wa Sallam) is wajib to follow just like Allah’s command is wajib.

9) There are a lot of people in the world who follow their own desires even though they know the authentic proofs.

10) Ali RadiAllahu Anhu is the narrator of temporary marriage being haram therefore those who claim to love him should follow him.
