Saying Labaik Or The Takbir Whilst Going From Mina To Arafat

Muhammad ath-Thaqafi: One Hadith

[100] From Malik that Muhammad ibn Abi Bakr ath-Thaqafi once asked Anas ibn Malik (Radi Allahu Anhu), while the two of them were going from Mina to Arafa, “What did you use to do on this day when you were with the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam)?” He said, “Those of us who were saying the talbiya would continue doing so, and no-one disapproved of it, and those of us who were saying ‘Allahu akbar’ would continue doing so, and no-one disapproved of that either.”

[GRADING] – [IT’S CHAIN IS SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 1/337 hadith no.760, book 20, chapter 13, hadith no.43), at-Tamhid (13/73, and he said, “This hadith is [Sahih] authentic), al-Istidhkar: (no.710)

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.1659) and Muslim (no.1285) from the hadith of Malik

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) The person performing Hajj reaches Mina (a valley in Maka) on the 8th day of Dhul Hijja. Then on the next day, the 9th of Dhul Hijja, the person performing Hajj goes to Arafat. 

2) It is allowed to say the labaik or takbir whilst going from Mina to Arafat.

3) You should not refute other Muslim brothers in things which are allowed in Islam.

4) If you don’t know about particular issues in Islam then you should ask the people of knowledge.

5) The scholars should answer with proofs from the Quran, hadith and Sharia evidences. 

6) It is allowed to use the actions that the companions (Radi Allahu Anhum) did in the lifetime of the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) as evidences on the condition that the action does not go against a clear authentic text (evidence).

7) The Talbiya and Takbir should be said in a loud voice whilst performing Hajj or Umrah.

8) It is allowed to use the sayings (Athar) of the Salaf Salihin as proof.

The Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) Performed Hajj Ifrad (On Its Own)

Abul Aswad: Four Ahadith

[88] From Malik, from Abul-Aswad Muhammad ibn Abd ur-Rahman, from Urwah ibn az-Zubayr, from A’isha, Um ul-Mu’minin (Radi Allahu Anha) that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) did Hajj (Ifrad) on its own.

[GRADING] – [IT’S CHAIN IS SAHEEH, THERE ARE NO DOUBTS IN IT]

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 1/335 hadith no.755, book 20, chapter 11, hadith no.38), at-Tamhid (13/98), al-Istidhkar: (no.705)    

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.1562), Muslim (118/1211) and Ibn Majah (no.2965) from the hadith of Malik

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) It is clear from this Sahih hadith from A’isha (Radi Allahu Anha) that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) performed Hajj (Ifrad) on its own. Ibn Umar (Radi Allahu Anhu) narrated that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said the labaik of Hajj Ifrad (Sahih Muslim no.1231)

In the narration of Jabir (Radi Allahu Anhu) it is mentioned that we said the labaik of Hajj Ifrad with the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) (Sahih Muslim no.1213) 

In contrast, Anas (Radi Allahu Anhu) narrated that the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said the labaik of Hajj and Umrah (Sahih Bukhari no.4353, no.4354 and Sahih Muslim no.1232). There are narrations like this from other companions (Radi Allahu Anhum) and this is mutawatir. The reconciliation of these 2 narrations is that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) first said labaik for Hajj Ifrad and later on he said the labaik for Hajj Qiran (Umrah and Hajj). Each companion (Radi Allahu Anhum) narrated this according to their own knowledge. For more details see Sharh az-Zirqani of Muwata al-Imam Malik (vol. 2 pg. 251)

2)  Hajj Ifrad, Hajj Qiran and Hajj Tamatu’ are the three types of Hajj, and acting upon any one of them is allowed up until the day of judgement. The saying of some scholars about the hadith of Hajj Ifrad that it is Shadh [Strange] or Mansukh [Abrogated], is false and rejected.  Abu Hurairah (Radi Allahu Anhu) narrates that Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said “By Him in Whose Hand is my life. Ibn Maryam (Esa (Alayhisalam)) would certainly pronounce Talbiya for Hajj or for Umrah or for both (simultaneously as a Hajj Qiran).” (Sahih Muslim no.1252, Darusalam Numbering no.3030, as-Sunan al-Kubra of al-Bayhaqi 5/2, the night and day of a person who performs Hajj pg. 82) 

From this Sahih hadith it is known that Hajj Ifrad, Hajj Qiran and Hajj Tamatu’ will remain until the day of judgement. 

3) Ali (Radi Allahu Anhu) said, “Whoever performs Hajj Ifrad then that is good, and whoever performs (Hajj) Tamatu’ then he has acted upon the Sunnah of the Majestic Qur’an and the noble Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) (as-Sunan al-Kubra of al-Bayhaqi 5/21 and it’s chain is Sahih, the night and day of a person who performs Hajj pg. 83)

Abu Bakr, Umar and Uthman (Radi Allahu Anhum) performed Hajj Ifrad. (Sunan at-Tirmidhi no.820 and it’s chain is Hasan)

Note: After taking into consideration other evidences, Hajj Tamatu’ is the most virtuous.

The Prohibition of Hunting Whilst in the State of Ihram

[53] And from the same chain of narration from Abdullah Ibn Abas, that as-Sab Ibn Jaththama al-Laythi once gave a wild ass (donkey) to the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) while he was at al-Abwa, or Wadan, and the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) gave it back to him. However, when the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) saw the expression on the man’s face he said, “We only gave it back to you because we are in Ihram.” 

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri clarified hearing (this hadith from Ubaydullah Ibn Abdullah Ibn Utba Ibn Mas’ud) in Bukhari (no.2592)

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 1/353 hadith no.801, book 20, chapter 25, hadith no.83) at-Tamhid (9/54) al-Istidhkar: (no.751)    

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.1825) and Muslim (no.1193) from the hadith of Malik.

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]

1) In the state of Ihram it is not permissible to hunt on land, or get someone to hunt, or buy a land hunted (animal). See at-Tamhid (9/58) and Sura al-Ma’ida (ayah: (no.96))

2) Whoever is not in Ihram if he hunts for himself and then afterwards gifts (the hunted animal) to those who are in Ihram then eating it is permitted. See this book hadith (no.492)

3) Abdullah Ibn Amir Ibn Rabi’a said, “I once saw Uthman ibn Afan in Ihram on a hot summer’s day at al-Arj,and he had covered his face with a red woolen cloth. Some game-meat was brought to him and he told his companions to eat. They said, ‘Will you not eat then?’, and he said, ‘I am not in the same position as you. It was hunted for my sake.’ “(because of this reason I don’t eat it) (al-Muwatta Abu Mus’ab az-Zuhri’s narration (1/452) hadith (no.1147), and its chain is authentic, and the hadith in another form Muwatta Yahya’s narration (1/354) hadith (no.802))

4) A’isha (Radi Allahu Anha) said, “If (in the state of Ihram) you get an urge to do something (meaning to eat the meat of the hunted animal), leave it,” by which she meant eating game-meat (al-Muwatta Yahya’s narration (1/354) hadith (no.803) and its chain is authentic)

5) If there is a fear of another brother falling into misunderstanding because of a certain task, then it should be clarified so that the heart stays clean and happy for each other.

The Description (Explanation) of the Types of Hajj

[38] And with the same chain of narration that “We set out with the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) in the year of the farewell Hajj and we went into ihram for Umra. Afterwards, the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) said, ‘Whoever has a sacrificial animal with him should go into ihram for Hajj and Umra together, and he should not leave ihram without leaving ihram for both of them at the same time.’ ” She continued “I was menstruating when I got to Makah, so I did not do tawaf of the House or sa’i between Safa and Marwa. I complained to the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) and he said, ‘Undo your hair and comb it and leave the Umra and go back into ihram for the Hajj.’ “She said, “I did so, and when we had completed the Hajj, the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) sent me with Abd ar-Rahman ibn Abu Bakr as-Sidiq to at-Tan’im and I performed an Umra and he said, ‘This is in place of your Umra.’ ” “Those who had entered ihram for the Umra did tawaf of the House and sa’i between Safa and Marwa, then left ihram. Then they did another tawaf after returning from Mina for their Hajj, whereas those who entered ihram for the Hajj or combined the Hajj and the Umra, only did one tawaf.”

 

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

Ibn Shihab (narrates with) anana and he followed Hisham Ibn Urwa in Bukhari (no.1786) and Muslim (115/1211) and Qasim Ibn Muhammad narrates from A’isha (Radi Allahu Anha), see al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 1/410,411 hadith no.951 and its chain is authentic)

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 1/411,410 hadith no.951, book 20, chapter 74, hadith no.223 and with it (the words): and wamtashiti (and I combed it)) at-Tamhid (8/198), al-Istidhkar: (no.892)     

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.1556) and Muslim (111/1211) from the hadith of Malik.

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]

1) Acting upon the three types of Hajj: Ifrad, Qiran and Tamat’u is completely correct. (at-Tamhid (8/205)) It is established from an authentic hadith of Sahih Muslim (no.1252) that the Hajj Ifrad will continue until the day of judgement. Also see as-Sunan al-Kubra of al-Bayhaqi (5/2) therefore saying that Hajj Ifrad is abrogated [mansukh] is false. Note: It is established from authentic narrations [Ahadith] that the most virtuous type of Hajj from the three types according to the most correct opinion is Hajj Tamat’u.

2) There is only one Tawaf (the tawaf which is done with the House of Allah in the beginning) in the Hajj Qiran and Hajj Ifrad whilst the person doing Hajj Tamat’u has to perform two Tawafs along with Qurbani. The more the actions which are sunnah (that are done) the more reward is given.

3) It is not allowed to perform the tawaf and sa’i in the state of menstruation.

4) There is ijma that the person doing Umra should first do tawaf of the House of Allah then do the sa’i of safa and marwa. See at-Tamhid (8/216) except when the person reaches Makah on the night of Arafah so in this condition he will go to Arafat first so that (a great pillar of) Hajj is not left.

5) The menstruating woman who’s Umra is wajib (obligatory) on her,  can go to Tan’im to perform Umra.

6) Tan’im is a place at Makah Mukaramah where today it is called Masjid A’isha. Some people perform optional Umrah from here whereby there is no proof from the authentic Ahadith and sayings of the salaf salihin. Also see hadith (no.39)

The Duha Prayer is Recommended

[37] And with the same chain of narration that “I never once saw the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) doing the voluntary prayer of duha (all the time), and I understand this prayer to be recommended. Sometimes the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) would refrain from a practice that he loved to do, fearing that people would do the same and it would become fard (obligatory) for them.”

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri clarified hearing (this hadith from Urwa Ibn az-Zubayr) in Ahmad (6/86 hadith (no.24559))

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 1/152,153 hadith no.357, book 9, chapter 8, hadith no.29 and with it (the words): and I pray this prayer) at-Tamhid (8/134), al-Istidhkar: (no.127)   

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.1128) and Muslim (no.718) from the hadith of Malik with the same meaning.

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]

1) In one narration A’isha (Radi Allahu Anha) was asked, “Did the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) pray the duha prayer? And she replied, “No, except when he came back from a journey (then he prayed it) Sahih Muslim (no.717) In another narration A’isha (Radi Allahu Anha) said, “The Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) prayed four rak’ahs or more for the duha prayer. (Sahih Muslim: (no.719)

It is known (from this) that according to A’isha (Radi Allahu Anha) The Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) prayed the duha prayer, but he didn’t always pray it. See Sunan al-Kubra of al-Bayhaqi (3/49)

2) The duha prayer is not wajib (obligatory) or sunnah mu’akidah rather it is recommended [mustahab] and more virtuous (if it is prayed then left out). [Trans Note: Salatul Duha should not be prayed everyday as their is no clear evidence for this]

3) The duha prayer is 2, 4 or 8 rakas. Sahih Muslim (no.720 and no.721, no.719) Sahih Bukhari: (no.1176), and Sahih Muslim: (no.336) after hadith (no.719) it is sunnah to perform the salaam after every 2 rakas.

4) The Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) commanded Abu Hurayrah (Radi Allahu Anhu) and Abu ad-Darda (Radi Allahu Anhu) to pray the duha prayer. (Sahih Muslim: (no.721, no.722))

5) The time for the duha prayer starts straight after sunrise and its most virtuous time (to pray it in) is when the feet of the female camel’s offspring become hot (feel the heat of the sun) (because of the sunshine). (Sahih Muslim: no.748)) This prayed is also called Salatul Awabin (the prayer of those who repent a lot). This time is approximately until the fourth part of the beginning of the day.

6) The summary of the blessed Prophet’s (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) saying is that whosoever does the ablution and leaves to the house to perform the compulsory prayer (in the masjid) then he receives the reward of Hajj and whosoever goes to pray the duha prayer then he receives the reward of Umra. Sunan Abu Dawud (no.558) and its chain is good [Hasan], and Musnad Ahmad (5/268) and All praise is due to Allah.
