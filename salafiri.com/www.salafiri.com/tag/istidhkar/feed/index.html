Seven Types of Blessed People 

[155] From Malik from Khubaib Ibn Abdirahman from Hafs Ibn Asim from Abu Hurairah (Radi Allahu Anhu) or Abu Sa’id al-Khudri (Radi Allahu Anhu) that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam),  said, ‘There are seven whom Allah will put under His shade (of His Throne) on the day on which there is no shade except His shade: a just leader, a youth who grows up worshiping Allah, a man whose heart is attached to the Masjid when he leaves it until he returns to it, two men who love each other for the sake of Allah and meet for that and part for that, a man who remembers Allah then he is alone and his eyes overflow with tears, a man who refuses the approach of a noble, beautiful woman, saying “I fear Allah the Rab of all the worlds,” and a man who gives charity and conceals it so that his left hand does not know what his right hand gives.’ 

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] – [MUSLIM]
Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (2/952, 953 hadith no.1841, book 51, chapter 5, hadith no.14), at-Tamhid (2/279) and al-Istidhkar (no.1777) 

Transmitted by Muslim (no.1031) from the hadith of Malik, and al-Bukhari (no.6806) from the hadith of Khubaib Ibn Abdurahman al-Ansari from Hafs Ibn Hashim with the same hadith.  

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) Hafidh Ibn Abdul Bar took the word “Dhilla” (shade) and understood it to mean mercy. If the meaning of the word literally means shade then this would mean the shade of Allah’s Throne as it is mentioned in another hadith that, “…seven people will be given shade under Allah’s Throne…” (Mushkil al-Athar of at-Tahawi, new edition 15/69 hadith no.5844, Tuhfatul Akhyar 7/195 hadith no.5117 and it’s chain is [Sahih] authentic). 

Whosoever is lenient with with the loan of someone who owes them (i.e. allows them a longer time to pay back, or reduces and forgives them part of the loan or pays of their debt etc.), “…Allah will shade him on the Day of Judgement under His Throne…” (Sunan at-Tirmidhi no.1302, and he said, “Hasan Sahih Garib” and it’s chain is [Sahih] authentic)

The wording in a hadith of Mu’adh Ibn Jabal (Radi Allahu Anhu) are, “…Allah will put them under the shade of His Throne…” (al-Mustadrak of al-Hakim 4/169 hadith no.7315, and it’s chain is [Sahih] authentic and al-Hakim authenticated it on the conditions of Bukhari and Muslim and adh-Dhahabi agreed with him)

2) This hadith points towards many important matters such as:

  • The virtue of a just leader
  • The virtue of a youth who spends their youthful days in the worship of Allah
  • The virtue of the one who is connected to the Masjid and loves it and gives it preference over and above worldly matters 
  • The virtue of the one who loves and hates someone for the sake of Allah only and not because of a worldly or personal gain 
  • The virtue of the one who remembers Allah and cries whilst they are alone
  • The virtue of the one who fears Allah when a noble and beautiful woman invites him to a sinful act of relationship 
  • The virtue of the one who gives charity in Allah’s path secretly

Fasting Whilst Travelling Is An Option

Chapter of  two (narrators whose names begin with the letter) Haa for both of them of Humayd at-Tawil from Anas: Six hadith

[147] From Malik from Humayd at-Tawil that Anas ibn Malik (Radi Allahu Anhu) said, “We once travelled with the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam), in Ramadhan, and those who were fasting did not find fault with those who were not, and those who were not fasting did not find fault with those who were.”

[GRADING] – [IT’S CHAIN IS SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] – [BUKHARI]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 1/295 hadith no.661, book 18, chapter 7, hadith no.23), at-Tamhid (2/169, and he said, “This hadith is [Muttasil Sahih] authentically connected), al-Istidhkar: (no.611)

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.1947) from the hadith of Malik, and Muslim (99/118) from the hadith of Humayd at-Tawil and he has clarified hearing from him with it.

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) It is allowed to either fast or not fast whilst travelling. If one does not fast whilst travelling in Ramadhan then the individual should make up the missed fasts later on. It is more virtuous to not fast whilst travelling on the condition that it is severely hot and there are severe difficulties.

2) Also see hadith no.465

3) Abdullah Ibn Umar (Radi Allahu Anhu) did not fast whilst travelling. (Muwatta Imam Malik 1/295, hadith no.663 and it’s chain is [Sahih])

Whilst Urwah Ibn Zubair used to fast whilst travelling. (Muwatta Imam Malik 1/295, hadith no.664 and it’s chain is [Sahih])

4) If there are two allowable ways of carrying out a deed that have been established in the Sharia then one should not condemn the other person. (T.N. for example if person A is fasting whilst travelling and person B is not fasting whilst travelling, then person A should not condemn person B and vice versa, since both methods been established in the Sharia). 

 5) It is absolutely correct to refute those things which go against the Qur’an and Sunnah.

Performing Sa’e between Safa and Marwa

[146] From Malik from Jafar ibn Muhammad from his father from Jabir ibn Abdullah that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam), walked when he came down from Safa and Marwa and then, when he reached the middle of the valley, he broke into a light run until he had left it.

[GRADING] – [IT’S CHAIN IS SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] 

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 1/374, 375 hadith no.851, book 20, chapter 42, hadith no.131), at-Tamhid (2/93), al-Istidhkar: (no.799)

Transmitted by Nasa’i (5/243 hadith no.2984) from the hadith of Abdurahman Ibn al-Qasim from Malik

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) An individual should walk between Safa and Marwa when doing Sa’e and if an individual has a sharia compliant excuse such as an illness then it is allowed for that individual to perform Sa’e on a wheelchair, being carried on their companions shoulders or lying down on a bed. 

2) If an individual performing Sa’e assists and carries another person to perform Sa’e for a wage or for free, then the Sa’e of the individual who provides the help and support is valid and this is the same ruling for Tawaf also. 

3) The Sa’e should be performed after performing the Tawaf of the House of Allah therefore doing Tawaf first and then doing the Sa’e is incorrect.

An Individual Should Sacrifice The Sacrificial Animal Himself 

[145] From Malik from Jafar ibn Muhammed ibn AIi from his father from Jabir ibn Abdullah (Radi Allahu Anhu) that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) sacrificed some of his sacrificial animals himself, and someone other than him sacrificed the rest.

[GRADING] – [IT’S CHAIN IS SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] 

Al-Muwatta (Abi Mus’ab az-Zuhri’s narration (1/534 hadith no.1381)

Transmitted by an-Nasa’i (7/231 hadith no.4424) from the hadith of Malik. And in Yahya Ibn Yahya’s narration (1/394 hadith no.909) “Malik from Ja’far from his father from Ali Ibn Abi Talib” and it is incorrect. See at-Tamhid (2/106) and al-Istidhkar (no.849)  

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) It is better and more virtuous for an individual to carry out the sacrifice himself. It is also allowed to appoint someone else to carry out the sacrifice on his behalf. 

2) It is allowed to appoint a Wakil.

3) The meaning of “other than him” in the hadith is Ali Ibn Abi Talib (Radi Allahu Anhu) just as it is established from other narrations.

Dua At Safa And Marwa

[144] From Malik from Jafar ibn Muhammed ibn AIi from his father from Jabir ibn Abdullah (Radi Allahu Anhu) that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) used to say (the takbir), “Allah is the Greatest” three times when he stopped on Safa, and “There is no diety worthy of worship in truth except Allah, alone, without any partner. To Him belong the Kingdom and praise, and He has power over everything” three times, and make dua. He would then do the same on Marwa.

[GRADING] – [IT’S CHAIN IS SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] 

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (1/372 hadith no.847, book no.20, chapter no.41, hadith no.127), at-Tamhid (2/91), al-Istidhkar no.795

Transmitted by an-Nasa’i (5/240 hadith no.2975) from the hadith of Ibn ul-Qasim from Malik and narrated by Muslim (no.1218) from the hadith of Jafar Ibn Muhamad. 

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) One should go on Safa and Marwa whilst being able to see the house of Allah (Ka’bah) as this is the sunnah. 

2) There are seven circuits (laps) whilst doing the Safa and Marwa Sa’i. 

(1) From Safa to Marwa (2) From Marwa to Safa (3) From Safa to Marwa (4) From Marwa to Safa (5) From Safa to Marwa (6) From Marwa to Safa (7) From Safa to Marwa 

The seventh circuit is the last one. After this the person who is performing Umrah can cut or shave their hair and take off their Ihram. The person performing Hajj Ifrad or Hajj Qiran becomes free from the restrictions of Ihram after throwing the pebbles at the Jamarat and then performing the Qurbani or shaving the hair. 

3) Abdullah ibn Umar (Radi Allahu Anhu) used to make dua on Safa saying, “O Allah, You have said, ‘call on Me – I will answer you’ and You do not break Your promise. So I am asking You, in the same way that You have guided me to Islam, not to take it away from me, and that You make me die while I am muslim.”(Muwatta Imam Malik 1/373 hadith no.848 and it’s chain is [Sahih] authentic). From this it is known that making other duas at Safa and Marwa is allowed.
