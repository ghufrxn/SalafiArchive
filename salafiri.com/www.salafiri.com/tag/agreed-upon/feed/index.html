What Should the Person Do if He Forgets In the Prayer

Abdurahman al-A’raj: Three Ahadith

[81] from Malik from Ibn Shihab from al-A’raj that Abdullah Ibn Buhayna (Radi Allahu Anhu) said, “The Messenger of Allah, (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam), prayed two rakas with us and then stood without sitting (in the tashahud position) and the people stood with him. When he had finished the prayer and we waited for him to say the taslim, he said ‘Allah is greater’ and did two sajdas from the sitting position before the taslim and then (came up from the sajdas and) said the taslim.”

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri clarified hearing (this hadith from Abdurahman al-A’raj) in Bukhari (no.829)

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 1/96 hadith no.214, book 3, chapter 17, hadith no.65), at-Tamhid (10/183), al-Istidhkar: (no.184, 185)          

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.1224) and Muslim (no.570) from the hadith of Malik

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) No one from the creation is free (or protected) from making mistakes and forgetting except for those who Allah protects.

2) If the person praying salah forgets (to sit down for) the first tashahud and stands up instead, then he shouldn’t sit down (straight away), rather he should complete the prayer (rak’a) and perform the prostration of forgetfulness before or after the salam. If someone stands up then sits back down (straight away after realising the mistake) then according to the majority of the scholars his prayer doesn’t become invalid. See at-Tamhid (10/185)

3) Sa’d Ibn Abi Waqas (Radi Allahu Anhu) was leading the prayer and stood up in the third rak’a (instead of sitting down for tashahud). The people said Subhan Allah however he didn’t sit back down (straight away) and rather he made an indication to the people to stand up and completed the prayer making the two (extra) prostrations (at the end). (Musanaf Ibn Abi Shaybah 2/34 hadith no.4493 and its chain is [Sahih] authentic)
This understanding is also established from Uqba Ibn Amir (Radi Allahu Anhu) in a detailed narration. See al-Mustadrak 1/325 hadith no.1214, and al-Awsat of Ibn al-Mundhir (3/288) and its chain is [Sahih] authentic)

4) Doing salam to one side whilst performing the prostration of forgetfulness is not established from the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) or the noble companions (Radi Allahu Anhum).

5) If the Imam forgets in the prayer and then he forgets to perform the prostration of forgetfulness then according to Hakam Ibn Utba the people praying behind the Imam should perform the prostration of forgetfulness and according to Hamad Ibn Abi Sulayman they don’t need to perform it. (Musanaf Ibn Abi Shayba 2/39 hadith no.4525 and its chain is [Sahih] authentic)
It is established from the hadith of Abdullah Ibn Mas’ud (Radi Allahu Anhu) (Sahih Bukhari no.1226 and Sahih Muslim no.572) that if the Imam forgets to perform the prostration of forgetfulness then he should still perform it after doing the taslim (with the people who were praying behind him)

6) The scholars have an ijma that it is fardh to perform ruku, sujud, qiyam and the last sitting down (tashahud) in salah. (at-Tamhid 10/189)
Therefore if any one of these have been missed out then the raka would have been missed, and it is necessary to repeat this raka.

How to Perform the Funeral Prayer

[14] And with the same chain of narration that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) announced the death of an-Najashi to everyone on the day that he died, and went out with them to the place of prayer, and then formed them into rows and said “Allah is the greatest” four times.

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]
Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri clarified hearing (this hadith from Sa’id Ibn Musayab) in Bukhari (no.3880, no.3881)

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]
Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (1/226,227 hadith no.533, book 16, chapter 5, hadith no.14) at-Tamhid (6/324) and al-Istidhkar (no.490)

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.1245) and Muslim (no.951) from the hadith of Malik.

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) It is allowed to inform the people when someone dies. Fath ul-Bari (3/116).

It is narrated from Ibn Umar (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) that he instructed the people to inform others of the death of Rafi’ Ibn Khadij (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) in the surrounding areas of Madinah. (al-Mu’jam al-Kabir of Tabarani ((4/239) hadith (no.4242) as-Sunan al-Kubra of Bayhaqi (4/74) and its chain is authentic)

2) Another narration prohibits informing the people about someones death (Sunan at-Tirmidhi: (no.986) and he said: this hadith is [Hasan] good) however this narration is weak because it is not established that Bilal Ibn Yahya met with Hudhayfa Ibn al-Yaman (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) [TN: Refer to Kitab al-Janaiz, Bulugh al-Maram]

Even if this narration was authentic then its meaning would be that it is prohibited to go around the and publicly announce the death of someone in public places just like the people of ignorance used to do. See the magazine al-Hadeeth  (11/20) and the book of funerals of Mubarakpuri (pg.18) [TN: The prohibition here is the public announcing in the market places etc as this resemblance the practice of the people of ignorance.]

3) It is better to say 4 takbirs in the funeral prayer however saying 5 takbirs is also permissible just as it is established from Sahih Muslim (72/ 957) [no.2216]) [TN: Although it is permissible the kayfiyyah of doing the 5th takbir is not detailed in the Sunnah as to when it is to be done]

4) To pray the funeral prayer outside the Masjid is better whilst praying in the Masjid is also allowed.

5) Allah the Blessed and most High informed His beloved (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) about affairs far afield through revelation.

6) If there is a valid legislated reason in the Shari’ah then praying the absentia (when the person involved is not present when it happens) funeral prayer is allowed. [TN there is contention of this issue, some of the scholars opine the person of absentia must be well known i.e. famous and or no one at his location prayed his funeral prayer. Some also use as evidence the hadith of the female companion who would clean the Masjid, thereafter the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wasallam) went and prayed her funeral prayer.]

7) It is not a condition to have even or odd rows in the funeral prayer. The noble Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) lead the funeral prayer and there were 2 rows behind him. (Sahih Muslim: (no.952), Dar us-Salam (numbering): (no.2209) [TN: this is another foreign practice no doubt introduced by some ardent madhabists of the subcontinent]

It is not allowed to go to an area where there is a plague and to run away from it (if you are there when the plague breaks out)

Abdullah Ibn Amir Ibn Rabia al-Adawi: One hadith

 

[9] From Malik from Ibn Shihab from Abdullah Ibn Amir Ibn Rabia that Umar Ibn al-Khattab (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) went out to ash-Sham. When he came to Sargh, near Tabuk, he heard that the plague had broken out in ash-Sham. Abd ar-Rahman Ibn Awf (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) told him that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) said, “If you hear that a land has a plague in it, do not go forward to it. If it comes upon a land which you are in, do not depart in flight from it.” So Umar Ibn al-Khattab (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) came back from Sargh.

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]
Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri clarified hearing this hadith with al-Qadhi Ahmad Ibn Mohammed Ibn Esa al-Burti in the Musnad of Abdurahman Ibn Awf (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) (no.6) and its chain is [Hasan] good

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]
Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (2/896,897 hadith no.1722, book 45, chapter 7, hadith no.24) at-Tamhid (6/210) and al-Istidhkar (no.1654)

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.5730, no.6973) and Muslim (no.2219) from the hadith of Malik

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADEETH]
1) Salim Ibn Abdullah Ibn Umar (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) narrated that because of the hadith of Abdurahman Ibn Awf (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) Umar (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) returned back. (Sahih Bukhari: (no.6973) and Sahih Muslim: (100/2219) this is the one of the most highest examples of following of the Sunnah.

2) Khabr Wahid is a proof with the condition that the person narrating is reliable and trustworthy.

3) If a person becomes victim to a plague the area they are in, it is possible the person’s belief can become corrupt. Perhaps this is the reason why it is forbidden to go to the area where there is a plague or to leave that area (if you are there when the plague breaks out).

4) This is an example of the love the companions (RadhiAllaahu Anhum) had for the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam)
