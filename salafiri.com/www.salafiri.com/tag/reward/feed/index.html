The Virtue Of The Person Whose 3 or 2 Children Have Died

Muhammad Ibn Abi Bakr: One hadith

[94] From Malik from Muhammad ibn Abi Bakr ibn Amr ibn Hazm from his father from Abu’n-Nadr as-Sulami (Radi Allahu Anhu) that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalhi Wa Sallam) said, “If three of a muslim’s children die, and he remains content with that, they will be a protection for him from the Fire.” A woman who was with the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, “Or two (children die), Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam)?”, and he said, “Or two. (children who die will be the cause of being protected from the Fire)”

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] – 

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 1/235 hadith no.558, book 16, chapter 13, hadith no.39 and with it, “from Abi an-Nadr” and this is the most correct), at-Tamhid (13/86, 87), al-Istidhkar: (no.512)

Transmitted by Abul Qasim al-Jawhari in Musnad al-Muwatta (245/262) from the hadith of Malik

And the hadith has a witnessessing report with Bukhari (no.101) and Muslim (no.2633) etc. and the hadith with it is authentic

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) Abu an-Nadr as-Sulami (Radi Allahu Anhu) was a companion. It is not necessary to know the condition of the companions rather every companion is classed as being trustworthy and righteous. 

All the companions are righteous by consensus of Ahlus Sunna wal Jama’a

2) It is not harmful to the authenticity of the hadith if the companion (Radi Allahu Anhu) is unknown.

3) There is a great reward in being patient.

4) Pious children are the cause for parents gaining reward.

5) For a more detailed explanation and further benefits see hadith no.15

Aspects Of Goodness In Hardship

[93] From Malik that Muhammad ibn Abdullah ibn Abi Sa’sa said that he heard Sa’id ibn Yasar Abu’l Hubab say that he had heard Abu Hurairah (Radi Allahu Anhu) say, “The Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, ‘Allah afflicts the one (in his health or wealth) for whom He desires good.'”

[GRADING] – [IT’S CHAIN IS SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] – [BUKHARI]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 2/941 hadith no.1816, book 50, chapter 3, hadith no.7), at-Tamhid (13/119) and he said, “This hadith is [Sahih] authentic”, al-Istidhkar: (no.1751)

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.5645) from the hadeeth of Malik

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) A’isha (Radi Allahu Anha) narrated that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, “No calamity befalls a Muslim but that Allah expiates some of his sins because of it, even though it were the prick he receives from a thorn.” (Sahih Bukhari no.5640 and Sahih Muslim no.2572)  

In one narration the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, “No fatigue, nor disease, nor sorrow, nor sadness, nor hurt, nor distress befalls a Muslim, even if it were the prick he receives from a thorn, but that Allah expiates some of his sins for that.” (Sahih Bukhari no.5641, no.5642 and Sahih Muslim no.2573)

2) Suhaib ar-Rumi (Radi Allahu Anhu) narrated that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, “Strange (i.e. lovely) are the ways of a believer for there is good in every affair of his and this is not the case with anyone else except in the case of a believer for if he has an occasion to feel delight, he thanks (Allah), thus there is a good for him in it, and if he gets into trouble and shows resignation (and endures it patiently), there is a good for him in it.” (Sahih Muslim no.2999 [no.7500])

Therefore the humankind should always be thankful and patient whenever doing anything. Allah the Most High says, “…And Allah loves those who are patient” (al-Imran no.146)

3) It is not correct to attribute every difficulty that befalls a believer to be a punishment, sometimes a difficulty is the cause for a believer to have his status elevated.

The Virtue of Qiyami Ramadhan (Tarawih, Tahajud)

[29] And with the same chain of narration from Abu Hurayrah that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) said, “Whoever stands in Ramadhan with belief and the intention of receiving reward, then all his previous sins will be forgiven.” 

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri clarified hearing (this hadith from Humayd Ibn Abd ar-Rahman Ibn Awf) in Nasa’i ((3/201,202) hadith (no.1604))

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 1/113 hadith no.247 lengthy, book 6, chapter 1, hadith no.2 from the hadith of Ibn Shihab from Abu Salamah Ibn Abdurahman Ibn Awf from Abu Hurayrah) at-Tamhid (7/95), al-Istidhkar: (no.218, no.219)   

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.2009) and Muslim (173/759) from the hadith of Malik.

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]

1) Here the meaning of Qiyam (standing) is Qiyam of Ramadhan (Tarawih, Tahajud). The noble Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) said about the Qiyam Ramadhan that, “Indeed, whoever stands (praying) with the Imam until he finished, then it is recorded for him that he prayed the whole night.” (Sunan at-Tirmidhi: (no.806) and its chain is authentic and Ibn Khuzaimah authenticated it: (no.2206), and Ibn Hibban, al-Muwarid: (no.919) and Ibn al-Jarud: (no.403 and Tirmidhi said: this hadith is Hasan Sahih)

2) Umar (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) commanded Ubay Ibn Ka’b (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) and Tamim ad-Dari (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) to lead the people in prayer in 11 rakats. (Muwatta Imam Malik Yahya’s narration 1/115 hadith no.249 and its chain is authentic, and Nimawi said in Athar as-Sunan: (no.775) “And its chain is authentic” and Tahawi referenced it in Ma’ani al-Athar (1/293)

3) Sa’ib Ibn Zayd (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) said, “In the lifetime of Umar Ibnul Khattab (RadhiAllaahu Anhu) we used to stand (Qiyam Ramadhan, Tarawih, Tahajud) for 11 rakahs. (Sunan Sa’id Ibn Mansur referenced by al-Hawi lil-Fatwa (1/349) and its chain is authentic)

In comparison to this the narration of Khalid Ibn Mukhlid (Ma’rifatus Sunan Wal Athar (2/305) hadith (no.1365) is [Shadh] (weak).

4) Tahtawi Hanafi writes, “Because without a shadow of a doubt the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) did not pray 20 (rakahs) rather he prayed 8 rakahs. (Hashiyyah Tahtawi Alal Durul Mukhtar (1/295)

5) Abu Bakr Ibn al-Arabi al-Maliki (died 543H) said, “And what is correct is that 11 rakahs should be prayed (this is) the prayer and standing of the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) and all the number (of rakahs) other than from this have no basis.” (Aridhatul Ahwadhi Sharh Sunan at-Tirmidhi (4/19))

6) The reward for qiyam (standing) is conditional to belief and sincerity. Also see my book “ Ta’dad Rakat Qiyam Ramadhan Ka Tahqiqi Ja’izah (An Overview of the verification of the number of rakahs for Qiyam Ramadhan)”
