Fasting Whilst Travelling Is An Option

Chapter of  two (narrators whose names begin with the letter) Haa for both of them of Humayd at-Tawil from Anas: Six hadith

[147] From Malik from Humayd at-Tawil that Anas ibn Malik (Radi Allahu Anhu) said, “We once travelled with the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam), in Ramadhan, and those who were fasting did not find fault with those who were not, and those who were not fasting did not find fault with those who were.”

[GRADING] – [IT’S CHAIN IS SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] – [BUKHARI]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 1/295 hadith no.661, book 18, chapter 7, hadith no.23), at-Tamhid (2/169, and he said, “This hadith is [Muttasil Sahih] authentically connected), al-Istidhkar: (no.611)

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.1947) from the hadith of Malik, and Muslim (99/118) from the hadith of Humayd at-Tawil and he has clarified hearing from him with it.

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) It is allowed to either fast or not fast whilst travelling. If one does not fast whilst travelling in Ramadhan then the individual should make up the missed fasts later on. It is more virtuous to not fast whilst travelling on the condition that it is severely hot and there are severe difficulties.

2) Also see hadith no.465

3) Abdullah Ibn Umar (Radi Allahu Anhu) did not fast whilst travelling. (Muwatta Imam Malik 1/295, hadith no.663 and it’s chain is [Sahih])

Whilst Urwah Ibn Zubair used to fast whilst travelling. (Muwatta Imam Malik 1/295, hadith no.664 and it’s chain is [Sahih])

4) If there are two allowable ways of carrying out a deed that have been established in the Sharia then one should not condemn the other person. (T.N. for example if person A is fasting whilst travelling and person B is not fasting whilst travelling, then person A should not condemn person B and vice versa, since both methods been established in the Sharia). 

 5) It is absolutely correct to refute those things which go against the Qur’an and Sunnah.

 The Virtue Of Mu’awiya (Radi Allahu Anhu) And The Virtue Of Um Haram Bint Milhan (Radi Allahu Anha)

[117] From Malik from Ishaq Ibn Abdillah Ibn Abi Talha from Anas Ibn Malik (Radi Allahu Anhu) that Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) used to visit Um Haram bint Milhan (Radi Allahu Anha), who would offer him meals. Um Haram (Radi Allahu Anha) was the wife of Ubada bin As-Samit (Radi Allahu Anhu). Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam), once visited her and she provided him with food and started looking for lice in his head. Then Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) slept, and afterwards woke up smiling. Um Haram (Radi Allahu Anha) asked, “What causes you to smile, O Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam)?” He (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said. “Some of my followers who (in a dream) were presented before me as fighters in Allah’s cause (on board a ship) amidst this sea caused me to smile; they were as kings on the thrones (or like kings on the thrones).” (Ishaq, a sub-narrator is not sure as to which expression the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) used.) Um Haram (Radi Allahu Anha) said, “O Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam)! Invoke Allah that he makes me one of them. Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) invoked Allah for her and slept again and woke up smiling. Once again Um Haram (Radi Allahu Anha) asked, “What makes you smile, O Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam)?” He (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) replied, “Some of my followers were presented to me as fighters in Allah’s Cause,” repeating the same dream. Um Haram (Radi Allahu Anha) said, “O Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam)! Invoke Allah that He makes me one of them.” He (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa SAllam) said, “You are amongst the first ones.” It happened that she sailed on the sea during the Caliphate of Mu’awiya bin Abi Sufyan (Radi Allahu Anhu), and after she disembarked, she fell down from her riding animal and died.

[GRADING] – [IT’S CHAIN IS SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]
Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (2/464, 465 hadith no.1026, book 21, chapter 18, hadith no.39), at-Tamhid (1/225) and al-Istidhkar (no.963) 

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.2788, no.2789) and Muslim (160/1912) from the hadith of Malik 

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) Um Haram and Um Sulaim were sisters (Radi Allahu Anhuma). Ibn Abdul Bar said, “I think that Um Haram or Um Sulaim (Radi Allahu Anhuma) breast fed the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) meaning one of them was his (Sallahu Alayhi Wa Sallams) foster mother and the other was the foster aunty. See at-Tamhid (1/226) the same thing was mentioned by Imam Abdullah Ibn Wahb al-Misri and other than him. 

2) If a woman uses her husband’s wealth to feed someone and the husband does not show any signs of being displeased then this food is halal, and the woman’s husband also receives the reward. 

3) Performing Jihad against the disbelievers at sea and making navy ships is a highly virtuous act to do. 

4) It is allowed for women to participate in Jihad.

5) It is allowed for men and women to travel by sea.

6) It is allowed for every Muslim to perform Jihad under the leader, whether he is a king or Khalifah etc.,  until the day of Judgement. 

7) This hadith shows the great virtue of Mu’awiya Ibn Abi Sufyan the one who wrote down the revelation. This great Jihad campaign was carried out under his supervision and the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) was happy about it. 

8) The reward for someone who is martyred in the path of Allah or someone who dies due to a medical reason in the path of Allah is the same and according to some scholars the martyr receives more reward.  

9) According to some scholars the Jihad performed at sea is more virtuous then the Jihad performed on land and this is seen to be the most correct view in this era therefore the Muslims should make a great amount of effort in manufacturing the best navy ships. 

10) It is mentioned in another version of this narration which has an authentic chain that the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) slept in the early afternoon i.e. he performed a siesta and that Um Haram carried out the Jihad at sea with her husband Ubadah Ibn Samit (Radi Allahu Anhuma). 

Further benefits from this hadith are as follows:

1) This battle campaign and Jihad at sea of Mu’awiya (Radi Allahu Anhu) was during the Caliphate of Uthman (Radi Allahu Anhu) and Um Haram (Radi Allahu Anha) died at a place called Qabras. 

2) The dreams of the Prophets (Alayhimusalam) are revelation. 

3) This hadith is also a proof of Muhamad (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam’s) Prophethood. The Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) explained about some of the unseen things which happened after his death and was witnessed by peoples eyes. 

4) Those narrations which mention that it is prohibited to travel by sea are all weak and rejected. 

5) Having the desire to participate in good deeds implies you have Iman. 

6) It is allowed to ask someone to make dua for you when it comes to good deeds.

Combining The Prayers Other Than Because Of Fear And Travelling

Sa’id Ibn Jubair: One hadith

[109] From Malik from Abu az-Zubair al-Makki from Sa’id Ibn Jubair from Abdullah Ibn Abbas (Radi Allahu Anhu) that he said: The Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) prayed the noon (Zuhr) and afternoon (Asr) prayers together, and the sunset (Magrib) and Isha’ prayers together without being in a state of fear or in a state of travelling.

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] – [MUSLIM]
Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (1/144 hadith no.328, book 9, chapter 1, hadith no.4), at-Tamhid (12/209) and he said,”This hadith has an [Sahih] authentic and established chain” and al-Istidhkar (no.301) 

Transmitted by Muslim (no.705) from the hadith of Malik and Abu az-Zubair clarified hearing this hadith from Sa’id Ibn Jubair in it (51/705)

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) Leaving aside a strange group, the scholars have an Ijma that it is not allowed to combine the prayers in the area which you are residing in. See at-Tamhid (12/210) 

2) It is allowed to combine prayers during the following circumstances:

Travelling, Hajj, Rain, during a battle against the disbelievers, whilst being in the state of fear, a sharia compliant excuse such as when a severe mass killing (anarchy) is happening and during a severe illness etc. 

3) For combining prayers whilst travelling see the previous hadith no.108 and hadith no.199. Additionally see the al-Hadith Magazine Hazro: edition no.52 pg.17

4) For the combining of Dhuhr and Asr during Hajj see Sahih Muslim (no.1218, Darusalam numbering no.2950) and Sahih Bukhari (hadith no.1662)

5) It is allowed to combine the prayers when it is raining. Abdullah Ibn Umar (Radi Allahu Anhu) used to pray with the leaders when they combined the Magrib and Esha prayer together due to rain. (al-Muwatta 1/145 hadith no.329 and its chain is [Sahih] authentic)

Combining Two Prayers Whilst Travelling

Abu at-Tufail: One hadith

[108] From Malik from Abu az-Zubair (Muhamad Ibn Muslim Ibn Tadrus) al-Makki from Abu at-Tufail Amir Ibn Wathila that Mu’adh Ibn Jabal (Radi Allahu Anhu) reported that he went along with Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) in the year of the expedition of Tabuk (for Jihad) and the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) combined the noon (Dhuhr) and afternoon (Asr) prayers together and the sunset (Magrib) and night (Esha) prayers together. 

Then he (Mu’adh Ibn Jabal (Radi Allahu Anhu)) said, on the other day he (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) deferred the prayers; he then came out (of his tent) and offered the noon (Dhuhr) and afternoon (Asr) prayers together. He then went in (the tent) and (later on) came out and then after that offered the sunset (Magrib) and night (Esha) prayers together and then he (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said “Allah willing, you will reach the fountain of  Tabuk by tomorrow and you will not be able to come to that until it is dawn, and he who happens to reach there should not touch its water until I come.”

Mu’adh Ibn Jabal (Radi Allahu Anhu) then said, “We came to that and two persons (amongst) us reached that fountain before us and it was a thin flow of water like the shoelace. Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) asked them both, “Have any of you touched the water from this fountain?” They said: Yes. So Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) scolded them, and he said to them what Allah wanted him to say. The people then took water of the fountain in their palms until it became somewhat significant and filled it into some container. Then Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) washed his hands and his face too in it, and then, took that water and put it in that (fountain) and there gushed forth abundant water from that fountain, until all the people drank to their fill. He (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) then said, “O Mu’adh, it is hoped that if you live long you would see its water irrigating  the gardens in this area well.”

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] – [MUSLIM]
Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (1/143, 144 hadith no.326, book 9, chapter 1, hadith no.2), at-Tamhid (12/193, 194) and he said,”this hadith is established as being [Sahih] authentic.” and al-Istidhkar (no.296) 

Transmitted by Muslim (10/706 after hadith no.2281) from the hadith of Malik and Abu az-Zubair clarified hearing this hadith from Abu at-Tufail Amir Ibn Wathila in Musnad Ahmad (5/229) and Ibn Khuzaima (no.966) 

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) It is allowed to combine the prayers whilst travelling as follows, Dhuhr and Asr (two rak’ah + two rak’ah) and Magrib and Esha (three rak’ah and two rak’ah). 

2) The Khalifa can participate in the battle with his soldiers and army against the disbelievers.  

3) The battle of Tabuk was against the Christians of Sham who wanted to attack the Muslims. Some people of knowledge have said that this occurred in the month of Rajab in the ninth year of Hijra. See at-Tamhid (12/196)

4) From this hadith it is known that the traveler can combine prayers whilst he is lodging during his journey. See at-Tamhid (12/196)

5) The meaning of combining early is to pray Asr at the time of Dhuhr time and to pray Esha at the time of Magrib. The meaning of combining late is to pray the Dhuhr at the time of Asr and to pray the Magrib at the time of Esha. The meaning of combining formally is to pray the Dhuhr at the end time of Dhuhr and to pray the Asr at the beginning time of Asr and combine this. Likewise to pray the Magrib at the end time of Magrib and to pray the Esha at the beginning time of Esha. These are the three types of combinations which are allowed for combining prayers. Accepting only one of these types and rejecting the other two is incorrect.

6) This hadith mentions a great miracle of the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam). The miracle was that Allah made an active fountain of water occur due to the blessed wudhu water of the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam). Centuries later Muhamad Ibn Wadah saw this fountain. See at-Tamhid (12/208 and its chain is [Sahih] authentic) 

7) The Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) used to inform about the unseen matters through the revelation of Allah. Ibn Abdul Bar said, “And in this is that the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) used to inform about the unseen things (through revelation) which would occur after him and this is not strange and neither was this an unknown due to his status.  May Allah elevate his (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) remembrance. ” at-Tamhid (12/208) 

Remember that the One who knows the unseen is a specific attribute of Allah alone.

8) It is never allowed to go against the guidance and command of the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam). 

9) Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri asked Salim Ibn Abdullah Ibn Umar, Can you join Dhuhr and Asr when travelling?” He said, “Yes, there is no harm in that. Haven’t you seen the people (combine and) pray on Arafah?” (Muwatta Imam Malik 1/145 hadith no.330 and its chain is [Sahih] authentic) 

10) Also see hadith no.109 and no.199

 The Hadith Is Also Part Of The Book Of Allah (Quran)

A man from the family of Khalid Ibn Usayd: One Hadith

[84] from Malik from Ibn Shihab from a man (Umaya Ibn Abdullah Ibn Khalid) of the family of Khalid ibn Usayd that he said to Abdullah ibn Umar (Radi Allahu anhu), “Abu Abdur-Rahman, we find the fear prayer and the prayer when settled mentioned in the Qur’an, but we do not find any mention of the travelling prayer in it.” Ibn Umar (Radi Allahu Anhu) said, “Son of my brother! Allah the Mighty and Majestic sent us Muhammad (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam), and we knew nothing. We only do as we saw him doing.”  

The hadith from Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri have been completed. These are 84 ahadith.

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] – 

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 1/145,146 hadith no.332, book 9, chapter 2, hadith no.7), at-Tamhid (11/161), al-Istidhkar: (no.303)

Transmitted by al-Hakim al-Kabir in Awali Malik (no.209) from the hadith of Malik and narrated by Muhammad Ibn Abdullah ash-Sha’ithi from Umaya Ibn Abdullah Ibn Khalid Ibn Usayd that Ibn Umar said to him (the hadith), transmitted by an-Nasa’i (1/226 hadith no.458) and its chain is [Hasan] good, and Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri clarified hearing this hadith from Abdul Malik Ibn Abi Bakr Ibn Abdurahman with Bayhaqi (3/136) and Ya’qub Ibn Sufyan al-Farisi in al-Ma’rifah and at-Taarikh (1/372) and the hadith has another route with Abi Dawud (no.457) and Nasa’i (no.1435) and Ibn Maja (no.1066) and its chain is [Hasan] good and Ibn Khuzaima authenticated it (no.946) and Ibn Hiban (al-Mawarid: no.101) and al-Hakim (1/258) and adh-Dhahabi agreed with him.

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) Whether you are in a state of fear or security, it is still sunna to shorten the prayers whilst travelling. 

2)  The hadith is the explanation and commentary of the Quran. Therefore saying about an authentic hadith that it is not in the Quran or that it goes against the Quran is a false principle and an incorrect understanding.  

3) From other evidences it is also established that praying the full units of prayer whilst travelling is also allowed. For example see Sunan an-Nasa’i (3/121 hadith no.1457, and its chain is [Sahih] authentic, al-‘Ala Ibn Zuhair is Thiqa and there is no Shudhudh in the narration (i.e. the narration does not contradict  a more authentic one)
