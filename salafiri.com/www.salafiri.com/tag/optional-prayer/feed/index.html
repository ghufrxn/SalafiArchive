It Is Allowed To Perform The Optional Prayers In Congregation Sometimes

[115] From Malik from Ishaq Ibn Abdillah Ibn Abi Talha from Anas Ibn Malik (Radi Allahu Anhu) that my grandmother Mulaika (Radi Allahu Anha) invited Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) for a meal which she had prepared specially for him. He (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) ate some of it and said, “Get up. I shall lead you in the prayer.” I brought a mat that had become black owing to excessive use and I sprinkled water on it. Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) stood on it and prayed two rakat; and the orphan was with me (in the first row), and the old lady (Mulaika (Radi Allahu Anha)) stood behind us.

[GRADING] – [IT’S CHAIN IS SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]
Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (1/153 hadith no.359, book 9, chapter 9, hadith no.31), at-Tamhid (1/263) and al-Istidhkar (no.329) 

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.860) and Muslim (no.658) from the hadith of Malik 

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) During Salah the men should be in the first row behind the Imam and the women should stand in the row behind the men.

2) If the first row does not have men then it is allowed for children to stand there. Rather it is established as being a Sunnah from this hadith. 

3) It is not allowed for men and women to stand in the same row next to each other to pray Salah.

4) Apart from the accepting invitations for a Walimah, it is also Sunnah to accept other invitations as long as you do not have a Sharia compliant excuse.  

5) When one Imam and two people are reading the Salah together, then the Imam will stand at the front separately (and the two people will stand behind). 

6) The meaning of ‘the old lady’ who is mentioned in the hadith is the grandmother Mulaikah or Um Sulaim (Radi Allahu Anhuma). And Allah knows best

7) From this hadith and other ahadith it is established that if one woman stands on her own behind the row of men during congregational Salah then her Salah will be valid. However if a man stands on his own behind the row in the congregational Salah to pray then his Salah will not be valid and it will be obligatory for him to repeat his Salah.

8) There is no difference in opinion meaning there is an ijma on the issue that if there are two men and one woman who are performing the Salah together then one man will stand on the right side of the Imam and the woman will stand on her own behind them. This hadith was about the optional duha prayer therefore it establishes the fact that it is allowed to sometimes perform the optional prayers in congregation.  

9) Generally it is allowed to perform the Salah in the house on a spread out rug or mat etc. on the condition that it is clean.

Half Of The Reward Will Be Received If One Sits Down And Prays

Chapter of the seven alif, to all of them is 19 ahadith. Isma’il Ibn Muhamad Ibn Sa’d Ibn Abi Waqas: One hadith 

[112] From Malik from Isma’il Ibn Muhamad Ibn Sa’d Ibn Abi Waqas from the mawla of Amr Ibn al-‘As or Abdullah Ibn Amr from Abdullah Ibn Amr Ibn al-As (Radi Allahu Anhu) that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, “The reward for the prayer of one of you who sits down and prays (the optional prayer) is half the amount for the one received for the prayer of one of you who stands up and prays (the optional prayer).”

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] – 
Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (1/136 hadith no.305, book 8, chapter 6, hadith no.19), at-Tamhid (1/131), and he authenticated it, and al-Istidhkar (no.375) 

Transmitted by al-Johari in Musnad al-Muwatta (no.271) from the hadith of Malik and there is an Ila (hidden defect) in it, and the hadith has an authentic witnessing report in Sahih Muslim (no.735) and this authenticates the hadith. wal-Hamdullilah  

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) The one who prays the optional prayer standing up has twice as virtuous then the one who sits down and prays the optional prayer. 

2) There is an Ijma that the obligatory prayer of one who does not stand up and pray whilst he has the ability to do so becomes invalid and it is obligatory for him to repeat his prayer. For the individual who is incapable of standing then the obligation of standing to pray becomes waived and is not applicable for him. 

3) There is an Ijma that standing in the obligatory prayer is an obligation for the one who has the ability and in the optional prayer he has a choice. There is an exception to this rule which is established from authentic ahadith that mentions if the Imam sits down to perform the obligatory prayer due to a sharia compliant excuse then all the people praying behind the Imam will sit down and pray even if they have the ability to stand. See the previous hadith no.1. For the Ijma on performing the obligatory prayer standing see at-Tamhid (1/136)

4) How should an individual sit down and pray if they have to due to a sharia compliant excuse? Some scholars say that the individual should sit down in the whole prayer by being in the tashahud position and act on the the pillars of the salah and the sunan of the salah for example the prostration will be much lower than the bowing. Some scholars say that in the standing position the individual will sit on four joints and during the tashahud position the person will sit as per the tashahud. Some scholars say that the individual will pray by sitting on ones backside and have the knees standing. From all these sayings only the first one is most correct. One should remember that you should perform the tawrak in the last rak’ah if it is possible just as it is establisehd from the Sunnah.  

5) Nowadays there are some people who perform the optional prayer by sitting down deliberately without any sharia compliant excuse. Whilst there is no sharia compliant proof for doing this type of behavior rather this is a means of getting a decrease in reward.    

6) See hadith no.455

Qiyam Ramadan (Tarawih, Tahajud) and Other Optional Prayers Can be Performed in Congregation Sometimes

[36] And with the same chain of narration that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) prayed in the mosque one night and people prayed behind him. Then he prayed the next night and there were more people. Then they gathered on the third or fourth night and the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) did not come out to them. In the morning, he said, “I saw what you were doing and the only thing that prevented me from coming out to you was that I feared that it would become obligatory (fard) for you.” This happened in Ramadan.

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri clarified hearing (this hadith from Urwa Ibn az-Zubayr) in Bukhari (no.924)

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 1/113 hadith no.246, book 6, chapter 1, hadith no.1) at-Tamhid (8/108), al-Istidhkar: (no.217)    

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.1129) and Muslim (no.761) from the hadith of Malik.

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]

1) The tarawih prayer is sunnah and not wajib (obligatory) or fard (compulsory). (Sahih Muslim (174/759) and Muwatta Yahya’s narration (1/113) hadith (no.247))

2) Since the fear of (it) being made compulsory in not applicable, the Tarawi prayer in a group is more virtuous. See hadith (no.29) understanding point: (no.1)

3) There is ijma that there is no adhan or iqamah for the optional [nawafil] (prayers) (Tarawi etc.). (at-Tamhid (8/108))

4) The Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) was very compassionate and caring (sympathetic) towards his Umma.

5) There is a big difference (of opinion) of the noble scholars on the number of (rakas of) Tarawi. See at-Tamhid (8/113) and Umda ul-Qari of Ayni (11/126), Hawi lil- Fatawa (1/348), Mufham li Mashakil Min Talkhis Kitab Muslim (2/389) and Sunan at-Tirmidhi (no.806) Qurtubi (died 686 hijri) said, “And most of the scholars have said that 11 rakas should be prayed, they have used the previous (mentioned) hadith as proof in this (issue). (al-Mufham (2/390)

6) The claim of 20 rakas being ijma is false. Imam Shafi’i said, “if the rakas are less and the standing is long then it is better and this is what i prefer. (Summarised from Maqrizi’s Mukhtasar Qiyam ul Layl of al-Marwazi (pg.202, 203), An overview verification of the number of rakas of Qiyam Ramadhan (pg.85)

7) This hadith shows the passion (and zeal) of the noble companions (Radhi Allahu Anhum) for worship.

The Manner of Performing the Optional [Nafl) Raka’hs

as-Sa’ib Ibn Yazid: One hadith

[7]      From Malik from Ibn Shihab from as-Sa’ib Ibn Yazid from al-Muttalib Ibn AbI Wadaa as-Sahmi that Hafsa, the wife of the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhee Was-Sallam) said, “I never saw the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhee Was-Sallam) praying nawaafil sitting, until a year before his death, when he began to pray them sitting. He would recite the surah with a measured slowness so that it would seem to be longer than other Surahs which were actually longer than it.”

[GRADING] – [SAHEEH]

Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri clarified hearing this hadeeth from Sa”ib Ibn Yazid (RadhiAllaahu Anhu)) in Ibn Hibbaan (al-Ihsaan: (no.2521),or: (no.2530))

[REFERENCES] – [MUSLIM]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (1/137 hadeeth no.307, book 8, chapter 7, hadeeth no.21  at-Tamheed (6/220) and al-Istidhkaar (no.277)

Transmitted by Muslim (no.733) from the hadeeth of Maalik.

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADEETH

1.Hafidh Ibn Abdul Barr said, “In the Arabic language the basis of salah is dua, however the Shariah name is  superior because it is decisive over its linguistic meaning.” [At-Tamheed [6/221]

2. Praying Nafl (optional) standing is more virtuous, praying them whilst purposely sitting down without any valid legislated reason in the Shari’ah is not indicative of reward.

3. The Majestic Qur’aan should be recited slowly, in a good way, with consideration, concern and pondering.

4. Standing whilst praying the nafl should be long and lengthy according to ones capability.

5. It is highly recommended to recite the Quran abundantly in the Nafl prayer.

6. It is permissible to sit and pray if their is a legislated reason.

7. There is only half the reward for praying whilst sitting however our Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wasallam) would receive the full reward for praying whilst sitting.
