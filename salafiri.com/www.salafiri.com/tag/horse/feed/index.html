The Meat of Domestic Donkeys

[371] Narrated Jabir bin `Abdullah (Radi Allahu Anhu):

The Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) prohibited eating domestic donkey meat and allowed the eating of horse flesh. (Narrated by Bukhari)

And (narrated) by Muslim (by Jabir bin ‘Abdullah) We ate during the time of Khaibar the (flesh) of horses and of wild donkeys, but Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) prohibited us (to eat) the flesh of domestic donkeys.

[GRADING] – [NARRATED BY BUKHARI]

[REFERENCES]

Transmitted by

Bukhari, Book of Hunting, Slaughtering, Chapter: The meat of domestic donkeys, [no. 5520],

Muslim, Book of The Book of Hunting, Slaughter, and what may be Eaten, Chapter: Chapter: The permissibility of eating horse meat, [no.1941]

Abu Dawud [no.3788], Nasai [7:210], Ahmad [3:361], Baihaqi [9:326], Darimi [no.1993], Ibn al-Jarud [no.885]

Also see the previous hadith.

[RULINGS FROM THE HADITH]

1) It is unlawful i.e. haram to eat the meat of domestic donkeys. see also the hadith of Amr ibn Shuʿayb with a similar meaning. Ahmad [2:219], Abu Dawud [no.3811], Nasai [7:239], Baihaqi [9:333], Hakim [2:103]. There are also numerous narrations in this regard also in Ibn Hibban and Ahmad.

2) Some hadith mention the reason it is impermissible to eat their meat is because they are unclean and unhygienic animals [Bukhari no.5528, 4198] Muslim [no.1940]. The hadith of Amr ibn Shuʿayb above mentions also animals that eat filth. 

3) The hadith of Bukhari [no.5527], Muslim [no.1936] mentions when eating the meat of domestic donkeys was announced the companions were cooking the meat at the very instance and whilst demonstrating great love and understanding of this Dīn and reverential love for the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wasallam); they drained and spilt their dishes.

4) Both cooked and uncooked meat of donkeys is unlawful as per the hadith of Bara (Radi Allahu Anhu) Bukhari [no.4226] 

5) It is lawful and thus permissible to eat horse (meat). See previous hadith no.370

6) It is permissible however to eat the meat of wild donkeys. See Muslim [no.1194, 1196]

The Permissibility of Eating Horse Meat

[370] Narrated Asma bint Abu Bakr (Radiullahu Anhuma)
We slaughtered a horse during the lifetime of the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) and ate it. And in another narration she said, “and we were in Madina”

[GRADING] – [NARRATED BY BUKHARI]

[REFERENCES]

Transmitted by

Bukharee, Book of Hunting, Slaughtering, Chapter: An-Nahr and Adh-Dhabh [no.5510]

Muslim, Book of The Book of Hunting, Slaughter, and what may be Eaten, Chapter: The permissibility of eating horse meat [no.1942]

Nasai [7:227], Ibn Majah [no.3190], Ahmad [6:345]

[RULINGS FROM THE HADITH]

1) This hadith proves that the meat of horses is halal and thus permissible to eat

2) The hadith of Jabir (RadhiAllahu Anhu) is a Qawli hadith in which the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wasallam) gave permission for horse meat to be consumed  and in the wording of Bukhari it mentions the word Rakhasa i.e. if need be instead of explicit permission. [Bukhari [4219], Muslim [1941], Abu Dawud [3788], Nasai [7:210], Ahmad [3:361], Baihaqi [9:326], Darimi [1993], Ibn al-Jarud [885] . See the next hadith of this chapter i.e. no,371

3) Imams Ahmad, Shafi, Abu Yusuf, Muhammad Shaybani held it is permissible and lawful to eat where as Imams Abu Hanifah and Malik were of the position it is unlawful i.e. haram to eat. [Subl al-Salam [4:1824] 

4) This incident took place in Madinah after Jihad was textually obligated as other narrations elucidate Asma (RadhiAllahu Anha) said we were in Madinah.

5) Animals that one can make Dhahb one can also make Nahr of them and vice versa and it is recommended to make Nahr of Camels only and dhahb of other animals.

Ill Luck (Bad Luck) and Bad Omens are Nothing (Don’t Exist)

Hamzah and Salim: One Hadith

[61] From Malik from Ibn Shihab from Hamzah and Salim Ibn Abdullah Ibn Umar from Abdullah Ibn Umar (Radi Allahu Anhu) that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) said, “Bad luck (misfortune) (if it exists then) is in a house, a woman and a horse.”

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri clarified hearing (this hadith from Salim and Hamzah Ibn Abdullah Ibn Umar) in Bukhari (no.5772)

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]

Al-Muwatta ((Yahya’s narration) 2/972 hadith no.1883, book 54, chapter 8, hadith no.22) at-Tamhid (9/278) al-Istidhkar: (no.1819)  

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.5093) and Muslim (no.2225) from the hadith of Malik.

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]

1) The foundation (and reasons behind) most general corruption and killing in the time of the noble Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) and before him was in 3 important things:
1. The house, meaning the land
2. Women
3. Horses, meaning cavalry (armies, troops)

Therefore the meaning of ill luck here (in this hadith) is the above however this hadith is abrogated because of other [Sahih] authentic hadith. The noble Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) said, “If superstition (misfortune) existed in anything then it would be in houses, women and horses.” (Sahih Bukhari (no.5094) and Sahih Muslim (no.2225) [no.5807, no.5808] The Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) said, “There is no superstition.” (Sahih Bukhari (no.5754), Sahih Muslim (no.2223) also see Fath ul-Bari (6/60-63 under hadith (no.2858, no.2859) and at-Tahmid (9/290) and he said, “Then this became abrogated and the Qur’an and Sunnah declared this to be false.”

2) The narration which has been transmitted in Muwatta Imam Malik that states one house will have numerous superstitions and less wealth, then the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) said (about it), “it is evil, leave it” (2/972 hadith (no.1884) the chain of narration is [Daif] weak because of it being [Munqati’] broken.

In Sunan Abu Dawud (no.3924) it is a weak narration because the chain of narration contains the tadlis of Ikrimah Ibn Amar the mudallis.

3) In one narration it has come that A’isha (Radi Allahu Anha) censured Abu Hurayrah (Radi Allahu Anhu) because of the hadith of Ibn Umar (Radi Allahu Anhu), this chain of narration is weak because it contains Qatadah the mudalis. The other chain of narration is [Munqati’] broken because of “Makhul From A’isha”

4) Also see hadith (no.412)
