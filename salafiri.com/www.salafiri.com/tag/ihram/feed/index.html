Dua At Safa And Marwa

[144] From Malik from Jafar ibn Muhammed ibn AIi from his father from Jabir ibn Abdullah (Radi Allahu Anhu) that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) used to say (the takbir), “Allah is the Greatest” three times when he stopped on Safa, and “There is no diety worthy of worship in truth except Allah, alone, without any partner. To Him belong the Kingdom and praise, and He has power over everything” three times, and make dua. He would then do the same on Marwa.

[GRADING] – [IT’S CHAIN IS SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] 

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (1/372 hadith no.847, book no.20, chapter no.41, hadith no.127), at-Tamhid (2/91), al-Istidhkar no.795

Transmitted by an-Nasa’i (5/240 hadith no.2975) from the hadith of Ibn ul-Qasim from Malik and narrated by Muslim (no.1218) from the hadith of Jafar Ibn Muhamad. 

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) One should go on Safa and Marwa whilst being able to see the house of Allah (Ka’bah) as this is the sunnah. 

2) There are seven circuits (laps) whilst doing the Safa and Marwa Sa’i. 

(1) From Safa to Marwa (2) From Marwa to Safa (3) From Safa to Marwa (4) From Marwa to Safa (5) From Safa to Marwa (6) From Marwa to Safa (7) From Safa to Marwa 

The seventh circuit is the last one. After this the person who is performing Umrah can cut or shave their hair and take off their Ihram. The person performing Hajj Ifrad or Hajj Qiran becomes free from the restrictions of Ihram after throwing the pebbles at the Jamarat and then performing the Qurbani or shaving the hair. 

3) Abdullah ibn Umar (Radi Allahu Anhu) used to make dua on Safa saying, “O Allah, You have said, ‘call on Me – I will answer you’ and You do not break Your promise. So I am asking You, in the same way that You have guided me to Islam, not to take it away from me, and that You make me die while I am muslim.”(Muwatta Imam Malik 1/373 hadith no.848 and it’s chain is [Sahih] authentic). From this it is known that making other duas at Safa and Marwa is allowed.

If One Forgets or Leaves Out to do a Necessary Action During Hajj then Dam (Sacrificing a Goat) is Necessary

Esa Ibn Talha: One Hadith

[66] From Malik, from Ibn Shihab from Esa Ibn Talha that Abdullah Ibn Amr Ibn al-As said, “The Messenger of Allah, (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) stopped for the people at Mina, and they questioned him and a man came and said to him, ‘Messenger of Allah, I was unclear about what to do and I shaved before sacrificing,’ and the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, ‘Sacrifice, and don’t worry.’ Then another came to him and said ‘Messenger of Allah, I was unclear about what to do and I sacrificed before throwing the stones.’ He advised, ‘Throw, and don’t worry.’ “

Amr continued saying, that day whatever the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) was asked about in which it was delayed or done early then he replied do it and there is no problem.

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri clarified (hearing this hadith from Esa Ibn Talhah Ibn Ubaydillah) with Bukhari (no.1738)

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 1/421 hadith no.970, book 20, chapter 81, hadith no.242), at-Tamhid (7/264) and he said, “this hadith is authentic”, al-Istidhkar: (no.911)        

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.83, no.1736) and Muslim (no.1306) from the hadith of Malik

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]

1) There is no problem if the stones have been thrown or the sacrifice is done late or early unknowingly, and there is no dam (sacrificing a goat) that is necessary.

2) If a necessary action is left after having knowledge about it, then dam is necessary. Ibn Abas (Radi Allahu Anhu) said, “Whoever forgets or doesn’t do a (necessary) action from Hajj or Umrah then that person has to do dam meaning sacrifice a goat and distribute (it) to the poor people in the Haram. (as-Sunan al-Kubra of al Bayhaqi 5/30 and its chain is authentic, al-Muwatta of Imam Malik 1/419, hadith no.968 and its chain is authentic)

3) A summary of one hadith is that if a person performing Hajj can’t perform the tawaf before the evening on the day of sacrifice (10 Dhul Hijjah) then all the (Ihram’s) bounding (required) actions have to performed again by him meaning he has to put the Ihram back on and do the visiting tawaf. See Sunan Abu Dawud (no.1999) and its chain is [Hasan] good and Ibn Khuzaimah has authenticated it (no.2958)

4) The people of knowledge should tell the people about knowledgeable things that are in accordance to the Quran, Sunnah and the Shar’iah compliant proofs during the days of Hajj (and additionally before and after it).

5) There is a consensus that the person performing Hajj and Umrah that is in the state of Ihram will not cut his hair or have it cut. If the hair has to be cut because of a Shar’iah compliant excuse then dam will need to be done.

6) It is best to cut (shave) the hair after throwing the stones at Jamarah Aqabah.

7) For more detailed issues about the Hajj and Umrah see my (translated and verified) book “the day and night of the person performing Hajj”

The Prohibition of Hunting Whilst in the State of Ihram

[53] And from the same chain of narration from Abdullah Ibn Abas, that as-Sab Ibn Jaththama al-Laythi once gave a wild ass (donkey) to the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) while he was at al-Abwa, or Wadan, and the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) gave it back to him. However, when the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) saw the expression on the man’s face he said, “We only gave it back to you because we are in Ihram.” 

[GRADING] – [SAHIH]

Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri clarified hearing (this hadith from Ubaydullah Ibn Abdullah Ibn Utba Ibn Mas’ud) in Bukhari (no.2592)

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 1/353 hadith no.801, book 20, chapter 25, hadith no.83) at-Tamhid (9/54) al-Istidhkar: (no.751)    

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.1825) and Muslim (no.1193) from the hadith of Malik.

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]

1) In the state of Ihram it is not permissible to hunt on land, or get someone to hunt, or buy a land hunted (animal). See at-Tamhid (9/58) and Sura al-Ma’ida (ayah: (no.96))

2) Whoever is not in Ihram if he hunts for himself and then afterwards gifts (the hunted animal) to those who are in Ihram then eating it is permitted. See this book hadith (no.492)

3) Abdullah Ibn Amir Ibn Rabi’a said, “I once saw Uthman ibn Afan in Ihram on a hot summer’s day at al-Arj,and he had covered his face with a red woolen cloth. Some game-meat was brought to him and he told his companions to eat. They said, ‘Will you not eat then?’, and he said, ‘I am not in the same position as you. It was hunted for my sake.’ “(because of this reason I don’t eat it) (al-Muwatta Abu Mus’ab az-Zuhri’s narration (1/452) hadith (no.1147), and its chain is authentic, and the hadith in another form Muwatta Yahya’s narration (1/354) hadith (no.802))

4) A’isha (Radi Allahu Anha) said, “If (in the state of Ihram) you get an urge to do something (meaning to eat the meat of the hunted animal), leave it,” by which she meant eating game-meat (al-Muwatta Yahya’s narration (1/354) hadith (no.803) and its chain is authentic)

5) If there is a fear of another brother falling into misunderstanding because of a certain task, then it should be clarified so that the heart stays clean and happy for each other.
