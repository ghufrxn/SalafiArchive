Reciting The Fatiha Behind The Imaam 

Abu Sa’ib Mawla Of Hisham Ibn Zuhrah: One Hadith

[139] From Malik from Al-‘Ala Ibn Abdurahman that he heard Aba Sa’ib Mawla of Hisham Ibn Zuhrah say I heard Abu Hurairah (Radi Allahu Anhu) say that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, “Whoever offers a prayer in which he does not recite Umm Al-Quran (Al Fatihah), it is deficient, it is deficient, it is deficient, incomplete.” I (Abu As-Sa’ib) said: ‘O Abu Hurairah, sometimes I am behind the Imam.’ He pulled my forearm and said: ‘Recite it to yourself, O Persian! For I heard the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) say: “Allah says: “I have divided prayer between Myself and My slave into two halves, and My slave shall have what he has asked for.'” The Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said: “Recite, for when the slave says: All the praises and thanks be to Allah, the Rab of all that exists, Allah says: ‘My slave has praised Me.’ And when he says: The Most Gracious, the Most Merciful, Allah says: ‘My slave has extolled Me.’ And when he says: The Only Owner (and the Only Ruling Judge) of the Day of Recompense (i.e. the Day of Resurrection), Allah says: ‘My slave has glorified Me’ . And when he says: You (alone) we worship, and You (alone) we ask for help (for each and everything), He says: ‘This is between Me and My slave, and My slave shall have what he has asked for.’ And when he says: ‘Guide us to the straight way, the way of those on whom You have bestowed Your grace, not (the way) of those who earned Your anger, nor of those who went astray, He says: ‘This is for My slave, and My slave shall have what he asked for.’

(The comprehensive Imam and author of this book) Abul Hasan (al-Qabisi) said, “The wording in this hadith has Itirab in from our (Muwatta’s) narrators. Due to this I have used the words of (Abul Hasan Ali Ibn Muhamad Ibn Masrur) ad-Dabagh except for the fact that this is based on the words of Esa (Ibn Miskin) and the manuscript was with ad-Dabagh, and this is the case. 

[GRADING] – [IT’S CHAIN IS SAHIH]

[REFERENCES] – [MUSLIM]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration (1/84, 85 hadith no.185, book 3, chapter 9, hadith no.39), at-Tamhid (20/187) al-Istidhkar (no.161)

Transmitted by Muslim (39/395) from the hadith of Malik. (T.N. Narrated by an-Nasa’i (no.909))

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) From this hadith it is known that Abu Hurairah (Radi Allahu Anhu) held the view that it is necessary to recite the Fatiha behind the Imam. In one narration it is mentioned that the student asked Abu Hurairah (Radi Allahu Anhu), “I am hearing the Imam’s recitation? So he replied, O Persian! Recite (the Fatiha) in your heart.” (Musnad Abi Awanah 2/128, and it’s chain is [Sahih])

Mulla Ali Qari said in his commentary to this, “Recite it quietly, do not recite it loudly.” (Mirqatul Mafatih 2/549 hadith no.823) 

There is a concensus that the meaning of recitation in this hadith is to recite it quietly and not loudly. See Kitab al-Qira’at of al-Bayhaqi (pg no.31) 

Shah Waliullah Dehlawi Hanafi said, “Meaning recite it quietly so that no one else can hear it apart from you.” (Maswa Musafa Sharh Muwatta vol no.1 pg no.106) 

From this it is known that those who say that the reciation is not movement of the lips and it is only reflecting and pondering over the recitation then this is incorrect. Abu Hurairah (Radi Allahu Anhu) said to his student, “When the Imam recites Surah al-Fatiha then you should recite it and finish it before the Imam.” (Juz Qir’at al-Bukhari no.337 and it’s chain is [Sahih] and an-Naymawi the blind follower (Taqlidi) said in Athar as-Sunan no.358 “It’s chain is [Hasan]“

It is known from this that recitation is not limited to only contemplating rather it is the physical act of moving the lips whilst reciting quietly otherwise what is the meaning of completing it before the Imam?

Apart from Abu Hurairah (Radi Allahu Anhu) the following Companions and Tabi’in held the view of reciting the Fatiha behind the Imam and also acted upon it:

  1. Umar (Radi Allahu Anhu)                          (Juz al-Qira’ah of al-Bukhari no.51 and it is [Sahih])
  2. Abu Sa’id al-Khudri (Radi Allahu Anhu) (Juz al-Qira’ah of al-Bukhari no.11, no.105 and it’s chain is  is [Hasan])
  3. Ubadah Ibn Samit (Radi Allahu Anhu) (Musanaf Ibn Abi Shaibah 1/375 hadith no.3770 and it’s chain is [Sahih])
  4. Ibn Abbas (Radi Allahu Anhu)               (Musanaf Ibn Abi Shaibah 1/375 hadith no.3773 and it is [Sahih])
  5. Anas (Radi Allahu Anhu)                        (Kitab al-Qira’ah of al-Bayhaqi no.231 and it’s chain is [Hasan])
  6. Sa’id Ibn Jubair                                        (Kitab al-Qira’ah of al-Bayhaqi no.237 and it’s chain is [Hasan])
  7. Hasan Basri                                               (as-Sunan al-Kubra of al-Bayhaqi 2/171 and it’s chain is [Sahih])
  8. Amir ash-Shu’bi                                       (Musanaf Ibn Abi Shaibah 1/374 hadith no.3764 and it’s chain is [Sahih])
  9. Abu Mulih Usamah Ibn Umair              (Musanaf Ibn Abi Shaibah 1/374 hadith no.3768 and it’s chain is [Sahih])
  10. Hakam Ibn Utaibah                                 (Musanaf Ibn Abi Shaibah 1/374 hadith no.3766 and it’s chain is [Sahih])

Imam Shafi’i, Imam Awza’i and Imam Bukhari and other than them held the view of reciting the Fatiha behind the Imam and also acted upon it. Therefore saying that it has been aborgated or that it goes against the Qur’an is incorrect. For further details see my book “al-Kawakib ad-Duriyah Fi Wujub al-Fatiha Khalf ul-Imam Fil Bahriyah” and “Nasrul Bari Fi Tahqiq Juz al-Qira’ah of al-Bukhari”

2) The Hadith is also revelation. 

3) The foundation of Salah is the Fatiha meaning without reciting it the Salah is invalid. 

4) The meaning of Hamd and Thana is Surah Fatiha. And other than that from the benefits.

 It Is Not Allowed To Go To An Area Where There Is A Plague And To Run Away From It (If You Are There When The Plague Breaks Out)

[87] From Malik from Muhammad ibn al-Munkadir and Abin-Nadr, the mawla of Umar ibn Ubaydillah from Amir ibn Sa’d ibn Abi Waqas from his father (Radi Allahu Anhu) that he asked Usamah ibn Zayd (Radi Allahu Anhu), “Have you heard anything from the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam), about the plague?” Usamah said, “The Messenger of Allah, (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam), said, ‘The plague is a punishment that was sent down on a party of the Bani Isra’il or whoever was before them. So when you hear of it in a land, do not enter it. If it comes upon a land and you are in it, do not depart in flight from it.”

Malik said that Abu an-Nadr said, “That is, do not depart with no other intention but flight.”

[GRADING] – [IT’S CHAIN IS SAHEEH]

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 2/896 hadith no.1721, book 45, chapter 7, hadith no.23), at-Tamhid (12/249, 21/183), al-Istidhkar: (no.1653)       

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.3473) and Muslim (no.2218) from the hadith of Malik

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]
1) A plague is a punishment for the non-belivers and those who reject the truth and is a mercy for the believers. The Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, “That it was a punishment sent by Allah on whom he wished, and Allah made it a source of mercy for the believers, for if one in the time of an epidemic plague stays in his country patiently hoping for Allah’s Reward and believing that nothing will befall him except what Allah has written for him, he will get the reward of a martyr.” (Sahih Bukhari no.3474) 

The Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) said, “Plague is the cause of martyrdom of every Muslim (who dies because of it).” (Sahih Bukhari no.2830 and Sahih Muslim no.1916) 

2) See hadith no.63 for a further detailed understanding of this issue

The Permissibility of Eating Horse Meat

[370] Narrated Asma bint Abu Bakr (Radiullahu Anhuma)
We slaughtered a horse during the lifetime of the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) and ate it. And in another narration she said, “and we were in Madina”

[GRADING] – [NARRATED BY BUKHARI]

[REFERENCES]

Transmitted by

Bukharee, Book of Hunting, Slaughtering, Chapter: An-Nahr and Adh-Dhabh [no.5510]

Muslim, Book of The Book of Hunting, Slaughter, and what may be Eaten, Chapter: The permissibility of eating horse meat [no.1942]

Nasai [7:227], Ibn Majah [no.3190], Ahmad [6:345]

[RULINGS FROM THE HADITH]

1) This hadith proves that the meat of horses is halal and thus permissible to eat

2) The hadith of Jabir (RadhiAllahu Anhu) is a Qawli hadith in which the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Wasallam) gave permission for horse meat to be consumed  and in the wording of Bukhari it mentions the word Rakhasa i.e. if need be instead of explicit permission. [Bukhari [4219], Muslim [1941], Abu Dawud [3788], Nasai [7:210], Ahmad [3:361], Baihaqi [9:326], Darimi [1993], Ibn al-Jarud [885] . See the next hadith of this chapter i.e. no,371

3) Imams Ahmad, Shafi, Abu Yusuf, Muhammad Shaybani held it is permissible and lawful to eat where as Imams Abu Hanifah and Malik were of the position it is unlawful i.e. haram to eat. [Subl al-Salam [4:1824] 

4) This incident took place in Madinah after Jihad was textually obligated as other narrations elucidate Asma (RadhiAllahu Anha) said we were in Madinah.

5) Animals that one can make Dhahb one can also make Nahr of them and vice versa and it is recommended to make Nahr of Camels only and dhahb of other animals.

The Superiority of That Person Who Leaves All Doubtful (Unclear) Things for the Sake of His Religion

[368] Narrated An-Nu’man bin Bashir (Radi Allahu Anhu):
I heard Allah’s Messenger (Sallalahu Alayhi Wa Sallam) saying and he pointed with his fingers towards his ears, ‘Both legal and illegal things are evident but in between them there are doubtful (suspicious) things and most of the people have no knowledge about them. So whoever saves himself from these suspicious things saves his religion and his honor. And whoever indulges in these suspicious things is like a shepherd who grazes (his animals) near the Hima (private pasture) of someone else and at any moment he is liable to get in it. (O people!) Beware! Every king has a Hima and the Hima of Allah on the earth is His illegal (forbidden) things. Beware! There is a piece of flesh in the body if it becomes good (reformed) the whole body becomes good but if it gets spoilt the whole body gets spoilt and that is the heart.

[GRADING] – [AGREED UPON]

[REFERENCES]

Transmitted by

Bukhari, Book of faith, Chapter: The superiority of that person who leaves all doubtful (unclear) things for the sake of his religion

Muslim, Book of distribution of water, Chapter: Taking that which is lawful and leaving that which is unclear

[RULINGS FROM THE HADITH]

1) The halal things are absolutely clear and the haram things are absolutely clear. Every muslim is commanded to use the halal and refrain and avoid the haram.

2) It is a sign of taqwa to refrain and avoid the unclear matters.

3) The heart is such an important piece of the body that when it is upright the whole body is upright and when it is corrupt, the whole body becomes corrupt.

The Issue of the Prophet’s (Alayhim Was-Sallatu Was-Sallaam) Inheritance

[44] And with the same chain of narration that when the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) died, the wives of the Prophet (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) wanted to send Uthman Ibn Afan to Abu Bakr as-Sidiq to ask him about their inheritance from the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) A’isha said to them, “Didn’t the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) say, ‘No one inherits from us. What we leave is sadaqa.’ “

[GRADING] – [SAHEEH]

Ibn Shihab az-Zuhri clarified hearing (this hadith from Urwa Ibn az-Zubayr) in Bukhari (no. 4034)

[REFERENCES] – [AGREED UPON]

Al-Muwatta (Yahya’s narration 2/993 hadith no.1935, book 56, chapter 12, hadith no.27) at-Tamhid (8/150), al-Istidhkar: (no.1877)

Transmitted by Bukhari (no.6730) and Muslim (51/1758) from the hadith of Malik.

[UNDERSTANDING FROM THE HADITH]

1) This hadith, “We (Prophets and Messengers [Alayhim Was-Sallatu Was-Sallaam]) do not have any inheritors, whatever we leave behind that is sadaqa” is mutawatir. See Qutfal azhar (no.100) luqtul ali (no.26) and nadhmul mutanathar (no.272)

2) It is not necessary that the knowledge of one authentic hadith will definitely and surely be known by every single scholar rather it is more likely (and possible) that despite the knowledge of some scholars at a particular time they do not remember the hadith or proof, and remember it when a necessity comes along or someone reminds them of it.

3) It is narrated from A’isha (Radi Allahu Anha) that Abu Bakr (Radi Allahu Anhu) said at the time when he was on his death bed that, “According to my knowledge I have only this wealth (that is) 1. A milking she camel 2. A sword polishing servant. When I die then take these (2 things) and give them to Umar (Radi Allahu Anhu). When these two things were given to Umar (Radi Allahu Anhu) then he said, “May Allah have mercy on Abu Bakr (Radi Allahu Anhu), he has put those people after him into difficulty.” (Tabaqat Ibn Sa’d (3/192) and its chain is Sahih) It is known from this that the rulers should be very careful in the baytul mal and government affairs so that after death he is saved from impeachment. Every person will be given their review of deeds on the day of judgement which will have details of all the small and big deeds listed.

4) It is said in an authentic narration which is according to the names of men (biographies of narrators) of the Shia that Abu Abdullah (Ja’far Ibn Muhamad Sadiq) narrated that the Messenger of Allah (Sallalahu Alayhi Was-Sallam) said, “And without a shadow of a doubt the inheritors of the Prophets (Alayhimus Salam) are the scholars, without a shadow of a doubt the inheritance of the Prophets (Alayhimus Salam) is not dirham or dinar however they leave behind the inheritance of knowledge, whoever takes it then they have taken a big part.” (al-Usul Minal Kafi of Kayni (vol.1 pg.34) chapter of reward of the teacher and student hadith (no.1), and its chain is authentic according to the Shia)
