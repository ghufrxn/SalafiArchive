Ibn Taymiyyah’s stance in brief, is that he is a legitimate ruler but he ruled with force, he was not a Kafir but he wasn’t a companion or from pious friends of Allah, we do not like him but we do not curse him, he is responsible for what happened to Husayn and in al-Harrah because he did not deal with events wisely.

Ibn Taymiyyah says also about the direct killers of Husayn such as Shamir and ibn Ziyad:

وَأَمَّا مَنْ قَتَلَ ” الْحُسَيْنَ ” أَوْ أَعَانَ عَلَى قَتْلِهِ أَوْ رَضِيَ بِذَلِكَ فَعَلَيْهِ لَعْنَةُ اللَّهِ وَالْمَلَائِكَةِ وَالنَّاسِ أَجْمَعِينَ ؛ لَا يَقْبَلُ اللَّهُ مِنْهُ صَرْفًا وَلَا عَدْلًا

“As for the one who killed al-Husayn and the one who helped kill him or was pleased by this, then may the curse of Allah, his angels and all humanity be upon him; Allah shall not accept his worship or excuse.”
