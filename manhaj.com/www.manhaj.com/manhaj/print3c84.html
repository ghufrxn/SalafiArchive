Is Tazkiyah (or Graduation) Required Before One Teaches and Gives Da'wah? Part 6: Shaykh Ubayd al-Jaabiree
Tuesday, October 01 2013 - by Abu.Iyaad
Read more articles at Manhaj.Com

Following the role of Ahl al-Sunnah in the West of conveying the speech of the Scholars regarding certain figureheads who went astray, the followers of many of these figureheads spread doubts about some of the callers in the West, with respect to teaching and giving da'wah, claiming that a person must have a tazkiyah to teach, or he must have graduated from an institution and so on. However, the reality of the matter is that those whom they target in this way actually have connections with the Scholars, they travel to them, and take knowledge from them in aqidah, manhaj and fiqh, even if they have not formally studied in an institution, or graduated or have a specific tazkiyah. The real reason for these doubts is to undermine what the callers do in the West (in addition to calling to the Sunnah) of warning from doubts, innovations and desires and warning from the people who are upon them or promote them. The articles in these series will shed light on this matter and repel the doubts spread. No one article in this series should be read in isolation as it is possible for a person with ulterior motives to utilize some of these fatwas from the scholars to justify isolation and separation away from the main body of Salafis in that land and from their centres and mosques with the claim that everybody has the green light to teach, and so some of them teach in the mosques and centres of the hizbees and others teach in their homes (often secretly) whilst spreading poison that aims to isolate people from the students of knowledge. Rather, all the statements of all the Shaykhs should be put together and understood collectively to get a clearer picture of this whole affair. This is because there are generalizations in some fatwas, clarifications in others, important minor details in others, and contexts in some fatwas not found in others. All of this should be put together to get a full, comprehensive picture. Alongside the clarifications in these articles, it should be still be stressed that seeking knowledge from the Scholars, by travelling to them and sitting in their lessons is of utmost importance and should not be neglected as it is the primary route of seeking knowledge. And for those who are unable to do this, then they should take from the students of knowledge in their lands, those who have connections with the Scholars, have studied with them and take from them. So they should attend their lessons, in the Salafi mosques and centres, and any issues that arise such as requiring a fatwa, or removal of a shubhah or clarification in an issue of knowledge, then those students of knowledge can return these questions to the Scholars on behalf of the people of that land if the answer is not already known.

Shaykh Ubayd al-Jaabiree was asked the following question as occurs in a recording dated 29th Muharram 1433H in a open meeting with the Shaykh:

هل التزكية واجبة لطالب العلم لكي يدرس وإن لم يجد من يزكيه هل يدرس ؟ وهل فى التزكية سلفٌ , أفيدونا أفادكم الله ؟

Is a tazkiyah (commendation) obligatory for a seeker of knowledge so that he may teach, and if there is not to be found anyone who has commended him, should he teach? And is there any precedence (from the Salaf) in this issue of commendation? Benefit us may Allah benefit you.

The Shaykh's answer:

يحتاج إلى التزكية المجهول سواء كان وافدًا على القطر أو في قطره , أم من عُرف فإن كان خارج قطره , والعلماء علموا أنه يدرس وأنه يلقي الدروس من الكتاب والسنة وعلى فهم السلف الصالح فهذا لا مانع أن يدرس إن شاء الله تعالى . وكذلك لا مانع من الجلوس إليه حتى يجرحوه علماء القطر , ولا يجب جرح الكل بل إذا قام الدليل عالم منهم ورع , تقي صاحب سنة , واسع النظر قبل جرحه , وأما إن كان فى قطره وقومه يعرفونه ومكنوه من تدريسهم في مسجد أو في مجالسهم فعليه أن يتقى الله فى هؤلاء وأن ينتهج الحكمة والروية ولا ينفر الناس عنه , فإن كان عنده نظر فى أدلة الأحكام الشرعية من الكتاب والسنة استدل واستنبط , وإن كان فى البداية فإنه ينقل كلام أهل العلم سواء ذكرهم أولم يذكرهم ولكن لا يقول قلت أو أقول فينسب الكلام إلى نفسه , بل الأولى أن يقول قال الشيخ فلان , الشيخ فلان فتواه فى هذا كذا حتى يطمئن الناس إليه

The one who is unknown (majhool) is the one who requires a tazkiyah, irrespective of whether he was sent to a land or in his own land. As for the one who is known, then if he is (teaching) outside of his own land, and the Scholars know he is teaching and giving lessons from the Book and the Sunnah upon the understanding of the Salaf, then there is nothing to prevent him teaching if Allaah, the Exalted wills. And likewise, thehre is nothing to prevent sitting with him until the scholars of his region disparage him, and is not necessary that all the scholars disparage him, rather if the evidence is established through one (Allaah-fearing), pious scholar amongst them, a person of the Sunnah, who has expansive vision (into these matters) then his disparagement is accepted.

As for when he is in his own land and his people know him and enable him to teach them in a mosque or in their gatherings, then it is upon him to fear Allaah with respect to those (people) and that he traverses (the path) of wisdom, deliberation and that he does not drive the people away from himself. And if he has the ability to look into the evidences for the shari'ah rulings from the Book and the Sunnah, then he can deduce evidence. And if he is in the beginning (stages of seeking knowledge) then he should quote the statements of the people of knowledge, irrespective of whether he mentions them or does not mention them. However, he is not to say, "I said" or "I say" attributing the speech to himself, rather it is more befitting that he say, "So and so Shaykh said", "Shaykh so and so's fatwa in this matter is such and such" so that the people are at ease with him.
