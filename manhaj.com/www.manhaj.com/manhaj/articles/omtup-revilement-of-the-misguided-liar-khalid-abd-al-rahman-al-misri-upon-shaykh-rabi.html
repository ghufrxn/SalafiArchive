Khālid ʿAbd al_Raḥmān al-Miṣrī--over a period of time--has tried to engineer the slanderous claim that Shaykh Rabīʿ bin Hādī has opposed a foundation of the Sunnah and has traversed the manhaj of the Khawārij. In a recent audio recording, he came out with this explicitly, after having only alluded to it indirectly in the past. He tried to present scholarly gatherings of shūrā [consultative meetings in which legislative rulings on nawāzil such as qitāl are discussed] as innovative secretive meetings and that to affirm such meetings is an affirmation (taqrīr) of the Khawārij. He expressly accused Shaykh Rabīʿ of this. As soon as he was refuted and declared misguided and astray for reviling an Imām in the Sunnah in our era, Aḥmad Bazmul came swiftly to his defence, thereby exposing himself also.

This jamāʿah--or this network--is the one that has been exposed and brought to light through the Muṣaʿfiqah fitnah unleashed by Muḥammad bin Hādī who has become the prominent visible face of a wider undercurrent that stretches back many years. What we have is a collection of individual networks that were previously unconnected or loosely-tied and which have now come together upon common goals (despite prior differences between themselve in some cases). They are desirous of having affairs returned back to themselves. They see the major scholars such as Shaykh Rabīʿ, Shaykh ʿUbayd as being reclusive and not in touch with people, their situations or their lands--which are blatant falsehoods. As for targeting some of the students of knowledge and mashāyikh through the "Ṣaʿāfiqah" label--this was simply a strategy among the strategies to undermine these scholars--the real goal was to present a new type of scholarship to replace these major, senior, experienced scholars who have wisdom and foresight, by presenting them in the same way that the Quṭbiyyah Surūriyyah were presenting the major scholars two decades ago: Out of touch with the realities because they are surrounded by unscrupulous, evil people and ill-informed, or being blindly-followed in their alleged errors. And when the falsehood of this becomes known, these people are forced--because they committed so much time, wealth, resources and effort into this agenda--to reveal their true colours and just come out with what they have been concealing all along: Accusing these major scholars of opposing the Sunnah and traversing the ways of major innovated sects such as the Murjiʾah or the Khawārij.

Alḥamdulillāh, the response to this misguided liar and muṣaʿfiq has been immediate, strong and robust and we present here some of the best resources.

Resources in Arabic and English:

  • Shaykh Rabīʿ's Response: Khalid 'Abd al-Rahman is a liar (kadhdhaab). Arabic. Contains good refutation of al-Miṣrī's doubts.
    Shaykh Rabīʿ: "He is a liar (kadhdhāb), rather, he is the one who has opposed Ahl al-Sunnah."

    And from the excellent points by the author, exposing the slandering liar and his double standards:

    1. His support for the fitnah of al-Ṣaʿfaqah (falsely declaring students and shaykhs as bankrupt in knowledge), which was overseen by [Muḥammad Ibn Hādī], thereby turning away from the Book of Allāh which calls to unity and warns from splitting.
    2. Likewise, his turning away from the Book of Allāh in its ruling upon the one who makes a false accusation, in that he is a fāsiq (sinner) whose testimony is rejected.
    3. Also from his oppositions is revilement upon the senior people of knowledge, lying upon them and inciting the common-folk against them, as occurs in two audios of his which he filled with lies, sinfulness, apportioning of blame and deception. And it is from the signs of the people of innovation that they revile the people of athar (traditions).
    4. Also from his oppositions is throwing the words of the scholars about the fitnah of al-Ṣaʿfaqah against the wall.
    5. And from his oppositions is changing colours in the issue itself. So whereas he used to advise with returning to Shaykh ʿUbayd in the affair of Libya, as occurs in his audio recording, he now blames the senior scholars whom he used to advise with and accuses them of having the opinion of the Khawārij!! Sublime are you O Allāh, this is a mighty slander.
    6. And from his oppositions is judging politically with two scales, and this is his silence over Ibn Hādī who agreed with the Khārijites that [persistence in sinful] action indicates istiḥlāl (holding something lawful), as occurs in his audio.
    7. And likewise, his silence over Ibn Hādī who opposed the waliy al-ʾamr (leader) after agreeing that he would not speak against the students of knowledge, and he had actually signed [a resolution] over this in the presence of the judge as occurs in the official document containing the judge's ruling.
    8. And from his oppositions is his silence about Ibn Hādī who permits backbiting of the ruler, and this is from the actions of the Khārijites in truth. So this one called Khālid remains silent over all of this, yet reviles the people of knowledge whose hearts, tongues and pens are purified of the actions of the Khārijites, and those who warned against the Khārijites such as the Ikhwān, Dāeshites, Qāʾidah and others.
    9. And from his oppositions is their independence and isolation away from the senior people of knowledge and not caring about their advices, particularly in this fitnah.

  • Shaykh ʿUbayd al-Jābirī's Defence of Shaykh Rabīʿ and Clarification of Khālid al-Miṣrī's Falsehood. Translation:

    In the name of Allāh, and all praise is due to Allāh. May Allāh make good mention and grant safety and peace to our Prophet Muḥammad, his family and his companions. That which I know and worship Allāh with is that the people of knowledge from olden times mutually consult between themselves in the affairs that are presented to them. All [of the scholars] give their opinion and they may agree or they may disagree [with each other]. And this is not from the partisan secretive gatherings. This is because they consult with each other in matters that are open for ijtihād and subject to dispute. Everyone can express his view. This is known from olden times, it is known by everyone who is well-informed about the biographies of the people of knowledge, starting from the Companions, then the Successors and those after them from the People of Knowledge, Leadership and Excellence in the religion. [And in all that time] no one said this statement [of Khālid al-Miṣrī]. Rather, this [saying] has been chosen by ignorant youngsters who stir in the murky waters. And from this corroboration [of the truth in this matter] it is known that Shaykh Rabīʿ-may Allāh preserve him-is our elder, our elder brother and he is free of this accusation, as is said, with the freedom of the wolf from the blood of Yūsuf (may Allāh mention him with good and grant him safety). I and Shaykh Rabīʿ bin Hādī-may Allāh grant him success- have such affection and love between us that is known to one who is far and near. He is free of what that Egyptian has accused him of, [Allāh says]: "And who conduct their affairs by mutual consultation" (42:38). This is general, we consult with each other, myself, and my brother Shaykh Rabīʿ and our student, companion, Shaykh ʿAbd Allāh bin ʿAbd al-Raḥīm al-Bukhārī. And I say openly [in this gathering], I dictate it with my mouth that he [Shaykh ʿAbd Allāh] is an ʿAllāmah, he has striving-in nurturing the youth and beautiful authorships-that is to be thanked for. So I say, may Allāh bless you, spread this, this is permission from me to spread this.

  • Shaykh ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz Sayr al-Mubārikī's Defence of Shaykh Rabīʿ. Arabic.

    Summary: The accusation of Khālid al-Miṣrī against Shaykh Rabīʿ of deviating from the Sunnah and traversing the manhaj of the Khārijites is refuted in short by saying that if returning to the scholars in nawāzil is khurūj (revolting against the rulers), then what exactly is the Salafi methodology in that case (in this affair). For it is known that those who are scholars in these affairs are consulted with and their verdicts are taken. He cites "But if they had referred it back to the Messenger or to those of authority among them, then the ones who [can] draw correct conclusions from it would have known about it." (4:38). As is not hidden, those in authority are the scholars and the rulers, and those who derive rulings are the scholars. So if this is khurūj in your view, and opposition to a foundation of the Sunnah and deviation from the Sunnah,then you explain to us what is the manhaj of the Salaf in these affairs. Rather, Shaykh Rabīʿ has not opposed the Sunnah or agreed with the manhaj of the Khawārij. Rather, your way is the way of the Khawārij, because of your revilement because this is from their way. You have resembled the Ḥaddādiyyah in this revilement. It is upon you to repent from this evil and to abandon partisanship to individuals and to abandon hawā. We request all people in Egypt to explain the truth and to defend Shaykh Rabīʿ.
