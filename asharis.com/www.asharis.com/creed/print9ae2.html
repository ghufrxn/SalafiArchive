The Jahmites and the Hadith Mentioning 'Harwalah' (Allah's Haste in Reciprocating and Rewarding His Servant)
Posted by Abu.Iyaad on Saturday, January, 11 2020 and filed under Articles
Key topics: Harwalah

The great Imām of the Salaf, ʿAbd Allāh bin al-Mubārak (d. 181H) said: "Whoever says to you so and so is a Mushabbih, then know that he is a Jahmite." And Imām al-Barbahārī (d. 329H) said: "If you hear a man say: 'So and so is a mushabbih' and 'So and so speaks about tashbīh', then know that he is a Jahmite." The Jahmites--operating upon the notion of divine perfection outlined by Aristotle and Plato which entered the theology of the Jews, Christians and later, that of the Muslims as "ilm al-kalām"--are always very keen on finding ways to accuse Ahl al-Sunnah of being Mushabbihah.
